## Relevant Evidence by Severity Range

[pubmed_40551470] - **Systematic Review and Meta-analysis (Evidence: 1b)**
- Evaluated 13 studies involving 1,492 patients with moderate AIS (30-45° curves) and minimum 10-year follow-up
- Assessed outcomes including radiographic curve progression, surgery rates, sagittal alignment, back pain rates, and quality of life
- **Note:** Specific numerical outcomes for the 40-50° subrange were not detailed in the abstract provided

### Surgical Treatment (Severe Range ≥70°)

- **Not applicable:** Focuses on curves substantially larger than the 40-50° range

# Evidence Synthesis: Surgical vs. Conservative Treatment of AIS (Cobb Angle 40-50°)

## Summary Assessment

**Critical Limitation**: The retrieved papers **do not contain direct comparative evidence** between surgical and conservative treatment for AIS in the 40-50° Cobb angle range. The papers focus on surgical techniques, diagnostic methods, and anesthesia approaches rather than surgical versus conservative management comparisons.

---

## Available Evidence Overview

### 1. Conservative Management Evidence

**[pubmed_40551470]** - Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis (Evidence: 1b)
- **Study Type**: Systematic review of conservative management
- **Population**: Moderate adolescent idiopathic scoliosis (AIS curves 25-30° to 40-45°)
- **Key Finding**: States that "bracing aims to prevent curve progression through external force application, but its **long-term efficacy and impact on curve behavior after removal remain unclear**"
- **Critical Gap**: Despite being a meta-analysis on conservative management, **no specific efficacy rates, complication rates, or progression percentages are provided** in the abstract/content supplied
- **Recommendation Context**: Clinical guidelines recommend bracing for curves 25-30° to 40-45°

### 2. Surgical Intervention Evidence

**[pubmed_41636888]** - Comparative Analysis (Evidence: 2b)
- **Study Type**: Comparative analysis
- **Population**: Large-magnitude AIS (≥70°)
- **Relevant Note**: Addresses curves >70°, **outside the 40-50° range queried**
- **Finding**: Compares posterior-only fusion versus anterior release with posterior fusion for large curves
- **Status**: Does not address the 40-50° Cobb angle group

**[pubmed_40932300]** - Meta-Analysis (Evidence: 1a)
- **Study Type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Comparison**: Robot-assisted versus conventional surgery (surgical technique comparison only)
- **Cobb Correction Results**: 
  - Mean difference in correction: **MD -0.59° (95% CI -2.14 to 0.96, p=0.46, I²=0%)**
  - Curve correction: **MD -1.48° (95% CI -5.83 to 2.87, p=0.50, I²=80.1%)**
- **Critical Gap**: Compares surgical approaches, **not surgery versus conservative treatment**
