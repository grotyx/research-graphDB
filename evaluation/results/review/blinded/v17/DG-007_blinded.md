# Evidence Summary: LLIF/XLIF vs TLIF for Degenerative Spondylolisthesis

## Critical Finding: Limited RCT Evidence

**No randomized controlled trials (RCTs) directly comparing LLIF/XLIF versus TLIF specifically for degenerative spondylolisthesis were identified in the provided papers.** The highest-quality evidence available is from systematic reviews and meta-analyses (Evidence level 1a), which primarily compare OLIF (oblique lateral) rather than traditional LLIF/XLIF techniques.

---

## Available Evidence by Comparison Type

### 1. OLIF vs TLIF for Degenerative Spondylolisthesis (Most Relevant)
**[pubmed_38622724]** - Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis (Evidence 1a)

OLIF demonstrated **statistically significant advantages** over TLIF across multiple outcomes:

| Outcome | Findings |
|---------|----------|
| **Surgical Duration** | Shorter in OLIF (specific values not provided) |
| **Intraoperative Bleeding** | Reduced with OLIF |
| **Hospital Stay** | Shorter with OLIF |
| **Postoperative Pain (VAS)** | Superior improvement in OLIF |
| **Disability (ODI)** | Superior improvement in OLIF |
| **Disc Height Restoration** | Better with OLIF |
| **Lumbar Lordosis Correction** | More effective with OLIF |

---

### 2. LLIF vs TLIF (Non-Spondylolisthesis Cohorts)
**[pubmed_39575949]** - Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis (Evidence 1a)

This review included general lumbar degenerative disease (not limited to spondylolisthesis):

- **LLIF demonstrated improved segmental lordosis (SL) restoration compared with TLIF in 2 of 3 studies** directly comparing these procedures [pubmed_39575949]
- **OLIF demonstrated superior restoration of segmental lordosis compared with TLIF** [pubmed_39575949]

---

### 3. OLIF vs PLIF (Complication Rates)
**[pubmed_37950267]** - Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis (Evidence 1a)

While not directly TLIF comparison, these data are relevant to lateral approaches:

| Complication Category | Finding |
|----------------------|---------|
| **Overall Postoperative Complications** | OLIF: OR=0.46 (95% CI 0.27–0.78; p=0.004) - **significantly lower than PLIF** |
| **Heterogeneity** | I²=0%, P=0.79 (homogeneous) |
| **Serious Complications** | OLIF had lower rates of iatrogenic nerve root injury vs PLIF |
| **Specific Complications** | OLIF had higher risk of retrograde ejaculation (mechanism-related to anterior approach) |

---

### 4. Minimally Invasive Lateral Techniques (Indirect Evidence)
**[pubmed_39895414]** - Network Meta-Analysis (Evidence 1a)

Compared six minimally invasive techniques (including X/DLIF, OLIF, SA-LLIF, MIS-TLIF):

- **Equivalent clinical outcomes in pain and disability relief** across all techniques [pubmed_39895414]
- **SA-LLIF (stand-alone lateral) showed superior early efficacy metrics:**
  - Shorter operative time
  - Faster hospitalization recovery
- **Risk identified:** Cage subsidence and reoperation needs (specific rates not fully detailed) [pubmed_39895414]

## Adjacent Segment Disease Risk

**[pubmed_39318116]** - Retrospective Cohort Study (Evidence 2b) - TLIF-specific:

This TLIF study identified that **inadequate L4-S1 lordosis restoration is associated with significantly higher ASD:**
- Adequate lordosis: 6.7% ASD incidence
- Inadequate lordosis: 33.3% ASD incidence (p=0.032)
- Revision rates: 5.7% (adequate) vs 25.9% (inadequate) (p=0.133)

*Implication:* Lateral approaches showing superior lordosis restoration may reduce this risk, though direct evidence is lacking.

---

## Evidence Gaps

1. **No RCTs directly comparing LLIF/XLIF vs TLIF for degenerative spondylolisthesis** identified
2. **Specific numerical comparisons limited:** Most meta-analyses report directions of superiority without precise effect sizes or mean differences
3. **OLIF data dominate lateral approach literature** - traditional LLIF/XLIF comparison data are sparse
4. **Subgroup analysis for spondylolisthesis missing:** Most studies include mixed degenerative pathology
5. **Long-term outcomes (>2 years) incompletely reported** across comparisons
6. **Specific complication rates for cage subsidence** in lateral approaches not quantified in provided papers

---

## Clinical Conclusion

**The latest evidence suggests OLIF (a lateral approach variant) demonstrates radiographic and functional advantages over TLIF for degenerative spondylolisthesis [pubmed_38622724], including superior lordosis restoration and shorter operative time. However, traditional LLIF/XLIF-specific RCT evidence for spondylolisthesis does not appear in the provided literature, limiting definitive recommendations at the highest evidence level.**

# Evidence Summary: LLIF/XLIF vs TLIF for Degenerative Spondylolisthesis

## Key Finding
**No RCTs are available in the provided papers.** The highest-quality evidence comes from systematic reviews and meta-analyses of observational studies.

---

## Available Evidence by Study Design

### Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analyses (Evidence Level 1a)

**OLIF vs TLIF for Spondylolisthesis** [pubmed_38622724]
- Systematic review and meta-analysis comparing oblique lateral approach (OLIF) with TLIF for **degenerative lumbar spondylolisthesis**
- Study design: Systematic review of comparative studies (January 2014-February 2024)
- *Specific outcome data not provided in abstract*

**Endoscopic TLIF vs OLIF-360 for Spondylolisthesis** [pubmed_41525955]
- Retrospective cohort comparison (Evidence: 2b, not RCT)
- **Complementary advantages noted:**
  - Endo-TLIF: Superior minimization of perioperative morbidity through direct visualization
  - OLIF-360: Superior structural reconstruction

### General Lateral Fusion Comparisons (Evidence Level 1a)

**Network Meta-Analysis of Multiple MIS Techniques** [pubmed_39895414]
- Included X/DLIF, OLIF, SA-LLIF, MIS-TLIF, UELIF, and BELIF
- Finding: **Equivalent clinical outcomes** in pain and disability relief regardless of fusion approach
- Note: Stand-alone LLIF (SA-LLIF) showed superior early efficacy (shorter operative time, faster recovery)

**Segmental Lordosis Restoration** [pubmed_39575949]
- OLIF demonstrated **superior restoration of segmental lordosis** compared with TLIF
- LLIF demonstrated **improved segmental lordosis restoration** compared with TLIF in 2 of 3 directly comparative studies

---

## Evidence Gaps

- **No randomized controlled trials** identified comparing LLIF/XLIF with TLIF specifically for degenerative spondylolisthesis
- Highest-quality evidence for spondylolisthesis comes from observational comparative studies
- Long-term outcome data limited in retrieved papers
