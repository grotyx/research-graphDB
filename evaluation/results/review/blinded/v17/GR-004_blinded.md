# Comparative Outcome Measure Analysis: Cervical Disc Replacement (CDR)

## Summary Finding

Based on the provided papers, **Cervical Disc Arthroplasty (CDA)** and **Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF)** are the primary interventions that share the most outcome measures with Cervical Disc Replacement (CDR). These three interventions are compared across nearly identical outcome domains in the meta-analyses.

## Shared Outcome Measures Across Interventions

| Outcome Measure | Papers Reporting | Interventions Compared |
|---|---|---|
| **Neck Disability Index (NDI)** | [pubmed_31863642], [pubmed_38843960], [pubmed_29753897], [pubmed_29595628], [pubmed_35113188] | CDR vs ACDF; CDA vs ACDF |
| **VAS Neck Pain** | [pubmed_31863642], [pubmed_38843960], [pubmed_30992666] | CDA vs ACDF |
| **VAS Arm Pain** | [pubmed_31863642], [pubmed_38843960] | CDA vs ACDF |
| **SF-36 Physical Component Score** | [pubmed_31863642] | CDA vs ACDF |
| **Neurological Success Rates** | [pubmed_40797275], [pubmed_30062947] | CDA/CDR vs ACDF |
| **Range of Motion (ROM)** | [pubmed_40797275], [pubmed_29753897], [pubmed_29595628] | CDA/CDR vs ACDF |
| **Adjacent Segment Disease (ASD)** | [pubmed_29753897], [pubmed_30062947] | CDR vs ACDF |
| **Reoperation/Secondary Surgery Rates** | [pubmed_29753897], [pubmed_28700452] | CDR vs ACDF |
| **Complication Rates** | [pubmed_29753897] | CDR vs ACDF |

## Key Comparative Data

### NDI (Neck Disability Index) - Most Commonly Reported
- **CDR vs ACDF (Bi-level)**: SMD 0.41 (95% CI: 0.29–0.52, p<0.00001) favoring ACDR [pubmed_29595628]
- **CDR vs ACDF (Multilevel)**: MD -0.55 (p=0.33, not significant) [pubmed_29753897]
- **CDA vs ACDF**: MD -2.88 (95% CI [-5.93, 0.17], p=0.06, no significant difference) [pubmed_31863642]

### VAS Pain Scores
- **VAS Neck Pain (CDA vs ACDF)**: MD -5.50 (95% CI [-8.49, -2.52], p=0.0003) favoring CDA [pubmed_31863642]
- **VAS Neck Pain (4 studies, 1,426 patients)**: MD -2.30 (95% CI [-3.72, -0.87], p=0.002) favoring CDA [pubmed_30992666]
- **VAS Arm Pain (CDA vs ACDF)**: MD -3.78 (95% CI [-7.04, -0.53], p=0.02) favoring CDA [pubmed_31863642]

### Range of Motion Preservation
- **CDA vs ACDF**: WMD 10.37 degrees (p<0.01) preserved in CDA [pubmed_40797275]
- **CDR vs ACDF (Multilevel)**: MD 13.83 degrees (p<0.00001) preserved in CDR [pubmed_29753897]

### Disease Progression & Reoperation
- **Adjacent Segment Disease (CDR vs ACDF)**: OR 0.27 (p=0.001) favoring CDR [pubmed_29753897]
- **Reoperation Rates (CDR vs ACDF)**: OR 0.25 (p<0.00001) favoring CDR [pubmed_29753897]
- **Secondary Surgical Procedures (CDR)**: Lowest rate (p=0.0178) [pubmed_28700452]

### Neurological Success
- **CDA vs ACDF**: OR 1.60 (95% CI: 1.30–1.98, p<0.01) favoring CDA [pubmed_40797275]

## Patient Phenotype Distinction

An important finding regarding outcome measure interpretation:

**[pubmed_40882232]** reports that patient selection significantly influences comparative outcomes:
- **Radiculopathy-predominant cohorts**: Favor CDA/CDR
- **Myelopathy-predominant cohorts**: Favor ACDF for decompressive efficacy

This suggests that outcome measure interpretation requires stratification by baseline pathology.

## Conclusion

**CDA and ACDF** share the most comprehensive outcome measurement overlap with CDR across 8+ standardized domains. The papers overwhelmingly focus on these three interventions for cervical degenerative disc disease, with NDI, VAS pain scores, ROM, and complication rates representing the most frequently reported shared metrics. No other interventions in the database achieve comparable measurement overlap.

# Interventions Sharing Most Outcome Measures with Cervical Disc Replacement (CDR)

Based on the provided papers, **Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF)** and **Cervical Disc Arthroplasty (CDA)** are the interventions that share the most outcome measures with CDR across the literature.

## Shared Outcome Domains

### 1. **Functional/Pain Outcomes**
- NDI (Neck Disability Index) [pubmed_29753897, pubmed_31863642, pubmed_30062947]
- VAS (Visual Analog Scale) - neck and upper limb pain [pubmed_31431880, pubmed_30062947]
- Neurological success rates [pubmed_40797275]

### 2. **Motion Preservation**
- Range of motion at operated segment [pubmed_40797275, pubmed_31431880, pubmed_38843960]
- C2-C7 range of motion measurements [pubmed_31431880]

### 3. **Adjacent Segment Outcomes**
- Adjacent segment disease (ASD) [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_29753897, pubmed_30062947, pubmed_38843960]
- Adjacent level surgery rates [pubmed_31863642]
- Adjacent segment degeneration risk [pubmed_38843960]

### 4. **Secondary Surgery Rates**
- Total secondary surgery [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_29753897]
- Secondary surgery at index level [pubmed_31863642]
- Reoperation rates [pubmed_29753897, pubmed_38843960]

### 5. **Perioperative Measures**
- Hospital length of stay [pubmed_35113188, pubmed_29753897]
- Blood loss [pubmed_29753897]
- Complication rates [pubmed_29753897]

### 6. **Quality of Life**
- SF-36 physical component [pubmed_30062947]
- Patient satisfaction [pubmed_30062947]
- Dysphagia assessment [pubmed_30062947]

## Evidence Level
All comparisons derive from **Level 1a evidence** (systematic reviews and meta-analyses of RCTs) across multiple papers [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_40797275, pubmed_29595628, pubmed_40882232, pubmed_29753897, pubmed_28700452, pubmed_31431880, pubmed_35113188, pubmed_30062947, pubmed_38843960].

**Gap:** The papers focus primarily on CDR versus ACDF comparisons; direct three-way comparisons of CDR, CDA, and ACDF using identical outcome metrics are not explicitly presented.
