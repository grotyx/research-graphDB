# Shared and Unique Outcomes: TLIF vs PLIF

## Shared Outcomes Between TLIF and PLIF

Based on the available evidence, there is **limited direct comparison** between TLIF and PLIF in the provided papers. However, one study provides relevant information:

**Fusion Success and 2-Year Outcomes:**
A systematic review [pubmed_23646277] (Evidence: 1a) comparing fusion techniques reported that "the type of fusion, PLF or PLIF did not affect 2-year outcomes" in patients with isthmic spondylolisthesis. Additionally, "they reported no cases of pseudoarthrosis in either group" in a comparative study of PLF versus PLIF (n=11 per group). This suggests both interbody fusion approaches achieve comparable long-term fusion rates and clinical outcomes.

## Unique Outcomes by Procedure

### TLIF-Specific Outcomes
The papers provide extensive TLIF outcome data but do NOT directly compare TLIF-unique benefits to PLIF:

- **Complication profiles:** TLIF showed higher rates of dural tears and wound complications compared to LLIF [analyzed_ba35d6e5] (Evidence: 2b), though this comparison does not involve PLIF
- **Surgical approach variations:** Outcomes differ between endoscopic, biportal endoscopic, and minimally invasive TLIF approaches [pubmed_41420307] (Evidence: 1a)

### PLIF-Specific Outcomes
The provided papers contain **no outcome data specific to PLIF** that would allow differentiation from TLIF.

## Evidence Gaps

**Critical limitation:** The retrieved papers do NOT contain direct head-to-head comparisons of TLIF versus PLIF clinical outcomes. A comprehensive answer to this question would require additional literature specifically comparing these two approaches.

# Comparative Outcomes: TLIF vs PLIF

## Shared Outcomes Between TLIF and PLIF

### Fusion Rates
Both procedures demonstrate comparable fusion rates at 24 months [pubmed_25955186]:
- **PLIF/TLIF with rhBMP-2**: 95.7% (n=141)
- **PLIF/TLIF without rhBMP-2**: 89.5% (n=86)
- **Difference**: 6.2% absolute difference, OR 1.98 (95% CI: 0.39-10.1), not statistically significant [pubmed_25955186]

### Complication Profiles
Both procedures share similar complication types [pubmed_23646277]:
- Subsidence
- Osteolysis
- Postoperative radiculopathy
- Heterotopic ossification
- Dural tear
- Interbody implant migration
- Intraoperative neurologic injury
- **Overall complication rate range**: 8-80% (average 36.4%) [pubmed_23646277]

### Long-term Functional Outcomes
Across multiple outcome measures, TLIF and PLIF demonstrate comparable results [pubmed_41420307]:
- Long-term functional improvement comparable
- Fusion rates comparable
- No significant differences in ultimate structural fusion achievement [pubmed_41420307]

### Clinical Efficacy for Degenerative Disease
Both procedures effectively treat lumbar degenerative disease with equivalent pain and disability relief [pubmed_39895414]

## Outcomes Unique or Differentiated by Procedure Type

### ALIF vs TLIF (Posterior Variant) - Comparative Context
While not directly PLIF-specific, the meta-analysis comparing TLIF to ALIF provides relevant posterior procedure data [pubmed_40066769]:
- **Patient-reported outcomes**: No significant differences in pain and disability between TLIF and ALIF [pubmed_40066769]
- **ALIF advantages**: Superior sagittal alignment and lower complication rates [pubmed_40066769]
- **Clinical implication**: Approach selection may be guided by surgeon experience, anatomical considerations, and alignment goals rather than functional outcomes [pubmed_40066769]

### Endoscopic TLIF Variants (Minimally Invasive Comparison)
When TLIF is performed via endoscopic techniques, short-term advantages emerge compared to traditional open approaches [pubmed_41420307]:
- **Short-term advantages**: Favored endoscopic TLIF techniques
- **Long-term convergence**: All three approaches (MIS-TLIF, FE-TLIF, BE-TLIF) ultimately equivalent at long-term follow-up [pubmed_41420307]

---

## Critical Evidence Gaps

**Major limitation**: The provided papers do NOT contain direct head-to-head RCT comparisons of traditional TLIF vs PLIF with specific quantitative outcome differences. The fusion rate data [pubmed_25955186] pools PLIF and TLIF together rather than comparing them. To answer which outcomes are truly unique to each procedure requires primary comparative studies not included in this evidence base.

**Recommendation**: Clinical selection between TLIF and PLIF should be based on:
- Surgeon experience and technical familiarity
- Anatomical considerations
- Patient-specific factors (coagulopathy, comorbidities)
- Institution-specific outcome data

Rather than procedure-specific outcome superiority, which the current evidence does not establish [pubmed_40066769].
