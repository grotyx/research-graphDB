# Evidence Synthesis: Optimal UIV Selection (T10 vs L1) in Lenke 5C Adult Degenerative Scoliosis

## Critical Limitation

**The retrieved papers do NOT contain direct comparative data between T10 and L1 as UIV choices in Lenke 5C degenerative scoliosis.** No papers specifically address Lenke 5C classification or provide head-to-head comparisons of these specific UIV levels.

## Relevant Indirect Evidence

### UIV Level Distribution in Adult Spinal Deformity
[pubmed_35586905] reports UIV distribution across their ASD population:
- Upper thoracic (T1-T9): 25 patients (14%)
- **Lower thoracic (T10-T12): 118 patients (66%)**
- **Upper lumbar (L1-L3): 36 patients (20%)**
- Median instrumentation length: 8 levels (IQR 7-9)

This demonstrates that T10-T12 is the most common UIV choice (66%), with L1-L3 used in 20% of cases, but does NOT indicate superiority.

### Proximal Junctional Complications and UIV Selection

**Overcorrection Risk:**
[pubmed_37740115] found that PJK was significantly more frequent in overcorrected groups (p = 0.05), suggesting aggressive correction increases proximal junctional failure risk. However, this study does not differentiate by specific UIV level.

[pubmed_38270393] demonstrated that **overcorrection beyond normative segmental lordosis values at proximal spinal levels (T10-L2) resulted in dramatically increased proximal junctional failure rates**. This applies to both T10 and L1 UIV selections and suggests strict adherence to normative alignment is critical regardless of UIV choice.

### Segmental Lordosis Optimization

[pubmed_39318116] emphasizes restoration of appropriate lordosis at the instrumented region (specifically L4-S1 in TLIF): patients achieving adequate L4-S1 lordosis (35°-45°) had 5.7% revision rates vs. 25.9% in inadequately corrected groups. By extension, this principle likely applies to UIV selection—achieving normative lordosis at the proximal junction is critical.

### Interbody Fusion and PJK Risk

[pubmed_29087808] reports that LLIF (lateral lumbar interbody fusion) combined with PSF (posterolateral fusion) demonstrated:
- **Significantly lower rates of PJK requiring revision**
- **Fewer proximal junctional fractures**
- **Fewer vertebral slips**

This suggests biomechanical support proximal to the instrumented region may reduce complications, potentially independent of specific UIV level choice.

## Clinical Implications of Available Evidence

1. **Normative Alignment is Critical**: Both T10 and L1 as UIV fall within the T10-L2 range where overcorrection risks dramatic PJK increases [pubmed_38270393]. Whichever UIV is selected, segmental lordosis must approximate normative values.

2. **Interbody Fusion Benefits**: Addition of interbody support (LLIF/ALIF) reduces PJK complications [pubmed_29087808], potentially offsetting UIV-level differences.

3. **Insufficient Evidence**: The literature does not provide level 1a evidence comparing T10 vs L1 UIV selection in this specific patient population.

- No stratified analysis by Lenke classification
- No outcome data specific to degenerative (non-idiopathic) scoliosis with UIV comparison
- Optimal UIV selection likely patient-specific based on deformity magnitude, curve pattern, and comorbidities—not addressed in retrieved papers

**Conclusion**: Evidence-based guidance for this specific clinical question cannot be provided from the retrieved literature.

# Evidence Synthesis: Optimal UIV Selection for Lenke 5C Adult Degenerative Scoliosis

## Summary Statement

The provided papers do not contain specific evidence comparing T10 versus L1 as the upper instrumented vertebra for Lenke 5C adult degenerative scoliosis. However, general principles for UIV selection from the literature are relevant.

## General UIV Selection Principles

**Anatomic Considerations:**
- UIV selection should avoid spanning across major coronal deformities and consider the apex of thoracic kyphosis [pubmed_37718099, Evidence Level 4]
- The thoracolumbar junction (T10-L2 region) is a transitional area with higher risk of mechanical complications due to mobility differences between the mobile lumbar spine and less mobile thoracic spine [pubmed_40033732, Evidence Level 4]

**Risk Factor Data:**
- More proximal UIV placement (T2 or more proximal) was associated with increased distal junctional kyphosis (DJK) development in adolescent idiopathic scoliosis [pubmed_40103372, Evidence Level 3]. This suggests that proximal UIV placement alters thoracic sagittal alignment and may cause compensatory distal junctional changes.

**General Recommendation:**
- A "neutral and stable vertebra" is recommended as the UIV to minimize proximal junctional kyphosis/failure (PJK/PJF) risk [pubmed_40033732, Evidence Level 4]

## Evidence Gaps

**Critical limitation:** No studies directly comparing T10 versus L1 selection for Lenke 5C curves were identified. The optimal choice requires consideration of:
- Curve apex location
- Preoperative thoracic kyphosis
- Sagittal alignment targets
- Patient-specific factors

**Recommendation:** Evidence-based guidance for this specific clinical scenario requires studies comparing these instrumentation levels in Lenke 5C populations.
