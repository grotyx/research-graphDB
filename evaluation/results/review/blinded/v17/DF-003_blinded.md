## RADIOGRAPHIC OUTCOMES

### Sagittal Correction

**Severe Thoracolumbar Kyphosis (PVCR):** [pubmed_40513242]
- Preoperative local kyphosis: 107.3°
- Immediate postoperative correction: 32.0° (75.3° correction achieved; p < 0.05)
- Final follow-up (mean 105.7 months): 37.1° (p < 0.05 vs preoperative)
- **Loss of correction:** 5.1° over 8.8 years (minimal durability loss)

**ACR with/without PSO:** [pubmed_25820352]
- PSO addition increased segmental lordosis change by **72.7% compared to ACR alone** (p < 0.001)
- This demonstrates PSO provides substantially greater correction as an adjunct to ACR

**Long-Term Alignment Durability:** [pubmed_41559490]
- Adult spinal deformity surgery reliably achieves planned sagittal alignment in early postoperative period across diverse approaches
- **Long-term durability is variable and inconsistent** across the surgical approaches studied
- Specific durability metrics not quantified in abstract

## COMPLICATION PROFILE

### Neurological Complications

**Modified PVCR vs Traditional PVCR:** [pubmed_40381021]
- Modified PVCR (with posterior ligamentous preservation): 5/31 patients (16.1%) with neurological complications
- Traditional PVCR: 12/31 patients (38.7%) with neurological complications
- **Absolute risk reduction:** 22.6%
- **Relative risk reduction:** 58.4% (p = 0.046)
- **NNT:** 4 patients to prevent one neurological complication with modified technique

### Mechanical Complications

**Restoration of Roussouly Profile:** [pubmed_37768339]
- Restored group (n=26): 7 mechanical complications (27%)
- Unrestored group (n=26): 23 mechanical complications (88%)
- **Absolute risk reduction:** 61% (p < 0.001)
- **Relative risk reduction:** 69.3%
- **NNT:** 1.6 patients (approximately 2)

### Overall Morbidity Profile

**PSO General Morbidity:** [pubmed_41497819]
- Identified as associated with substantial intraoperative morbidity including:
  - High blood loss
  - High neurological injury risk
  - Prolonged operative times
- Specific quantitative rates not provided in abstract

**3-CO Complication Risk in Elderly:** [pubmed_40968391]
- Traditional 3-CO carries high risk of complications, especially in septuagenarians (>70 years)
- Specific complication rates not provided in abstract
- Study compares to minimally invasive alternatives (lateral column realignment)

## CLINICAL SUMMARY

**Efficacy:** Three-column osteotomies (PSO, PVCR) achieve powerful sagittal corrections (75.3° in severe cases), with PSO adding 72.7% greater segmental lordosis change when combined with ACR. Early postoperative alignment goals are reliably achieved but long-term durability is variable.

**Safety:** Neurological complication rates are significant (16.1-38.7% depending on technique modification), with modified PVCR reducing neurological complications to 16.1% versus 38.7% with traditional approach. Mechanical complication rates reach 27-88% depending on achieving optimal radiographic targets (Roussouly restoration).

**Age Considerations:** Traditional 3-CO carries particularly high complication risk in septuagenarians; minimally invasive alternatives are being evaluated as safer approaches for elderly patients.

# Outcomes of Three-Column Osteotomies for Fixed Sagittal Imbalance

## Radiological Outcomes

### Sagittal Correction Capability
Three-column osteotomies (3-CO), particularly PSO, demonstrate superior correction compared to anterior-only approaches:

- **PSO vs. ACR alone**: Addition of PSO to ACR increased segmental lordosis correction by **72.7%** compared to ACR alone (p < 0.001) [pubmed_25820352], demonstrating that PSO provides significantly greater correction for rigid deformity [pubmed_32382878]

- **Evidence quality**: A meta-analysis [pubmed_41497819] (Evidence: 1a) included three prospective/retrospective clinical studies (2017-2023) and two biomechanical simulation studies (2022, 2024) comparing correction techniques, with lumbar lordosis measurements and complication rates reported across studies with follow-up periods ranging heterogeneously

## Complication Profile

### Overall Complication Rates
In septuagenarians undergoing 3-CO for thoracolumbar kyphosis [pubmed_40968391] (Evidence: 2b):
- **Total complications**: 28 complications in 19 patients (**35.2%**)
- Complications exclusive to 3CO included: surgical site infection (5.6%), hematoma (1.9%), and DVT [pubmed_40968391]

### Neurological Complications
- **New-onset neurological deficits**: 5 cases (9.3%) in 3CO group, all classified as Frankel C, with 3 improving to Frankel D after 2-year rehabilitation [pubmed_40968391]

## Evidence Gaps

- Limited data specifically stratified for PSO vs. VCR outcomes
- Heterogeneous follow-up periods across included studies [pubmed_41497819]
- Systematic review of osteotomy approaches [pubmed_38765779] (Evidence: 4) provided descriptive synthesis but limited outcome quantification
- Long-term HRQOL data synthesis available but specific metric details not fully detailed in retrieved papers [pubmed_32382878]
