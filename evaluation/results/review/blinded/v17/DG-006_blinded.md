# Laminoplasty versus Laminectomy with Fusion for Multilevel Cervical Myelopathy

## Summary of Evidence

Both approaches are effective for treating multilevel cervical myelopathy, but **laminoplasty demonstrates superior outcomes across multiple domains** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362].

---

## Clinical Outcomes

### Neurological Improvement
Both procedures achieve **comparable neurological recovery**:
- Similar improvements in Japanese Orthopaedic Association (JOA) scores between groups [pubmed_30921202, pubmed_30813208]
- Both procedures effectively decompress the spinal cord and improve neurological function [pubmed_30921202]
- Visual analog scale (VAS) neck pain improvements are similar between approaches [pubmed_30921202]

### Neck Disability and Function
- **Laminoplasty achieved lower Neck Disability Index scores** compared to laminectomy with fusion [pubmed_38764362]

---

## Operative Characteristics (Laminoplasty Advantages)

**Shorter operative time** [pubmed_38764362]

**Less estimated blood loss** [pubmed_38764362]

**Comparable hospital length of stay** between both approaches [pubmed_38764362]

---

## Complication Profiles

### Overall Complication Rates
- **Laminoplasty: Lower overall complication rates** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362]
- Laminectomy and fusion: Associated with more complications [pubmed_30921202]

### Specific Complications
- **C5 palsy: Lower incidence with laminoplasty** [pubmed_38764362]
- Laminectomy and fusion: Associated with postoperative segmental instability and kyphosis when performed without fusion [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_33819935]

---

## Biomechanical Outcomes

### Cervical Range of Motion
- **Laminoplasty: Greater postoperative cervical range of motion preservation** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_33819935]
- Laminoplasty preserves motion and minimizes alteration to cervical spine biomechanics [pubmed_27281067]

### Cervical Alignment
- **Laminoplasty: Lower T1 slope angle** (more favorable sagittal alignment) [pubmed_38764362]

---

## Procedure Indications

**Laminectomy with fusion** is favored over laminectomy alone because fusion reduces postoperative segmental instability [pubmed_33819935]

**Laminoplasty** is an alternative approach that provides advantages in motion preservation [pubmed_33819935]

---

## Evidence Quality and Limitations

- **High-quality evidence (1a)**: Multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362, pubmed_27281067, pubmed_25815808, pubmed_30921202]
- **Moderate-quality evidence (2a)**: Systematic reviews with nonrandomized comparative studies [pubmed_27781191, pubmed_33819935]
- **Important limitation**: No randomized controlled trials comparing these approaches; most evidence derives from observational studies with potential surgeon selection bias [pubmed_27781191]
- **Special populations**: Limited specific evidence for ossification of the posterior longitudinal ligament (OPLL) subgroup [pubmed_27781191]

---

## Clinical Recommendation

**Laminoplasty emerges as the preferable approach** for multilevel cervical myelopathy based on superior outcomes in operative time, blood loss, complication rates, C5 palsy incidence, motion preservation, and functional scores [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362], while maintaining comparable neurological improvement to laminectomy with fusion.

# Laminoplasty vs. Laminectomy with Fusion for Multilevel Cervical Myelopathy

## Summary of Evidence

This question is addressed by **seven level 1a meta-analyses** and one level 2a systematic review, providing robust evidence for comparison.

---

## Functional Outcomes & Myelopathy Improvement

### JOA (Japanese Orthopedic Association) Scores
Both approaches achieve **substantial improvements in functional scores** [pubmed_30813208]. However, the papers do not provide specific postoperative JOA values or differential improvements between groups in the available abstracts.

### VAS (Visual Analog Scale) Pain Scores
Both procedures achieve **significant pain reduction** [pubmed_30813208], but specific numerical differences are not provided in the abstracts.

---

## Cervical Range of Motion (ROM) Preservation

**Laminoplasty superiority:**
- Laminoplasty demonstrates **greater postoperative cervical range of motion preservation** compared to laminectomy with fusion [pubmed_30813208]
- This is a key advantage, as laminectomy with fusion sacrifices motion at the fused levels

---

## Complication Rates: Overall

### Overall Complication Rates
**Laminoplasty emerges as superior:**
- Laminoplasty has **lower overall complication rates** compared to laminectomy with instrumented fusion [pubmed_30813208]

The specific meta-analysis [pubmed_38764362] emphasizes laminoplasty demonstrates:
- Shorter operative time
- Reduced blood loss
- **Lower complication rates**
- Reduced C5 palsy incidence
- Improved functional outcomes

---

## C5 Palsy Incidence: Critical Safety Finding

**Laminoplasty significantly safer:**

| Approach | C5 Palsy Incidence |
|----------|-------------------|
| Laminoplasty | **6%** |
| Laminectomy-Fusion | **10%** |

[pubmed_41017260] demonstrates laminoplasty **reduces risk compared to laminectomy alone or with instrumented fusion**, with laminoplasty representing "a safer posterior approach option when decompression is indicated."

---

## Postoperative Deformity & Instability

### Kyphosis and Segmental Instability
- Laminectomy alone is associated with **postoperative segmental instability and kyphosis** [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_30921202]
- Laminectomy with fusion addresses these drawbacks by reducing postoperative kyphosis [pubmed_30921202]
- Laminoplasty preserves motion while minimizing alterations to cervical spine biomechanics [pubmed_27281067]

---

## Adjacent Segment Disease (ASD)

**Laminectomy with fusion disadvantage:**
- Fusion approaches may cause **adjacent segment disease and require additional surgery** [pubmed_30921202]
- This is an implicit advantage of laminoplasty's motion-preservation approach

---

## Special Consideration: Ossification of Posterior Longitudinal Ligament (OPLL)

**Differential outcomes by pathology:**

For OPLL specifically:
- Limited evidence suggests **laminectomy-fusion may offer superior myelopathy improvement** [pubmed_23963000]
- However, this advantage "must be weighed against pseudarthro[sis complications]" [pubmed_23963000]
- A dedicated meta-analysis compared both approaches for CSM and OPLL [pubmed_37062984], though specific OPLL outcome data is not detailed in the abstract

---

## Study Quality and Evidence Limitations

**Evidence quality concern:**
- While both procedures demonstrate "similar effectiveness for CSM based on **available low-quality evidence**" [pubmed_23963000]
- Most underlying studies are **nonrandomized controlled studies** with significant limitations [pubmed_27781191]
- Patient selection bias exists: surgeons chose procedures, and groups often differed at baseline (e.g., preoperative kyphosis, neurologic severity) [pubmed_27781191]

---

## Clinical Decision-Making

### When Evidence Supports Laminoplasty

**Preferred for:**
- Patients with multilevel cervical spondylotic myelopathy (CSM)
- Desire to preserve cervical motion
- Lower complication tolerance
- Avoid C5 palsy risk (6% vs. 10%)

### When Evidence Suggests Laminectomy-Fusion

**May be preferred for:**
- **OPLL with severe myelopathy** (limited evidence for superior outcomes) [pubmed_23963000]
- Preexisting cervical kyphosis
- Need for immediate spinal stability

### Individualization Essential

Both procedures represent **effective surgical treatments achieving substantial improvements** [pubmed_30813208]. Clinical decision-making should incorporate [pubmed_23963000]:
- Individual patient age
- Comorbidities
- Surgeon familiarity and technical expertise
- Patient preferences
- Baseline sagittal alignment

---

## Overall Conclusion

**Laminoplasty** emerges as the superior approach for multilevel cervical myelopathy based on:
1. **Lower overall complication rates** [pubmed_30813208]
2. **Reduced C5 palsy incidence** (6% vs. 10%) [pubmed_41017260]
3. **Greater ROM preservation** [pubmed_30813208]
4. **Shorter operative time and reduced blood loss** [pubmed_38764362]

However, **laminectomy with fusion remains appropriate** for specific scenarios, particularly OPLL with severe myelopathy. **Careful patient selection remains paramount** [pubmed_38764362] given heterogeneous patient populations and baseline differences in the underlying comparative studies.
