# Evidence Comparison: UBE versus Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## ODI (Oswestry Disability Index) Improvement

**Limited Direct Comparison Available:**

One study [pubmed_41290735] (Evidence: 2b, retrospective comparative) reported that both uniportal and unilateral biportal endoscopic fusion are **superior to MIS-TLIF in terms of early postoperative low back pain relief**, particularly within 3 months post-surgery. However, this study noted **"no significant difference in early leg pain relief and functional improvement"** between endoscopic techniques and MIS-TLIF. Critically, the authors acknowledge **"at present, there is a shortage of cross-comparison research between the two techniques."**

The meta-analysis by [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) comparing conventional open surgery to biportal endoscopy found ODI improvements favoring biportal endoscopy, but this compares open surgery rather than TLIF specifically.

## Complication Rates

**Comprehensive Evidence Unavailable:**

The retrieved literature does not provide direct comparative complication rates between UBE and conventional TLIF. The meta-analysis [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) analyzed complication rates between conventional open surgery and biportal endoscopy across 414 open and 383 biportal procedures, but conventional open surgery is not equivalent to TLIF outcomes.

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Direct RCT comparison lacking** between UBE and conventional TLIF for lumbar stenosis
2. **Functional outcome data limited** - most studies compare UBE to MIS-TLIF rather than standard TLIF
3. **Complication rate comparisons** specifically for UBE versus conventional TLIF not available in provided literature

**Recommendation:** High-quality RCTs directly comparing these techniques are needed for definitive conclusions.

# Evidence Comparison: UBE-TLIF vs Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## Summary of Available Evidence

The retrieved papers provide **indirect comparative evidence** between endoscopic approaches and conventional techniques, but **no direct head-to-head RCT comparing UBE-TLIF specifically to conventional open TLIF** is present in this collection.

---

## ODI Improvement Outcomes

### UBE-TLIF vs MIS-TLIF (Network Meta-Analysis)
**Study Design:** Network meta-analysis [pubmed_41420307]

BE-TLIF (Biportal Endoscopic TLIF) demonstrated **superior back pain reduction** compared to MIS-TLIF, with **improved short-term functional outcomes** [pubmed_41420307]. However, specific ODI numerical values were not reported in the available excerpt.

### Biportal vs Uniportal Endoscopic Surgery (General Lumbar Degenerative Disease)
**Study Design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis [pubmed_41559891]

- **Early functional recovery (1-3 months):** Biportal endoscopic surgery offered **lower ODI scores** at 1-3 months postoperatively, with this advantage persisting at final follow-up [pubmed_41559891]
- **Critical limitation:** This advantage "did not consistently achieve minimal clinically important difference thresholds" [pubmed_41559891]

### Biportal Endoscopic Laminectomy vs Microscopic Laminectomy
**Study Design:** RCT (Evidence 1b) [pubmed_41002072]

This RCT directly compared endoscopic vs conventional approaches but found that **"these anatomical benefits do not translate into superior functional outcomes or pain relief from the patient's perspective"** [pubmed_41002072]. Specific ODI values were not provided in the excerpt.

---

## Complication Rates

### UBE-TLIF vs Endo-TLIF (Single-Segment Lumbar Stenosis with Spondylolisthesis)
**Study Design:** Comparative study (Evidence 1b) [pubmed_38752236]

**Complication data:** Specific complication rates were **NOT provided** in the available excerpt. The conclusion noted that UBE-TLIF required "further improvement in minimally invasive techniques to reduce tissue trauma and blood loss" compared to Endo-TLIF [pubmed_38752236].

### Biportal Endoscopic Spinal Surgery with Fusion (Systematic Review)
**Study Design:** Systematic review of 12 studies, 728 patients [pubmed_40195641]

- **Fusion rates:** 70% or higher (specific percentage not completed in excerpt) [pubmed_40195641]
- **Operative time range:** 98–206 minutes [pubmed_40195641]
- **Complication details:** NOT fully specified in the provided excerpt

### Biportal vs Open Surgery (Lumbar Degenerative Disease)
**Study Design:** Meta-analysis of 8 comparative studies: 414 open vs 383 biportal endoscopic procedures [pubmed_40797374]

Outcomes analyzed included complication rates, but **specific numerical complication data was NOT provided** in the available excerpt [pubmed_40797374].

---

## Surgical Efficiency Metrics

### Operative Time
- **Biportal vs Uniportal:** Biportal demonstrated **significantly longer operative times** despite improved visualization and instrumentation ease [pubmed_41098550]
- Uniportal surgery showed **superior operative efficiency with significantly shorter operative times** [pubmed_41559891]

### Blood Loss and Hospital Stay
**Network Meta-Analysis Finding [pubmed_41420307]:**
- BE-TLIF and FE-TLIF (full endoscopic TLIF) demonstrated **reduced perioperative blood loss** compared to MIS-TLIF
- **Shorter hospital stays** compared to MIS-TLIF [pubmed_41420307]

---

## Critical Evidence Gaps

1. **No direct RCT comparing UBE-TLIF to conventional open TLIF** – The closest comparisons are with MIS-TLIF or other endoscopic variants
2. **Limited ODI numerical data** – Most papers report qualitative findings (e.g., "superior," "comparable") without exact ODI scores or mean differences
3. **Incomplete complication reporting** – While complication rates are identified as measured outcomes, specific percentages are not provided in available excerpts
4. **Clinical significance unclear** – Biportal advantages in ODI "did not consistently achieve minimal clinically important difference thresholds" [pubmed_41559891]
