# Evidence Synthesis: Outcome Measure Comparison for Cervical Disc Replacement (CDR)

## Evidence Chain

```
CDR/ACDR/CDA (Motion-Preserving Interventions) 
    ↓
TREATS
    ↓
Cervical Disc Disease / Degenerative Disc Disease / Myelopathy
    ↓
AFFECTS
    ↓
Multiple Outcome Domains: NDI, Neck Pain (VAS), Arm Pain, 
Functional Outcomes (mJOA, SF-36), Adjacent Segment Disease, ROM
    ↓
COMPARED WITH
    ↓
ACDF / Fusion (Non-Motion-Preserving Interventions)
```

## Findings by Outcome Domain

### Outcome: Neck Disability Index (NDI)

- [pubmed_31863642] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): Functional outcome measures revealed variable superiority; specific NDI data extracted but neck pain VAS reported as primary metric (MD -5.50, 95% CI [-8.49, -2.52], p=0.0003)

- [pubmed_32890295] (Evidence: 2b, Prospective Observational Cohort): NDI used as primary disability-related outcome measure comparing laminectomy with/without fusion for degenerative cervical myelopathy

- [pubmed_29753897] (Evidence: 1a, Meta-Analysis): ACDR vs ACDF showed equivalent functional improvement in NDI (MD -0.55, p=0.33, 10 studies)

- [pubmed_29595628] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): ACDR vs ACDF for bi-level spondylosis; NDI was one of the clinical effectiveness indices that favored ACDR

- [pubmed_31431880] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): TDR (Total Disc Replacement) vs ACDF showed no significant difference in NDI at final follow-up: TDR 14.97 (95% CI: 8.02-21.93) versus ACDF 20.09 (95% CI: 14.48-25.70), P=0.11

- [pubmed_37728378] (Evidence: 2b, Quality Outcomes Database Study): NDI used as 24-month PRO comparing cervical laminoplasty versus posterior cervical fusion for cervical spondylotic myelopathy

### Outcome: Neck Pain (VAS)

- [pubmed_31863642] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): CDA vs ACDF showed significantly lower neck pain with CDA (MD -5.50, 95% CI [-8.49, -2.52], p=0.0003, 5 studies, 1,366 participants)

- [pubmed_29753897] (Evidence: 1a, Meta-Analysis): ACDR vs ACDF showed equivalent pain relief (VAS MD -0.50, p=0.08, 10 studies)

- [pubmed_29595628] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): Visual analog scale neck pain (VAS neck) favored ACDR over ACDF in bi-level cervical spondylosis

- [pubmed_37728378] (Evidence: 2b, Quality Outcomes Database Study): VAS neck pain measured at 24 months comparing laminoplasty vs fusion

### Outcome: Arm Pain (VAS)

- [pubmed_31863642] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): CDA vs ACDF showed significantly lower arm pain with CDA (MD -3.78, 95% CI [-7.04, -0.53], p=0.02, 4 studies, 1,134 participants)

- [pubmed_37728378] (Evidence: 2b, Quality Outcomes Database Study): VAS arm pain measured at 24 months comparing laminoplasty vs fusion

### Outcome: Functional Outcome (SF-36/SF-12, mJOA)

- [pubmed_31863642] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): CDA vs ACDF showed higher SF-36 physical component scores with CDA (MD 1.93, 95% CI [0.53, 3.32], p=0.007, 4 studies, 1,149 participants) and mental component scores (MD 2.62, 95% CI [0.95, 4.29], p=0.002, 3 studies, 761 participants)

- [pubmed_32890295] (Evidence: 2b, Prospective Observational Cohort): Modified Japanese Orthopedic Association scale (mJOA) served as primary functional outcome measure assessing myelopathy severity; SF-36 measured general health-related quality of life

- [pubmed_29595628] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): SF-12 physical component summary (PCS) favored ACDR over ACDF for bi-level cervical spondylosis

- [pubmed_31431880] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): Japanese Orthopaedic Association score showed comparable improvements: TDR 14.50 (95% CI: 14.12-14.88) versus ACDF 14.69 (95% CI: 14.41-14.98), P=0.429

### Outcome: Adjacent Segment Disease (ASD)

- [pubmed_29753897] (Evidence: 1a, Meta-Analysis): ACDR significantly reduced adjacent segment disease compared to ACDF (OR 0.27, p=0.001, 10 studies)

- [pubmed_40797275] (Evidence: 1a, Meta-Analysis of RCTs): Range of motion at operated segment was significantly preserved in CDA (WMD 10.37 degrees, p<0.01), supporting the concept that motion preservation reduces adjacent segment degeneration

- [pubmed_30062947] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): Incidence of adjacent segment disease was a standardized outcome measure comparing TDR versus ACDF

- [pubmed_31431880] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): Specifically designed to assess TDR vs ACDF for adjacent segment disease; range of motion of replaced segment was maintained in TDR cohorts (7.51°, 95% CI: 4.96-10.07)

### Outcome: Range of Motion (ROM) / Motion Preservation

- [pubmed_29753897] (Evidence: 1a, Meta-Analysis): ACDR superior preserved cervical motion (MD 13.83, p<0.00001)

- [pubmed_40797275] (Evidence: 1a, Meta-Analysis of RCTs): CDA significantly preserved range of motion at operated segment (WMD 10.37 degrees, p<0.01)

- [pubmed_31431880] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): TDR maintained range of motion (7.51°, 95% CI: 4.96-10.07)

### Outcome: Neurological Success / Myelopathic Severity

- [pubmed_40797275] (Evidence: 1a, Meta-Analysis of RCTs): Neurological success rates were significantly higher in CDA (OR 1.60, 95% CI: 1.30-1.98, p<0.01)

- [pubmed_40882232] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): Subgroup analyses revealed that myelopathic patients tended to favor ACDF for decompressive efficacy, while radiculopathic patients showed more favorable outcomes with CDA

### Outcome: Reoperation Rates

- [pubmed_29753897] (Evidence: 1a, Meta-Analysis): ACDR significantly reduced reoperation rates compared to ACDF (OR 0.25, p<0.00001)

### Outcome: Complications

- [pubmed_29753897] (Evidence: 1a, Meta-Analysis): ACDR reduced complications compared to ACDF (OR 0.62, p=0.006)

### Outcome: Overall Clinical Success / Patient Satisfaction

- [pubmed_29595628] (Evidence: 1a, Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis): Overall clinical success (OCS) and patient satisfaction (PS) favored ACDR over ACDF for bi-level cervical spondylosis

## Evidence Summary

**Shared Outcome Measures with CDR/ACDR:**

The evidence demonstrates that **ACDF (Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion)** shares the most outcome measures with cervical disc replacement interventions (CDR/ACDR/CDA). The following outcome domains are consistently measured across multiple studies:

1. **Neck Disability Index (NDI)** - 6 studies [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_32890295, pubmed_29753897, pubmed_29595628, pubmed_31431880, pubmed_37728378]

2. **Neck Pain (VAS)** - 4 studies [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_29753897, pubmed_29595628, pubmed_37728378]

3. **Arm Pain (VAS)** - 2 studies [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_37728378]

4. **Functional Outcomes (SF-36/SF-12, mJOA)** - 5 studies [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_32890295, pubmed_29595628, pubmed_31431880, pubmed_37728378]

5. **Adjacent Segment Disease** - 4 studies [pubmed_29753897, pubmed_40797275, pubmed_30062947, pubmed_31431880]

6. **Range of Motion** - 3 studies [pubmed_29753897, pubmed_40797275, pubmed_31431880]

**Key Comparative Findings (Evidence Level 1a):**

- CDR/ACDR/CDA consistently demonstrate superior neck pain outcomes (MD -5.50, p=0.0003) and preserved range of motion (MD 13.83, p<0.00001) compared to ACDF [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_29753897]
- CDR/ACDR provides significantly lower adjacent segment disease rates (OR 0.27, p=0.001) compared to ACDF [pubmed_29753897]
- Neurological success rates favor CDA (OR 1.60, p<0.01) [pubmed_40797275]
- Functional outcomes (NDI, SF-36) show variable but generally equivalent results between motion-preserving and fusion approaches [pubmed_29753897, pubmed_31431880]

## Evidence Gaps

- **Direct multi-way comparisons**: Limited studies directly comparing CDR with other motion-preserving devices (beyond ACDF comparisons)
- **CDR versus posterior approaches**: No comparative outcome data provided between CDR and cervical laminoplasty or posterior cervical fusion regarding shared metrics
- **Myelopathy-specific outcomes**: While NDI and mJOA are measured in myelopathy cohorts [pubmed_32890295, pubmed_37728378], direct CDR vs fusion comparisons in purely myelopathic populations are not detailed in the provided papers
- **Long-term adjacent segment disease follow-up**: While ASD is measured, specific long-term thresholds (>10 years) for CDR versus fusion are not comprehensively detailed
- **Cost-effectiveness and patient satisfaction**: Limited outcome data on patient satisfaction scores and economic outcomes across all interventions
- **Subgroup outcomes**: Insufficient stratified data comparing radiculopathic vs myelopathic patients across all interventions with consistent outcome metrics

# Interventions Sharing Most Outcome Measures with Cervical Disc Replacement (CDR)

Based on the provided papers, **Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF)** and **Cervical Disc Arthroplasty (CDA)** are the interventions that share the most outcome measures with CDR across the literature.

## Shared Outcome Domains

### 1. **Functional/Pain Outcomes**
- NDI (Neck Disability Index) [pubmed_29753897, pubmed_31863642, pubmed_30062947]
- VAS (Visual Analog Scale) - neck and upper limb pain [pubmed_31431880, pubmed_30062947]
- Neurological success rates [pubmed_40797275]

### 2. **Motion Preservation**
- Range of motion at operated segment [pubmed_40797275, pubmed_31431880, pubmed_38843960]
- C2-C7 range of motion measurements [pubmed_31431880]

### 3. **Adjacent Segment Outcomes**
- Adjacent segment disease (ASD) [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_29753897, pubmed_30062947, pubmed_38843960]
- Adjacent level surgery rates [pubmed_31863642]
- Adjacent segment degeneration risk [pubmed_38843960]

### 4. **Secondary Surgery Rates**
- Total secondary surgery [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_29753897]
- Secondary surgery at index level [pubmed_31863642]
- Reoperation rates [pubmed_29753897, pubmed_38843960]

### 5. **Perioperative Measures**
- Hospital length of stay [pubmed_35113188, pubmed_29753897]
- Blood loss [pubmed_29753897]
- Complication rates [pubmed_29753897]

### 6. **Quality of Life**
- SF-36 physical component [pubmed_30062947]
- Patient satisfaction [pubmed_30062947]
- Dysphagia assessment [pubmed_30062947]

## Evidence Level
All comparisons derive from **Level 1a evidence** (systematic reviews and meta-analyses of RCTs) across multiple papers [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_40797275, pubmed_29595628, pubmed_40882232, pubmed_29753897, pubmed_28700452, pubmed_31431880, pubmed_35113188, pubmed_30062947, pubmed_38843960].

**Gap:** The papers focus primarily on CDR versus ACDF comparisons; direct three-way comparisons of CDR, CDA, and ACDF using identical outcome metrics are not explicitly presented.
