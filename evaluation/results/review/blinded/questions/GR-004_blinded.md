1. **Primary functional outcomes**: NDI (5 studies), VAS neck pain (2 studies), VAS arm pain (1 study)
2. **Secondary functional outcomes**: SF-36 physical component (1 study)
3. **Biomechanical outcomes**: Range of motion preservation (3 studies)
4. **Safety outcomes**: Neurological success (1 study), complications/reoperation rates (1 study), adjacent segment disease (2 studies)

**Evidence Level Summary**:
- All comparisons are supported by Level 1a evidence (systematic reviews and meta-analyses of RCTs)
- CDR demonstrates superior or equivalent outcomes to ACDF across most domains, with particular advantages in pain reduction, motion preservation, and reducing adjacent segment disease
- Functional disability (NDI) shows mixed results depending on disease level (equivalent for single-level, superior for bi-level)

# Outcome Measure Overlap Analysis for Cervical Disc Replacement

Based on the provided systematic reviews, **Cervical Disc Arthroplasty (CDA)/Total Disc Replacement (TDR) and Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF)** are the two interventions that share the most outcome measures in the database, as they are directly compared across all included meta-analyses.

## Shared Outcome Measures

### **Primary Clinical Outcomes**
1. **Pain Relief (VAS/NDI scores)** [pubmed_29753897, pubmed_31431880, pubmed_30062947]
   - Pain comparable between CDR and ACDF (VAS MD -0.50, p=0.08) [pubmed_29753897]
   - NDI functional improvement equivalent between groups [pubmed_29753897]

2. **Neurological Success** [pubmed_40797275]
   - CDA demonstrated superior rates (OR 1.60, 95% CI: 1.30-1.98, p<0.01)

3. **Range of Motion** [pubmed_40797275, pubmed_31431880, pubmed_38843960]
   - WMD 10.37 degrees preserved in CDA [pubmed_40797275]
   - C2-C7 ROM significantly higher in TDR (40.16° vs 35.10°, p=0.001) [pubmed_31431880]

### **Secondary Outcomes**
4. **Adjacent Segment Disease** [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_29753897, pubmed_38843960, pubmed_30062947]
   - OR 0.40 (95% CI [0.28, 0.58]) for CDA in US studies [pubmed_31863642]
   - OR 0.27 (p=0.001) for ACDR [pubmed_29753897]

5. **Reoperation/Secondary Surgery Rates** [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_29753897, pubmed_28700452]
   - OR 0.48 total secondary surgery [pubmed_31863642]
   - OR 0.25 reoperation rates [pubmed_29753897]

6. **Hospital Length of Stay** [pubmed_35113188, pubmed_29753897]
   - Comparable between CDR and ACDF groups

7. **Complications** [pubmed_29753897]
   - OR 0.62 (p=0.006) favoring ACDR

## Evidence Quality
All comparisons derive from **Level 1a evidence** (systematic reviews and meta-analyses of RCTs) [pubmed_31863642, pubmed_40797275, pubmed_29595628, pubmed_40882232, pubmed_29753897, pubmed_28700452, pubmed_35113188, pubmed_31431880, pubmed_30062947, pubmed_38843960].

**Gap in Evidence:** No direct comparisons with other spinal interventions (e.g., posterior cervical foraminotomy alone or non-surgical management) are comprehensively reported in this database.
