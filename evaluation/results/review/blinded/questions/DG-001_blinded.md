# Evidence Comparison: UBE versus Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## ODI (Oswestry Disability Index) Improvement

**Limited Direct Comparison Available:**

One study [pubmed_41290735] (Evidence: 2b, retrospective comparative) reported that both uniportal and unilateral biportal endoscopic fusion are **superior to MIS-TLIF in terms of early postoperative low back pain relief**, particularly within 3 months post-surgery. However, this study noted **"no significant difference in early leg pain relief and functional improvement"** between endoscopic techniques and MIS-TLIF. Critically, the authors acknowledge **"at present, there is a shortage of cross-comparison research between the two techniques."**

The meta-analysis by [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) comparing conventional open surgery to biportal endoscopy found ODI improvements favoring biportal endoscopy, but this compares open surgery rather than TLIF specifically.

## Complication Rates

**Comprehensive Evidence Unavailable:**

The retrieved literature does not provide direct comparative complication rates between UBE and conventional TLIF. The meta-analysis [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) analyzed complication rates between conventional open surgery and biportal endoscopy across 414 open and 383 biportal procedures, but conventional open surgery is not equivalent to TLIF outcomes.

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Direct RCT comparison lacking** between UBE and conventional TLIF for lumbar stenosis
2. **Functional outcome data limited** - most studies compare UBE to MIS-TLIF rather than standard TLIF
3. **Complication rate comparisons** specifically for UBE versus conventional TLIF not available in provided literature

**Recommendation:** High-quality RCTs directly comparing these techniques are needed for definitive conclusions.

# Evidence Summary: UBE versus Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## ODI (Functional) Improvement

### UBE vs. Conventional/Open TLIF
**Evidence Level 1a (Network Meta-Analysis):** BE-TLIF (biportal endoscopic TLIF) demonstrated **superior short-term functional outcomes** compared to MIS-TLIF, with improved ODI scores in the early postoperative period [pubmed_41420307]. However, specific ODI numerical values at follow-up timepoints are not provided in this comparison.

### UBE vs. Minimally Invasive Techniques (Broader Context)
**Evidence Level 1a (Meta-Analysis):** When comparing uniportal versus biportal endoscopic approaches, biportal surgery offered **improved early functional recovery with lower ODI at 1-3 months**, which persisted at final follow-up. However, this advantage **did not consistently achieve minimal clinically important difference (MCID) thresholds** [pubmed_41559891].

A prospective RCT protocol is underway comparing UBE-TLIF to microscopic tubular (MT) TLIF, using **ODI score at 1-year postoperation as the primary outcome with MCID to determine clinically meaningful differences** [pubmed_39322595], suggesting current evidence gaps requiring this direct comparison.

## Complication Rates

**Evidence Gap:** The provided papers do not report specific complication rate comparisons between UBE and conventional open TLIF. While [pubmed_40797374] mentions comparable postoperative outcomes between biportal endoscopy and open surgery, specific complication data are not detailed.

## Clinical Summary

**Direct evidence comparing UBE versus conventional TLIF is limited.** Available data suggests biportal techniques offer superior short-term functional recovery, but clinically meaningful differences require validation in ongoing RCTs. Comprehensive complication rate comparisons are not available in these papers.
