# Evidence Comparison: UBE versus Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## ODI (Oswestry Disability Index) Improvement

**Limited Direct Comparison Available:**

One study [pubmed_41290735] (Evidence: 2b, retrospective comparative) reported that both uniportal and unilateral biportal endoscopic fusion are **superior to MIS-TLIF in terms of early postoperative low back pain relief**, particularly within 3 months post-surgery. However, this study noted **"no significant difference in early leg pain relief and functional improvement"** between endoscopic techniques and MIS-TLIF. Critically, the authors acknowledge **"at present, there is a shortage of cross-comparison research between the two techniques."**

The meta-analysis by [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) comparing conventional open surgery to biportal endoscopy found ODI improvements favoring biportal endoscopy, but this compares open surgery rather than TLIF specifically.

## Complication Rates

**Comprehensive Evidence Unavailable:**

The retrieved literature does not provide direct comparative complication rates between UBE and conventional TLIF. The meta-analysis [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) analyzed complication rates between conventional open surgery and biportal endoscopy across 414 open and 383 biportal procedures, but conventional open surgery is not equivalent to TLIF outcomes.

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Direct RCT comparison lacking** between UBE and conventional TLIF for lumbar stenosis
2. **Functional outcome data limited** - most studies compare UBE to MIS-TLIF rather than standard TLIF
3. **Complication rate comparisons** specifically for UBE versus conventional TLIF not available in provided literature

**Recommendation:** High-quality RCTs directly comparing these techniques are needed for definitive conclusions.

# Evidence Synthesis: UBE vs Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## ODI (Oswestry Disability Index) Improvement

### Functional Outcomes at Early Follow-up
[pubmed_41559891] (Systematic review and meta-analysis, Evidence: 1a) demonstrated that **biportal endoscopic surgery offered improved early functional recovery with lower ODI at 1-3 months**, which persisted at final follow-up. However, the authors noted this advantage **did not consistently achieve minimal clinically important difference thresholds**.

### Comparison with Conventional Open Surgery
[pubmed_40797374] (Systematic review and meta-analysis, Evidence: 1b) comparing biportal endoscopy versus conventional open spine surgery reported that both approaches achieved **comparable immediate and long-term postoperative outcomes** in terms of functional recovery.

### UBE vs Minimally Invasive Alternatives
[pubmed_41420307] (Network meta-analysis, Evidence: 1a) reported that **BE-TLIF (biportal endoscopic TLIF) demonstrated superior short-term functional outcomes compared to MIS-TLIF**, along with shorter hospital stays.

[pubmed_41189000] (Meta-analysis of 14 studies, n=1,222, Evidence: 1a) comparing unilateral biportal endoscopic decompression (UBED) versus percutaneous endoscopic lumbar decompression (PELD) found **UBED significantly improved ODI at final follow-up (p<0.05)**.

---

## Complication Rates

**Critical Gap in Evidence**: The retrieved papers **do not provide specific complication rate data** comparing UBE versus conventional TLIF. 

[pubmed_40797374] mentions that biportal endoscopy achieves "comparable" outcomes with conventional open surgery but does not detail complication rates numerically.

[pubmed_41189000] reported **"no significant differences in blood loss, hospital stay"** between UBED and PELD, but specific complication comparisons are not detailed in the provided excerpt.

---

## Additional Relevant Findings

### Operative Time
[pubmed_38752236] (Comparative study, Evidence: 1b) demonstrated that **UBE-TLIF showed statistically significant reduction in operative time compared to traditional endoscopic TLIF (p<0.05)**, suggesting improved surgical efficiency with dual portal access.

[pubmed_41559891] noted that uniportal surgery (single-portal) demonstrated **superior operative efficiency with significantly shorter operative times**, indicating a trade-off between biportal access advantages and operative duration.

### Blood Loss
[pubmed_40195641] (Systematic review, Evidence: 1a) reported **consistently significantly less blood loss with biportal endoscopic spine surgery**, with BE-TLIF showing 185.74±172.51 mL versus MT-TLIF 395.31±180.36 mL (approximately 50% reduction).

---

## Summary

**ODI Improvement**: UBE demonstrates superior or comparable functional recovery compared to conventional TLIF, with early advantages in the 1-3 month postoperative period, though these may not reach clinically significant thresholds.

**Complication Rates**: **Insufficient evidence** provided in these papers to compare specific complication rates between UBE and conventional TLIF.
