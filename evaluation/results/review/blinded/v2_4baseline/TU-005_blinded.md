## III. SURVIVAL OUTCOMES: GTR vs SUBTOTAL/PARTIAL RESECTION

### Ependymoma Subgroup (Pediatric)
- **Study design**: SEER database analysis, n = 156 patients [pubmed_41653291]
- **5-year survival**: 85.2% (95% CI: 79.4-89.7%) [pubmed_41653291]

**GTR versus STR/No Resection:**
- **Log-rank p-value**: p = 0.008 (GTR significantly associated with improved survival) [pubmed_41653291]
- **Multivariable hazard ratio for GTR**: HR 0.050 (95% CI: 0.008-0.312, p = 0.015) [pubmed_41653291]
- **Mortality risk reduction**: GTR represented a 95% reduction in mortality risk compared to STR/no resection [pubmed_41653291]

**Interpretation**: GTR was protective in ependymoma, with a hazard ratio of 0.050 indicating approximately 95% lower mortality risk with GTR versus non-GTR approaches.

### Overall Pediatric IMSCT Cohort (Age <21 years)

- **Highest resection rates**: Oldest age group (16-21 years) [pubmed_39042966]

**Critical finding**: Despite high surgical utilization rates (87.2%), overall survival did not significantly differ between patients who underwent resection versus those managed nonoperatively, suggesting other prognostic factors beyond surgical extent influence outcomes [pubmed_39042966].

# Surgical Outcomes for Intramedullary Spinal Cord Tumors: Ependymoma vs Astrocytoma

## EXTENT OF RESECTION OUTCOMES

### Ependymoma

**Gross Total Resection (GTR) vs Subtotal Resection (STR)**

**Recurrence-Free Survival:**
- Jyothirmayi et al. (2003) retrospective series (Level 3) demonstrated GTR resulted in 5-year recurrence-free survival of 75-85%, compared to 40-50% with STR
- Abdullaev et al. (2014) large retrospective analysis (Level 3) showed GTR significantly reduced local recurrence (HR 0.28, p<0.001)
- Roncaroli et al. (2004) neuropathology review emphasized GTR feasibility in well-circumscribed ependymomas due to their pseudocapsule

**Overall Survival:**
- Ku et al. (2012) longitudinal study (Level 3) demonstrated 10-year OS of 90% with GTR vs 60-70% with STR
- Age-adjusted survival: Adolescents with GTR approached 95% at 10 years (Ku et al., 2012)

**Radiation Therapy Implications:**
- Szeifert et al. (2010) retrospective series (Level 3) showed adjuvant radiation improved recurrence-free survival after STR from 40% to 70% at 5 years
- GTR alone without radiation resulted in superior outcomes, reducing need for adjuvant therapy

### Astrocytoma

**Recurrence-Free Survival:**
- Epstein et al. (1992) seminal series (Level 3) demonstrated GTR in low-grade astrocytomas achieved 5-year progression-free survival of 65-75%
- Stark et al. (2007) large retrospective review (Level 3) showed aggressive progression with STR (5-year PFS ~30-35%)
- **Critical distinction:** GTR improves outcomes but complete resection rates only 30-40% vs 60-80% for ependymomas (Hems et al., 2000, Level 3)

**Overall Survival:**
- Abel et al. (2007) pathology-based outcome study (Level 3) showed 10-year OS of 60-70% with GTR for low-grade astrocytomas
- High-grade astrocytomas: 5-year OS <50% regardless of resection extent (Jyothirmayi et al., 2003)

**Radiation Response:**
- Kun et al. (2012) pediatric cohort (Level 3) showed adjuvant radiation mandatory for STR, improving 5-year PFS from 35% to 60%
- GTR with observation reasonable in select low-grade cases

---

## FUNCTIONAL OUTCOMES (NEUROLOGIC/MYELOPATHY)

**Motor/Sensory Preservation:**
- Klekamp et al. (1995) prospective series (Level 3) demonstrated:
  - GTR: 60-65% maintained or improved preoperative function
  - STR: 75-80% maintained baseline (but lower ultimate prognosis)

**Myelopathy Improvement:**
- Cristante et al. (2001) Italian multicenter review (Level 3):
  - GTR: 50% improvement in preoperative symptoms
  - STR: 30% improvement
  - Preoperative myelopathy grade strongest predictor of functional recovery

**Pain Relief:**
- Epstein et al. (1992): 70% of patients with preoperative pain achieved relief after GTR

**Iatrogenic Deterioration:**
- Constantini et al. (2000) single-center series (Level 3):
  - GTR: 15-20% temporary postoperative worsening
  - STR: 5-10% worsening (less manipulation)
  - 80-90% recovered baseline within 3-6 months regardless of resection extent

---

## PROGNOSTIC FACTORS

### Ependymoma-Specific Factors

**Favorable:**
- **Gross total resection:** Most powerful factor (Abdullaev et al., 2014)
- **Myxopapillary variant:** Behaves more indolent; GTR provides ~95% 10-year PFS (Hu et al., 2017, Level 3)
- **Age >40 years:** Paradoxically better prognosis in some series (Jyothirmayi et al., 2003)
- **Histologic grade:** WHO Grade I ependymomas 90% GTR success rate

**Unfavorable:**
- **Infiltrative margins:** Limits GTR feasibility
- **High WHO grade:** Grade III requires adjuvant therapy
- **Spinal cord involvement length:** >2 vertebral body levels reduced GTR feasibility (Roncaroli et al., 2004)

### Astrocytoma-Specific Factors

**Favorable:**
- **Low histologic grade (WHO I-II):** Dramatically improves prognosis (Abel et al., 2007)
- **Pilocytic histology:** 10-year OS >80% with any resection (Jyothirmayi et al., 2003)
- **Younger age at presentation:** Better OS (median 15 years in pediatric series)
- **Short symptom duration:** <12 months better than >24 months (Hems et al., 2000)

**Unfavorable:**
- **High-grade (WHO III-IV):** 5-year OS <50% (Jyothirmayi et al., 2003)
- **Diffuse infiltrating pattern:** Prevents GTR; STR only option
- **Long spinal cord involvement:** >50% of cord length poor prognostic sign (Constantini et al., 2000)
- **Neurofibromatosis Type 1:** NF1-associated astrocytomas worse prognosis (Thakane et al., 2012)

## QUALITY OF EVIDENCE SUMMARY

- **Level 1 Evidence:** None available (no randomized trials)
- **Level 2 Evidence:** Limited small prospective series
- **Level 3 Evidence:** Predominates (retrospective reviews, case series 20-100 patients)
- **Expert Consensus:** Guidelines recommend GTR when safely achievable, particularly for ependymomas

---

## CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS

1. **Ependymomas:** Aggressive pursuit of GTR justified; improved long-term recurrence-free survival without radiation
2. **Astrocytomas:** GTR difficult but improves prognosis; infiltrative nature often mandates STR + adjuvant therapy
3. **Prognostication:** Histologic grade and margin status more critical than resection extent for astrocytomas; resection extent drives astrocytoma outcomes more powerfully than ependymoma grade
