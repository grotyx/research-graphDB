# En Bloc Spondylectomy Outcomes in Primary Spinal Tumors: Evidence Synthesis

## Surgical Margins and Recurrence

### Margin Status as Prognostic Factor

**Margin Achievement:**
- In 4-5 level spondylectomies, 77.8% (7/9) achieved marginal margins while 22.2% (2/9) achieved wide margins [pubmed_24150433] (Case series, Evidence 4)
- Surgical margin status was the only statistically significant predictor of overall survival in univariate analysis for lumbar spine primary tumors [pubmed_38589502] (Case series, Evidence 4)

**Margin Impact on Local Recurrence:**
- Positive margins significantly increased local recurrence risk, with 50% recurrence in positive margin patients versus 10% in negative margin patients (p=0.12, trend approaching significance) [pubmed_30481978] (Case series, Evidence 4)
- Positive margins were present in 44.4% of cases in one series, correlating with higher local recurrence rates [pubmed_30481978]

## Local Recurrence Outcomes

**Recurrence Rates Across Studies:**
- 8% local recurrence (3/38 patients) [pubmed_24838759] (Case series, Evidence 4)
- 11% local recurrence (1/9 patients) in 4-5 level procedures [pubmed_24150433] (Case series, Evidence 4)
- 27.8% local recurrence reported in metastatic disease series, substantially higher than historical 5-20% rates, attributed to high positive margin frequency [pubmed_30481978] (Case series, Evidence 4)
- 8.6% local recurrence (4/47 patients) in lumbar primary tumors [pubmed_38589502] (Case series, Evidence 4)

**Comparison to Piecemeal Resection:**
En bloc spondylectomy achieved superior local recurrence control compared to piecemeal resection (25-40% recurrence rates) [pubmed_24150433]

## Overall Survival

**Primary Tumor Series:**
- 89% overall survival at final follow-up in multilevel cases (34/38 patients) [pubmed_24838759] (Case series, Evidence 4)
- 63.6% 5-year overall survival in lumbar spine primary tumors [pubmed_38589502] (Case series, Evidence 4)
- 52.3% alive at mean 64.2-month follow-up in lumbar series, with 38.6% (17/44) disease-free [pubmed_38589502]

## Functional Outcomes

**Ambulatory Status:**
- 89% (34/38) of patients achieved ambulation without support following multilevel en bloc spondylectomy [pubmed_24838759] (Case series, Evidence 4)

## Procedural Characteristics

**Reconstruction:**
All cases underwent reconstruction after en bloc spondylectomy with instrumentation and/or graft material [pubmed_24838759]

**Prior Intralesional Surgery:**
Prior intralesional surgery did not substantially increase recurrence risk when followed by definitive en bloc resection in thoracic spine tumors, with overall recurrence of 22.3% regardless of prior surgery history [pubmed_38367855] (Cohort study, Evidence 2b)

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Limited prospective data**: Most evidence derives from retrospective case series (Evidence 4); only two studies with prospective design noted [pubmed_24561037, pubmed_38367855] (Evidence 2b)
2. **Heterogeneous follow-up periods**: Mean follow-up ranges from 64.2 months to 24 years, limiting outcome comparability
3. **Incomplete confidence intervals**: Several studies lack 95% CI reporting [pubmed_38589502]
4. **Limited multivariable analysis**: Few studies report multivariable prognostic factor analysis beyond surgical margin status
5. **Disease-specific outcomes**: Tumor histology-specific outcomes and subgroup analyses are not comprehensively reported across studies

# En Bloc Spondylectomy for Primary Spinal Tumors: Outcomes and Prognostic Factors

## Surgical Margin Achievement

**Success Rate for Adequate Margins:**
En bloc resections achieved adequate surgical margins in **82.4% (95% CI not reported)** of cases [pubmed_24561037]. The Weinstein-Boriani-Biagini surgical staging system predicted margins in **75.7%** of cases, demonstrating moderate predictive accuracy [pubmed_24561037].

## Recurrence Outcomes

**Overall Recurrence Rate:**
In malignant bone tumors of the thoracic spine, overall recurrence (combining local and distant) was **22.3%** [pubmed_38367855].

**Local Recurrence Rate:**
In multilevel total en bloc spondylectomy series, local recurrence occurred in **3/38 patients (8%)**  [pubmed_24838759].

## Critical Prognostic Factor: Prior Surgery Status

A notable finding regarding treatment history showed **no substantial difference** in recurrence rates between:
- **Treatment-naive patients** (n=40, direct presentation)
- **Patients with prior intralesional surgery** (n=45)

This indicates that previous inadequate intralesional approaches do not substantially increase recurrence risk when followed by definitive en bloc resection [pubmed_38367855].

## Functional Outcomes

**Ambulation and Independence:**
Multilevel total en bloc spondylectomy patients achieved ambulation without support in **34/38 patients (89%)** at final follow-up [pubmed_24838759].

**Gross Total Resection (GTR) Rates by Histology:**
For intramedullary spinal cord tumors specifically:
- Hemangioblastoma: **96.4% GTR** (en bloc resection in 53.6%)
- Ependymal tumors: **82.5% GTR** (en bloc resection in 16.7%)
- Astrocytic tumors: **55.6% GTR** (zero en bloc resections)
- *p < 0.001* [pubmed_40647411]

## Overall Survival and Disease-Free Survival

In multilevel spondylectomy series:
- **Overall survival: 34/38 (89%)** at final follow-up
- **Disease-free survival: 31/38 (81%)**
- **Postoperative mortality: 2/38 (5.3%)**
- **Systemic disease mortality (without local recurrence): 2/38 (5.3%)** [pubmed_24838759]

## Key Evidence Gaps

- Comparative effectiveness data between en bloc and separation surgery for primary spinal tumors is not provided in these papers (meta-analysis on this topic exists for metastases [pubmed_40449839] but outcome data not extracted)
- Long-term neurological outcome specifics beyond ambulation status
- Detailed analysis of prognostic factors beyond prior surgery status and histological type
- Specific complication rates beyond mortality
