### Perioperative Safety:
- The study examined **mental health disorders and surgical safety in adolescent patients** undergoing spine surgery

⚠️ **Major limitations in provided literature:**

1. **No randomized controlled trials** comparing surgery directly to conservative treatment for AIS
2. **No clear surgical indications** are delineated in the retrieved papers

The provided evidence **cannot adequately answer the clinical question** regarding surgical versus conservative treatment indications for AIS. ## 1. CONSERVATIVE MANAGEMENT OUTCOMES

### Long-term Conservative Management (Moderate AIS, 30-45°)

**Study Design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis [pubmed_40551470]
- **Sample:** 13 studies, 1,492 patients with minimum 10-year follow-up
- **Indication:** Moderate AIS (Cobb angle 30-45°)
- **Outcomes Assessed:** Radiographic curve progression, surgery rates, sagittal alignment, back pain rates, quality of life

**Critical Gap:** The abstract provides no specific quantitative findings (progression rates, surgery rates, pain outcomes, or QOL measures). The numerical data necessary to understand conservative management effectiveness is not reported in the retrieved excerpt [pubmed_40551470].

---

## 2. SURGICAL INDICATIONS INFERRED FROM INCLUDED STUDIES

The papers provide evidence of current surgical practice patterns but not explicit indication guidelines:

### Surgical Decision-Making by Curve Magnitude

**Large-magnitude AIS (≥70°):**
- Comparative analysis notes these curves exhibit "increased rigidity" and "posing challenges to conventional posterior-based fusion techniques" [pubmed_41636888]
- Both posterior-only and anterior release with posterior fusion approaches are utilized, suggesting these magnitude curves typically require surgery [pubmed_41636888]

**Moderate AIS (30-45°):**
- Conservative management with orthotics/bracing is employed [pubmed_40551470], suggesting surgery is NOT routine in this range

**Severe AIS (Cobb angle >45°):**
- Identified as threshold warranting consideration for surgical intervention [pubmed_38690524]

---

## 3. SURGICAL TECHNIQUES AND OUTCOMES

### Pedicle Screw Accuracy (Robotic vs. Conventional)

**Study Design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis [pubmed_41230988]
- **Sample:** 11,434 pedicle screws analyzed (59.6% from AIS patients)
- **Overall accuracy:** 97.2% ± 3.64% [pubmed_41230988]
- **Malposition rate:** 2.67% ± 3.61% [pubmed_41230988]
- **Revision rate:** 0.79% ± 0.51% [pubmed_41230988]

**Robot-Assisted vs. Conventional Surgery [pubmed_40932300]:**
- **Cobb angle correction:** MD -0.59° (95% CI -2.14 to 0.96, p=0.46, I²=0%) — no significant difference [pubmed_40932300]
- **Curve correction:** MD -1.48° (95% CI -5.83 to 2.87, p=0.50, I²=80.1%) — no significant difference [pubmed_40932300]
- **Clinical Interpretation:** Superior screw accuracy with robotics did NOT translate to improved radiographic deformity correction [pubmed_40932300]

### Anterior Release with Posterior Fusion vs. Posterior-Only Fusion

**Study Design:** Comparative analysis (evidence level 2b) [pubmed_41636888]
- **Population:** Large-magnitude AIS (≥70°)
- **Specific outcomes:** Not quantitatively reported in the retrieved excerpt

## CLINICAL INDICATIONS INFERRED FROM PRACTICE PATTERNS

Based on the included studies, current practice suggests:

1. **Conservative management (bracing/orthotics)** is attempted for moderate curves (30-45°) with minimum 10-year follow-up data available [pubmed_40551470]

2. **Surgical intervention** appears indicated for:
   - Large-magnitude curves ≥70° [pubmed_41636888]
   - Curves >45° with evidence of rapid progression or rigidity [pubmed_38690524]

3. **Surgical technique choice** (robotic vs. conventional) does not significantly impact radiographic outcomes despite superior screw accuracy with robotics [pubmed_40932300]

---

## CONCLUSION

**The retrieved literature does NOT provide direct comparative evidence for surgical versus conservative treatment of AIS.** A systematic review of conservative management exists [pubmed_40551470] but specific quantitative outcomes are not reported in the available excerpts. The remaining papers focus exclusively on surgical techniques, anesthetic management, and comorbidities in patients undergoing surgery, without comparative group data from conservatively managed patients.

**To answer this clinical question comprehensively, papers directly comparing surgery and conservative management with parallel outcome measurement (progression rates, pain, QOL, functional outcomes) are required.**
