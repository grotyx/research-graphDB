# Role of ACR/ALIF in Adult Spinal Deformity Correction: Evidence Synthesis

## I. SAGITTAL ALIGNMENT RESTORATION

### Local and Segmental Lordosis (L5-S1 Single-Level)
**ALIF Superior to TLIF** [pubmed_41662143]
- Design: Retrospective cohort study
- ALIF segmental lordosis improvement: **4.2-4.6°** vs TLIF (specific TLIF values not reported in abstract)
- ALIF regional lordosis improvement: **4.2-4.6°**
- **Critical caveat**: These local improvements do NOT translate to significant global spinal alignment changes (L1-S1) or spinopelvic parameters (LPA, PT) in single-level fusion [pubmed_41662143]

### Overall Lordosis Restoration Advantage
**Meta-analytic Evidence** [pubmed_39575949]
- ALIF demonstrates superiority over TLIF and other techniques for lordosis restoration
- Mechanism: Anterior approach provides direct access to disc space and adjacent vertebral bodies, allowing larger implant heights and better segmental + global alignment restoration [pubmed_39575949]

**ALIF vs TLIF—High-Level Evidence** [pubmed_40066769]
- Meta-analysis (Evidence: 1a)
- ALIF is the **preferred approach for L5-S1 fusion** in degenerative spine disease
- Key finding: **Superior sagittal alignment restoration** demonstrated [pubmed_40066769]

### ACR for Multi-Level Deformity
**Mechanism of Action** [pubmed_25820352]
- Design: Multicenter analysis (Evidence: 2b)
- ACR employs **multiple anterolateral discectomies at consecutive levels** allowing **incremental angular correction** while preserving posterior elements [pubmed_41497819]
- Theoretically offers reduced blood loss, shorter operative time, decreased neurological injury risk vs PSO [pubmed_41497819]

## II. ROD FRACTURE AND MECHANICAL COMPLICATIONS

### ACR-Associated Rod Fracture Rate
**Direct Evidence** [pubmed_31229745]
- Design: Retrospective multicenter assessment (Evidence: 2b)
- ACR rod fracture rate: **4.4%**
- **Important association**: Longer fusion constructs associated with rod fracture risk [pubmed_31229745]

### Proximal Junctional Complications Prevention (Multi-Level Fusion)
**ALIF/LLIF + PSF Superior to PSF Alone** [pubmed_29087808]
- Design: Comparative analysis of 3 surgical strategies (Evidence: 2b)
- Study population: Adult spinal deformity with mild-to-moderate sagittal imbalance
- **LLIF+PSF group outcomes:**
  - **Significantly lower rates of PJK requiring revision** (specific percentages not provided in abstract)
  - **Fewer proximal junctional fractures** (specific rates not provided)
  - **Fewer vertebral slips** (specific rates not provided)
- Mechanism: Interbody fusion (LLIF or ALIF) provides improved load-sharing and maintenance of disc height [pubmed_29087808]

## VI. CRITICAL GAPS IN EVIDENCE

1. **Global Sagittal Alignment Impact**: While ALIF restores local L5-S1 lordosis (4.2-4.6°), single-level fusion does NOT significantly alter L1-S1 or spinopelvic parameters (PT, LPA). Multi-level ACR data on global alignment restoration remains limited [pubmed_41662143]

2. **ACR-Specific Long-Term Outcomes**: Rod fracture rate (4.4%) documented, but long-term fusion success rates, clinical outcome scores, and durability beyond 2 years not comprehensively reported [pubmed_31229745]

3. **Specific PJK/PJF Rates**: While LLIF+PSF superior to PSF alone, exact percentages of PJK revision, PJF, and vertebral slip rates not numerically reported [pubmed_29087808]

4. **ALIF vs TLIF Pseudoarthrosis**: Hypothesis stated but specific reoperation rates/percentages not provided in available abstract [pubmed_37146876]

5. **ACR Comparison to PSO**: While ACR theoretically superior (less blood loss, shorter operative time), direct randomized comparison with PSO lacking [pubmed_41497819]

## CLINICAL SUMMARY

**ALIF/ACR provides demonstrated advantage in sagittal alignment restoration** (particularly segmental lordosis at L5-S1: 4.2-4.6° improvement) with **lower overall complication rates** [pubmed_40066769]. However, these local improvements do **not translate to global spinopelvic correction in single-level fusion** [pubmed_41662143]. 

For **multi-level adult spinal deformity**, ACR (anterolateral approach with multiple discectomies) offers **incremental angular correction while preserving posteriors** [pubmed_41497819], with interbody fusion reducing proximal junctional complications [pubmed_29087808]. 

**Rod fracture at 4.4%** is an important complication risk, particularly with longer constructs [pubmed_31229745]. **ALIF demonstrates superior pseudoarthrosis resistance** over TLIF at L5-S1 (hypothesis-supported but specific rates pending) [pubmed_37146876], with **fusion rates enhanced to 97.8% using rhBMP-2** vs 88.2% without [pubmed_25955186].
