# Comparative Outcomes: Laminoplasty vs Laminectomy with Fusion for Multilevel Cervical Myelopathy

## Summary of Evidence Quality
All primary sources are Level 1a evidence (meta-analyses), with one systematic review providing Level 2a evidence for OPLL-specific comparisons [pubmed_27781191].

---

## CLINICAL OUTCOMES

### Functional Scores (JOA - Japanese Orthopedic Association)
Both procedures achieved **equivalent improvements** in neurological function:
- No significant difference in JOA scores between laminoplasty (LP) and laminectomy-fusion (LF) [pubmed_27281067], [pubmed_30921202], [pubmed_30813208]

### Pain Reduction (VAS - Visual Analogue Scale)
- **Equivalent between groups** [pubmed_27281067], [pubmed_30921202], [pubmed_30813208]
- No significant differences reported across meta-analyses

### Other Functional Measures
- **Cervical Curvature Index (CCI)**: Equivalent between LP and LF [pubmed_27281067], [pubmed_30921202]
- **Nurick Grade**: Equivalent outcomes [pubmed_30921202]
- **Reoperation Rate**: No significant difference [pubmed_30921202]

---

## CRITICAL SAFETY OUTCOMES

### C5 Palsy Incidence - MAJOR DIFFERENCE

| Comparison | Finding | Citation |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| **LP vs LF (General CSM)** | LP: Lower incidence (OR 0.26, p<0.001) | [pubmed_27281067] |
| **LP vs LF (General CSM)** | LP superior for C5 palsy prevention | [pubmed_30813208], [pubmed_38764362] |
| **CLF vs CLP (OPLL-specific)** | CLF had higher C5 palsy incidence (all 4 studies reviewed) | [pubmed_33819935] |
| **Posterior approach risk** | Posterior approaches carry elevated C5P rates | [pubmed_41017260] |

**Key finding**: C5 palsy is a major complication favoring laminoplasty, with laminectomy-fusion showing consistently higher rates across both general CSM and OPLL populations.

### Overall Complication Rates

| Study | Finding | Specific Data |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| [pubmed_30921202] | LP: Significantly lower overall complications | Not specified numerically in abstract |
| [pubmed_30813208] | LP: Lower overall complication rates | Stated as superior |
| [pubmed_38764362] | LP: Reduced complication rates, lower C5 palsy | Specific rates not provided in abstract |

### OPLL-Specific Complications
[pubmed_27781191] reported for OPLL (systematic review):
- **Higher C5 palsy with LF** (consistent finding)
- **Higher postoperative hematoma with LP**
- **Higher progressive kyphosis with LP**
- **Higher incomplete decompression with LP**
- **No procedure demonstrated superior effectiveness overall for OPLL**

---

## OPERATIVE AND RADIOGRAPHIC OUTCOMES

### Operative Time
- **LP advantage**: 19.57 minutes shorter than LF (p=0.002) [pubmed_27281067]
- Supports LP as less invasive option

### Blood Loss
- **LP advantage**: Reduced blood loss compared to LF [pubmed_27281067], [pubmed_38764362]
- Specific quantitative values not provided in abstracts

### Cervical Lordosis Preservation

**Short-term findings (≤2 years)**:
- **Equivalent lordosis loss** between LP and LF [pubmed_27281067], [pubmed_25815808]
- Both techniques show similar immediate radiological effects [pubmed_25815808]

**Long-term findings (≥2 years)**:
- **Trend toward better lordosis preservation with LF** [pubmed_25815808]
- LF may maintain sagittal balance superior to LP over extended periods
- This represents a potential long-term advantage for fusion

### Cervical Range of Motion
- **LP superior**: Greater postoperative cervical ROM preservation compared to LF [pubmed_30813208]
- Specific ROM measurements (flexion/extension degrees) not quantified in abstracts, though [pubmed_33819935] referenced one OPLL study showing significantly greater ROM in flexion, extension, and side bending for CLP

### Axial Pain Incidence
- **Equivalent between groups** [pubmed_27281067]

---

## OPLL-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

### Evidence Quality
Systematic review of OPLL literature [pubmed_27781191], [pubmed_33819935]:
- **No procedure demonstrated superiority** for overall effectiveness
- **Clinical outcomes equivalent** between LP and LF for OPLL

### OPLL Complication Pattern
[pubmed_27781191]:
- **LF complications**: Higher C5 palsy
- **LP complications**: Higher postoperative hematoma, progressive kyphosis, incomplete decompression

### Clinical Decision-Making for OPLL
[pubmed_27781191] noted: "There was no one procedure that was found to be superior regarding complications"

Literature suggests potential OPLL advantage for LF in some studies [pubmed_23963000], but this "must be weighed against pseudarthrosis risk" and is not consistently demonstrated.

---

## POSTERIOR APPROACH C5 PALSY RISK

[pubmed_41017260] meta-analysis identified:
- **Posterior surgical approach as a significant risk factor for C5P**
- Important given both LP and LF are posterior approaches
- This contextualizes why both procedures carry C5P risk, with LF showing elevated rates

## CRITICAL EVIDENCE GAPS

1. **Limited long-term data**: Most studies focus on short-term outcomes; long-term (>2 years) lordosis preservation favors LF but evidence is limited [pubmed_25815808]
2. **OPLL evidence quality**: Systematic review [pubmed_27781191] notes "limited evidence" for OPLL; no clear superiority established
3. **Pseudarthrosis rates not quantified** in provided abstracts for LF
4. **Specific ROM values in degrees** not provided for most studies
5. **Specific hematoma and kyphosis rates** for OPLL not numerically quantified

---

## CLINICAL RECOMMENDATIONS FROM META-ANALYSES

**Laminoplasty advantages** [pubmed_38764362]:
- Shorter operative time
- Reduced blood loss
- Lower C5 palsy incidence
- Reduced overall complications
- Better ROM preservation
- Favored as "important surgical option with favorable safety and efficacy profile"

**Both procedures equivalent for**:
- Neurological improvement (JOA scores)
- Pain reduction (VAS)
- Spinal cord decompression adequacy
- Short-term lordosis preservation

**Individualization required** [pubmed_23963000]:
- Consider patient age, comorbidities
- Surgeon familiarity and technical expertise
- Patient preferences
- For OPLL: weigh hematoma/kyphosis/incomplete decompression (LP) against C5 palsy (LF)
- Long-term sagittal balance considerations may favor LF with fusion

---

## CONCLUSION

**For multilevel cervical myelopathy generally**, laminoplasty demonstrates **superior safety** (lower C5 palsy: OR 0.26, p<0.001; lower complications; shorter operative time: 19.57 min, p=0.002) with **equivalent neurological outcomes** and **superior ROM preservation**.

**For OPLL specifically**, evidence shows **equivalent effectiveness** with different complication patterns—no single procedure is clearly superior, requiring individualized decision-making balancing C5 palsy risk (favoring LP) against long-term lordosis preservation (favoring LF) and OPLL-specific complications (hematoma, kyphosis, incomplete decompression with LP; C5 palsy with LF).
