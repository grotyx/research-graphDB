# CV-001: What is the evidence comparing cervical disc replacement (CDR) versus anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) for 1-2 level cervical disc disease in terms of adjacent segment degeneration, reoperation rates, and range of motion preservation?

# Evidence Synthesis: CDR vs ACDF for 1-2 Level Cervical Disc Disease

## Summary
Unfortunately, **the provided papers do not contain direct comparative evidence between CDR and ACDF** for adjacent segment degeneration, reoperation rates, or range of motion preservation in 1-2 level cervical disc disease.

## Available Evidence

### Cervical Disc Replacement (CDR)
The only CDR-related paper addresses device safety concerns rather than comparative outcomes:

**Device Safety Concerns** [pubmed_41396206]
- Evidence level 3 (Analysis of FDA MAUDE reports and device retrieval findings)
- Year: 2025
- Focuses on the Simplify cervical total disc replacement with emerging concerns about wear mechanisms and device failures
- Does not compare CDR to ACDF or address adjacent segment disease

### Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF)
Several papers examine ACDF outcomes but lack CDR comparisons:

**ACDF versus Posterior Foraminotomy** [pubmed_41077080]
- Evidence level 2b (Retrospective cohort study)
- Year: 2025
- Compares anterior vs posterior approaches for cervical radiculopathy with vertically narrow foramina
- **Does not compare to CDR**

**Revision ACDF Techniques** [pubmed_38143630]
- Evidence level 2b (Comparative analysis)
- Year: 2024
- Compares zero-profile stand-alone implants versus traditional PEEK cage with plate instrumentation
- **Does not address CDR or adjacent segment degeneration outcomes**

**Sagittal Alignment Changes** [pubmed_41372658]
- Evidence level 4 (Observational study)
- Year: 2025
- Examines degenerative changes and compensatory mechanisms after multilevel ACDF
- **No comparison to CDR**

## Critical Evidence Gaps

1. **No direct CDR vs ACDF comparative studies** in provided literature
2. **Adjacent segment degeneration**: Not specifically addressed in any paper
3. **Reoperation rates**: Not compared between CDR and ACDF
4. **Range of motion preservation**: Not quantified in comparative context

## Recommendation

This clinical question **cannot be answered using only the provided papers**. A literature search for Level 1-2 evidence (RCTs or large prospective comparative cohort studies) comparing CDR and ACDF would be necessary to address adjacent segment disease, reoperation rates, and motion preservation outcomes.

# Evidence Summary: CDR vs ACDF for 1-2 Level Cervical Disc Disease

## Study Overview

This analysis includes **9 meta-analyses of randomized controlled trials** (all Level 1a evidence), comprising multiple RCTs comparing cervical disc replacement/arthroplasty (CDA/TDR/CDR) with ACDF.

---

## PRIMARY OUTCOME: Adjacent Segment Degeneration (ASD)

### Adjacent Segment Degeneration Rates

| Metric | CDA/CDR | ACDF | Difference/Effect |
|--------|---------|------|-------------------|
| **ASD incidence** | 14.4% | 19.7% | 5.3 percentage points lower in CDA (p<0.001) [pubmed_35085804] |
| **ASD risk (OR)** | Reference | 0.33 (95% CI: 0.21-0.50) | CDA significantly lower risk (p<0.0001, I²=0.0%) [pubmed_30992666] |

**Key Mechanism**: CDA preserves intervertebral height and spinal functional units, reducing adjacent segment loading [pubmed_40797275].

---

### Adjacent Segment REOPERATION Rates

#### One-Level Procedures:

**2-Year Follow-up:**
- CDA: 2.3%
- ACDF: 3.6%
- **Difference**: Not statistically significant (p not reported) [pubmed_32309660]

**5-Year Follow-up:**
- CDA: 4.3%
- ACDF: 10.8%
- **Difference**: Significant divergence (p<0.001) [pubmed_32309660]

**7-Year Follow-up:**
- Difference persisted (p values not specified in excerpt) [pubmed_32309660]

**Summary**: 
- All CDA constraint levels significantly reduced adjacent segment reoperation need compared to ACDF (p<0.001 for all) [pubmed_38843960]
- CDA demonstrated **substantially lower rates** of reoperation need, with **NNT approximately 13-17** based on absolute risk reduction from 10.8% to 4.3% over 5 years [pubmed_32309660]

---

## SECONDARY OUTCOME: Index-Level Reoperation

**CDA advantage identified**: CDR showed the **lowest rate of secondary surgical procedures** (p=0.0178) compared to ACDF and posterior approaches [pubmed_28700452]

**Semiconstrained CDA specific finding**: Showed lowest index-level reoperation rate compared to both ACDF and Constrained CDA (p<0.001) [pubmed_38843960]

---

## TERTIARY OUTCOME: Range of Motion Preservation

### C2-C7 Cervical Range of Motion

| Group | ROM (degrees) | 95% CI | Follow-up |
|-------|--------------|---------|-----------|
| **TDR** | 40.16° | 33.99-46.34 | Minimum 1-year [pubmed_31431880] |
| **ACDF** | 35.10° | 32.51-37.69 | Minimum 1-year [pubmed_31431880] |
| **Difference** | **5.06°** | — | **p=0.001 (significant)** [pubmed_31431880] |

### Operated Segment ROM:

- **CDA preserved significantly more ROM** than ACDF (WMD 10.37 degrees, p<0.01) [pubmed_40797275]
- **Motion preservation hierarchy** by constraint level: Unconstrained CDA > Semiconstrained CDA > Constrained CDA > ACDF [pubmed_38843960]

---

## NEUROLOGICAL OUTCOMES

### Neurological Success Rates

- **CDA**: Superior efficacy vs ACDF
- **Odds Ratio**: 1.60 (95% CI: 1.30-1.98, p<0.01) [pubmed_40797275]
- **Mechanism**: Restoration of normal intervertebral height and preservation of spinal functional units [pubmed_40797275]

---

## PAIN OUTCOMES

### VAS Scores at Final Follow-up

| Outcome | TDR | ACDF | P-value |
|---------|-----|------|---------|
| **VAS neck** | 1.36 (95% CI: 0.83-1.88) | 2.39 (95% CI: 1.52-3.26) | 0.152 (NS) [pubmed_31431880] |
| **VAS upper limb** | 1.56 (95% CI: 0.86-2.25) | 1.99 (95% CI: 1.54-2.43) | 0.200 (NS) [pubmed_31431880] |

**Interpretation**: Both groups improved comparably; no significant difference favoring either approach [pubmed_31431880]

---

## FUNCTIONAL OUTCOMES

**TDR demonstrated advantages** in:
- Neck Disability Index (NDI) functional outcomes
- SF-36 physical component scores
- Dysphagia symptom relief
- Patient satisfaction [pubmed_30062947]

---

## OPERATIVE TIME TRADE-OFF

- CDA requires **longer operative time** compared to ACDF
- **Clinical consensus**: This trade-off is acceptable given long-term clinical benefits of reduced ASD and reoperation rates [pubmed_40882232]

---

## EVIDENCE QUALITY & PATIENT SELECTION

### Consensus Findings:
1. **84.3% of surgeons** currently perform ACDF as gold standard, despite ACDF complications [pubmed_31863642]
2. **ACDF baseline complication profile**: Pseudoarthrosis, instrument failure, and ASD requiring intervention in **2.9%** of cases [pubmed_31863642]
3. **Future optimization needed**: Focus on distinguishing patient populations best suited for each approach [pubmed_40882232]

---

## SUMMARY TABLE: Clinical Superiority Profile

| Outcome Domain | CDR/CDA Winner | Effect Size | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adjacent Segment Degeneration** | CDR/CDA | 5.3% lower incidence; OR 0.33 | Very strong (p<0.001) [pubmed_35085804, pubmed_30992666] |
| **5-Year Adjacent Reoperation** | CDR/CDA | 6.5% lower (4.3% vs 10.8%) | Strong (p<0.001) [pubmed_32309660] |
| **Index-Level Reoperation** | CDR/CDA | Significantly lower | Strong (p<0.001) [pubmed_38843960] |
| **Range of Motion** | CDR/CDA | 5.06-10.37° greater | Strong (p≤0.01) [pubmed_31431880, pubmed_40797275] |
| **Neurological Success** | CDR/CDA | OR 1.60 | Strong (p<0.01) [pubmed_40797275] |
| **Neck/Arm Pain** | Comparable | No difference | NS (p=0.152-0.200) [pubmed_31431880] |
| **Operative Time** | ACDF | Shorter | Trade-off acceptable [pubmed_40882232] |

---

## GAPS IN EVIDENCE

1. **Two-level procedures**: Limited specific data provided; analyses primarily focus on 1-level disease
2. **Cost-effectiveness**: Not addressed in provided papers
3. **Device-specific outcomes**: Limited comparison across different CDA device types beyond constraint level analysis
4. **Long-term (>7 years)**: Sparse data on outcomes beyond 7-year follow-up
