# Risk Factors for Cage Subsidence After Lumbar Interbody Fusion: Evidence Synthesis

## Risk Factors for Cage Subsidence

### Patient-Related Risk Factors

**Sarcopenia, Advanced Age, and Low Bone Mineral Density**
In a retrospective cohort study (n=98 non-osteoporotic patients) examining stand-alone oblique lumbar interbody fusion (SA-OLIF), cage subsidence (>2 mm height loss) occurred in 32.7% of cases [pubmed_41782914]. Sarcopenia, advanced age, and lower bone mineral density were identified as potential risk factors via multivariate logistic regression analysis, though specific odds ratios were not provided in the excerpt [pubmed_41782914].

**Endplate Bone Quality (EBQ)**
Endplate bone quality obtained from clinical MRI scans has been established as a reliable determinant of regional bone mineral density and is associated with cage subsidence risk following oblique lumbar interbody fusion (OLIF), though the specific quantitative relationship was not fully detailed in the provided excerpts [pubmed_38754736].

**Smoking**
Smoking significantly increases pseudoarthrosis risk through multiple mechanisms: nicotine-induced vasoconstriction reduces microvascular blood flow and nutrient availability, and smokers demonstrate lower vitamin D levels with increased comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension) [pubmed_41066602]. Implant material interaction occurs—smokers had worse fusion results with PEEK interbody cages specifically [pubmed_41066602].

**Obesity**
Obesity is confirmed as a significant risk factor for nonunion and worse patient-reported clinical outcomes following lumbar spinal fusion surgery [pubmed_40433998], though specific subsidence rates were not quantified in the excerpt provided.

## Cage Material Effects on Subsidence

### 3D-Printed Titanium (3D-pTi) vs. PEEK Cages

**Subsidence Rate Comparison**
In a systematic review comparing 3D-printed titanium versus polyetheretherketone interbody devices, the overall subsidence rate (Marchi grades I–III: 0-24% collapse) was **significantly less for 3D-pTi devices compared to PEEK devices (p = 0.003)** [pubmed_37401063].

When stratifying by high-grade subsidence (grade II or III), **3D-pTi cages demonstrated significantly lower subsidence rates** compared to PEEK [pubmed_37401063].

**Lateral Interbody Fusion Analysis**
In a cohort study of lateral lumbar interbody fusion (LLIF), 3D-printed porous titanium cages showed a trend toward reduced subsidence compared to PEEK cages:
- **Odds Ratio: 0.401 (95% CI 0.202-1.069; p=0.071)** [pubmed_41569690]
- This represents a moderate association with subsidence reduction, though not statistically significant
- **Clinical note: Zero reoperation cases** were documented in the 3D-printed porous titanium cage subgroup across the entire cohort, suggesting excellent long-term clinical performance [pubmed_41569690]

**ACDF and TLIF Case Series**
In early radiographic outcomes (Evidence 2b), a TLIF patient treated with 3D-printed titanium (3DPT) cages demonstrated only **2.5 mm height decrease at 6-month follow-up** with mild upper endplate collapse but maintained overall height preservation [pubmed_37771127].

## Cage Design and Positioning Effects

### Biomechanical Implications of Cage Shape

**Spinal Stiffness After Facetectomy**
Double banana-shaped cages in TLIF require facetectomy for transforaminal access, which significantly reduces functional spinal stiffness compared to intact spine:
- **Extension: 48% reduction** in stiffness
- **Lateral bending: 25% reduction**
- **Rotation: 39% reduction** [pubmed_36944178]

This biomechanical destabilization emphasizes the importance of constructional vertebral stability, making rigid and stable interbody support essential for preventing cage subsidence and achieving solid bony fusion [pubmed_36944178].

---

## Cage Design: Screw-Integral Fixation

**ALIF with Integral Fixation Cage**
An anterior lumbar interbody fusion study using titanium/polyetheretherketone (Ti/PEEK) 3-screw integral fixation cages (n=patients with 1-2 level procedures) demonstrated:
- **No cases of lucency or halo** adjacent to titanium endplates at 6-month follow-up [pubmed_35386238]
- Evidence of bioactive conversion and bone/porous titanium incorporation [pubmed_35386238]
- Effective treatment for lumbar isthmic spondylolisthesis, spondylotic radiculopathy, and discogenic low back pain [pubmed_35386238]

## Fixation Method Effects on Subsidence

### Transfacet Screws vs. Bilateral Pedicle Screws in OLIF

**Subsidence Rates by Fixation Type**
In oblique lateral interbody fusion (OLIF) with supplemental fixation, subsidence differed significantly between methods:
- **Transfacet Screws (TFS): 16% subsidence** from immediate postoperative state [pubmed_36522754]
- **Bilateral Pedicle Screws (BPS): 10% subsidence** from immediate postoperative state [pubmed_36522754]

**Net Disc Height Preservation Despite Subsidence**
Despite subsidence, both groups maintained improvement over preoperative baseline:
- **TFS: +2.2 mm net improvement (+26% height increase)** [pubmed_36522754]
- **BPS: +3.7 mm net improvement (+42% height increase)** [pubmed_36522754]

**Fusion Rate Success**
High fusion rates achieved in both groups indicate successful bony consolidation despite subsidence:
- **TFS: 91% fusion rate** [pubmed_36522754]
- **BPS: 100% fusion rate** [pubmed_36522754]

## Critical Evidence Gaps

1. **Specific multivariate analyses:** While sarcopenia, age, and BMD were examined as SA-OLIF risk factors, specific odds ratios, beta coefficients, and p-values were not provided [pubmed_41782914].

2. **Endplate preparation standardization:** No quantitative data on the effect of different endplate preparation techniques on subsidence rates.

3. **Bone graft type effects:** ALIF study used allograft with BMP-2 (4.2 mg/level), but comparative data on subsidence with different bone graft materials were not provided [pubmed_35386238].

4. **Long-term subsidence progression:** Most studies report early follow-up (6-24 months); longer-term subsidence patterns >2 years were not comprehensively addressed.

5. **Smoking and material interaction:** While smoking worsens outcomes with PEEK, specific subsidence rates for smokers are not quantified.

6. **Obesity subsidence quantification:** Obesity confirmed as risk factor but specific subsidence rates/odds ratios not provided [pubmed_40433998].
