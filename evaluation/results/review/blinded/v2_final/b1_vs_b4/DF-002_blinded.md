# Comparative Outcomes: PSO vs VCR for Fixed Sagittal Imbalance

## Study Overview

| Study | Design | Sample Size | Evidence Level |
|-------|--------|------------|-----------------|
| [pubmed_41497819] | Meta-analysis | Multiple RCTs/studies | 1a |
| [pubmed_38697559] | Retrospective cohort | PSO and VCR groups | 2b |
| [pubmed_34653992] | Comparative cohort | 34 PSO, 35 VCR patients | 2b |
| [pubmed_40513242] | Multicenter cohort | VCR for Pott's deformity | 2b |
| [pubmed_30267947] | Comparative cohort | PVCR ± ACS | 2b |

---

## 1. CORRECTION MAGNITUDE

### Sagittal/Lordosis Correction
- **VCR superior correction:** VCR achieved **17.3° lordosis gain** vs PSO **13.9° lordosis gain**, representing a **3.4° difference favoring VCR** [pubmed_38697559]

- **Rotational Kyphotic Angle (RKA) Corrections:**
  - PSO: Mean correction **31°** [pubmed_34653992]
  - VCR: Mean correction **49°** [pubmed_34653992]
  - **Difference: VCR achieved 18° greater RKA correction** (p-value not reported)

- **Long-term kyphosis correction (PVCR in Pott's disease):**
  - Immediate postoperative: **75.3° reduction** (from 107.3° to 32.0°) [pubmed_40513242]
  - At mean 105.7-month follow-up: **5.1° loss of correction** (final 37.1°), indicating **93.2% correction maintenance** [pubmed_40513242]

### Sagittal Balance Maintenance
- **VCR demonstrated superior long-term balance maintenance:** Sagittal balance improvement was maintained in VCR group (p=0.008) but **NOT maintained in PSO group (p=0.065)**, suggesting VCR provides more durable corrections [pubmed_38697559]

---

## 2. REOPERATION RATES

### Meta-Analysis Data
**PSO vs ACR comparison (PSO relevant comparator):**
- ACR (anterior approach) demonstrated **markedly lower reoperations** compared to PSO [pubmed_41497819]
- Specific reoperation rates not quantified in the meta-analysis abstract

### Comparative Study Findings
- The meta-analysis indicates that **PSO has higher reoperation requirements** than less invasive anterior approaches, supporting an inference that VCR (more extensive than PSO) may similarly carry reoperation risk, though direct PSO vs VCR reoperation data not explicitly reported across studies [pubmed_41497819]

---

## 3. COMPLICATION RATES

### Neurological Complications
- **Modified PVCR (posterior vertebral column resection with ligament preservation):** **16.1% neurological complication rate** [pubmed_40381021]
- **Traditional PVCR historical rates:** 35-45% neurological complications [pubmed_40381021]
- **Modified technique advantage:** 16.1% is **significantly lower than traditional PVCR (35-45% range)**, representing **19-29 percentage point reduction** [pubmed_40381021]

- **Multilevel VCR:** Showed **increased rate of postoperative neurologic deficits** vs single-level VCR (specific rates not quantified due to low sample size) [pubmed_30475336]

### Intraoperative Blood Loss
- **PSO vs ACR (anterior approach):** ACR demonstrated **markedly lower intraoperative blood loss** than PSO [pubmed_41497819]
- Specific blood loss volumes not provided in abstracts

### Osteotomy Distance Requirements
- **PVCR without anterior column support:** Required **smaller osteotomy distance (4.0 cm)** vs with anterior column support **(5.3 cm)**, p<0.001 [pubmed_30267947]
- **Interpretation:** Anterior fixation increases surgical extensiveness by 1.3 cm

## 4. BASELINE PATIENT CHARACTERISTICS AFFECTING OUTCOMES

| Factor | PSO | VCR | Clinical Significance |
|--------|-----|-----|----------------------|
| Mean age | 57 years | 29 years | Age disparity complicates direct comparison; older PSO patients may have lower physiologic tolerance for correction [pubmed_34653992] |
| Procedure type | 57% single-level, 43% multilevel | Variable | Multilevel procedures increase operative complexity [pubmed_34653992] |

---

## 5. META-ANALYSIS SUMMARY

**PSO vs ACR (proximal comparison for ASD):**
- Both achieve **meaningful sagittal correction** [pubmed_41497819]
- **ACR superiority:** Provides equivalent or superior lordosis restoration with:
  - Markedly lower intraoperative blood loss
  - Fewer reoperations
  - Supports role as less invasive alternative in selected patients [pubmed_41497819]

**Implication for PSO vs VCR:** VCR (more extensive three-column resection) likely carries greater operative burden than PSO but provides superior magnitude of correction and durability.

---

## 6. EVIDENCE GAPS

1. **Direct PSO vs VCR head-to-head complication comparison:** No RCT comparing PSO and VCR directly reported
2. **Specific reoperation rates:** PSO and VCR reoperation percentages not quantified in comparison
3. **Long-term follow-up (>5 years):** Limited long-term data for PSO outcomes; PVCR data extends to mean 105.7 months
4. **Age-stratified outcomes:** Baseline age differences (57 vs 29 years) between PSO and VCR cohorts limit direct comparison
5. **Quality of life outcomes:** While [pubmed_32382878] reviewed HRQOL impact, specific comparative data not extracted in available abstract

---

## CLINICAL SUMMARY

**VCR vs PSO for fixed sagittal imbalance:**
- **Correction:** VCR superior (18° greater RKA correction, 3.4° greater lordosis)
- **Durability:** VCR maintains sagittal balance long-term (p=0.008); PSO does not (p=0.065)
- **Complications:** Modified PVCR achieves 16.1% neurological rate vs 35-45% traditional PVCR; direct PSO comparison lacking
- **Invasiveness:** PSO less extensive but less durable; VCR more extensive but more effective for rigid deformities
- **Recommendation context:** VCR preferred for severe rigid deformities requiring durable correction; PSO appropriate for less severe cases or when patient factors limit tolerance for extensive surgery [pubmed_32382878]
