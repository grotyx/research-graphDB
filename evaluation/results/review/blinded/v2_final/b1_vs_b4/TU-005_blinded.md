# Surgical Outcomes and Prognostic Factors for Intramedullary Spinal Cord Tumors: Ependymoma vs Astrocytoma

## I. GROSS TOTAL RESECTION (GTR) RATES BY HISTOLOGY

### Overall GTR Achievement
- **Overall cohort**: 73.1-76.67% GTR rate [pubmed_40647411, pubmed_38608817]
- **Sample sizes**: 253 patients [pubmed_40647411], 60 patients [pubmed_38608817]

### GTR Rates by Tumor Type

| Tumor Type | GTR Rate | Study | Sample |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hemangioblastoma** | 96.4% | [pubmed_40647411] | 253 patients |
| **Ependymal tumors** | 82.5% | [pubmed_40647411] | 253 patients |
| **Astrocytic tumors** | 55.6% | [pubmed_40647411] | 253 patients |
| **P-value (between types)** | p < 0.001 | [pubmed_40647411] | — |

**Key finding**: GTR achievability differed significantly by histology, with astrocytic tumors having substantially lower GTR rates (55.6%) compared to ependymal tumors (82.5%), a difference of 26.9 percentage points [pubmed_40647411].

## III. SURVIVAL OUTCOMES: GTR vs SUBTOTAL/PARTIAL RESECTION

### Ependymoma Subgroup (Pediatric)
- **Study design**: SEER database analysis, n = 156 patients [pubmed_41653291]
- **5-year survival**: 85.2% (95% CI: 79.4-89.7%) [pubmed_41653291]

**GTR versus STR/No Resection:**
- **Log-rank p-value**: p = 0.008 (GTR significantly associated with improved survival) [pubmed_41653291]
- **Multivariable hazard ratio for GTR**: HR 0.050 (95% CI: 0.008-0.312, p = 0.015) [pubmed_41653291]
- **Mortality risk reduction**: GTR represented a 95% reduction in mortality risk compared to STR/no resection [pubmed_41653291]

**Interpretation**: GTR was protective in ependymoma, with a hazard ratio of 0.050 indicating approximately 95% lower mortality risk with GTR versus non-GTR approaches.

### Overall Pediatric IMSCT Cohort (Age <21 years)
- **Surgical resection rates**: 87.2% underwent surgical management [pubmed_39042966]
- **Highest resection rates**: Oldest age group (16-21 years) [pubmed_39042966]
- **Overall survival comparison (resection vs non-operative management)**: p = 0.315 (NOT statistically significant) [pubmed_39042966]

**Critical finding**: Despite high surgical utilization rates (87.2%), overall survival did not significantly differ between patients who underwent resection versus those managed nonoperatively, suggesting other prognostic factors beyond surgical extent influence outcomes [pubmed_39042966].

---

## IV. ASTROCYTOMA-SPECIFIC OUTCOMES

### Gross Total Resection Achievement
- **GTR rate in astrocytic tumors**: 55.6% (substantially lower than ependymomas at 82.5%) [pubmed_40647411]
- **En bloc resection in astrocytic tumors**: 0% [pubmed_40647411]

### Case Report Evidence
- **Single case of thoracic intramedullary astrocytoma with syringomyelia**: Microsurgical management achieved gross total resection with functional stabilization and improved quality of life in elderly patient [pubmed_41837020]
- **Key technique**: Careful microsurgical dissection allowed GTR without unacceptable morbidity [pubmed_41837020]

**Clinical note**: Astrocytomas are infiltrative tumors with lower GTR feasibility; when GTR can be safely achieved through meticulous microsurgical technique, functional improvement is possible [pubmed_40647411, pubmed_41837020].

## V. PEDIATRIC-SPECIFIC FACTORS

### Tumor Malignancy as Prognostic Factor
- **Finding**: Tumor malignancy emerged as an important factor influencing postoperative outcomes [pubmed_39672822]
- **Specific outcome**: Malignant tumors showed worse functional recovery, particularly regarding postoperative gait stability [pubmed_39672822]

### Long-term Functional Outcomes Considerations
- **Study population**: Pediatric patients <20 years with intradural spinal tumors [pubmed_39672822]
- **Critical issue**: Need to achieve complete tumor resection while preserving neurological function is particularly critical in young patients with long life expectancies [pubmed_39672822]

## VI. COMPLICATION AND MORBIDITY DATA

**Note**: The provided papers do not contain specific quantitative data on postoperative neurological complications, infection rates, or other morbidity measures comparing GTR versus STR. One study mentioned that in some astrocytic tumor cases, "complete tumor removal was not feasible or would result in unacceptable neurologic morbidity" [pubmed_38608817], but specific complication rates are not provided.

### Spinal Deformity Risk
- **Identified risk factors**: Preoperative deformity, thoracic location, and presence of syrinx [pubmed_41546829]
- **Population**: Children with low-grade intramedullary spinal cord tumors [pubmed_41546829]

---

## VII. SUMMARY OF KEY PROGNOSTIC FACTORS

**Factors favoring better outcomes:**
1. **Tumor histology**: Ependymoma > Astrocytoma (GTR rates: 82.5% vs 55.6%, p < 0.001) [pubmed_40647411]
2. **Gross total resection vs subtotal**: In ependymomas, GTR associated with 95% mortality risk reduction (HR 0.050, 95% CI: 0.008-0.312, p = 0.015) [pubmed_41653291]
3. **Tumor grade**: Low-grade tumors more amenable to GTR and better functional outcomes [pubmed_40647411, pubmed_39672822]
4. **Age**: Older pediatric patients (16-21 years) had higher resection rates [pubmed_39042966]

**Factors associated with worse outcomes:**
1. **Malignant tumor histology**: Associated with worse functional recovery and gait stability [pubmed_39672822]
2. **Astrocytic tumors**: 55.6% GTR rate, 0% en bloc resection rate [pubmed_40647411]
3. **Preoperative deformity/thoracic location**: Risk factors for postoperative spinal deformity complications [pubmed_41546829]
