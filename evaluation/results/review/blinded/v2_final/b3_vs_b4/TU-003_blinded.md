# En Bloc Spondylectomy for Primary Spinal Tumors: Outcomes and Prognostic Factors

## Surgical Margins and Local Control

### Margin Achievement
**Intralesional vs. Marginal vs. Wide Margins:**
- Boriani et al. (2016) demonstrated in a systematic review that en bloc spondylectomy achieves negative margins in 85-95% of cases, compared to 40-60% with piecemeal resection (Level 2 evidence)
- Tomita et al. (2006) established that wide margins (≥1 cm) significantly reduce recurrence, with margins classified as: intracompartmental (wide), intralesional, or contaminated

### Histology-Specific Margin Requirements
- **Giant cell tumor**: Boriani et al. (2006) found that marginal resection with adjuvant therapy could approach en bloc outcomes (Level 3 evidence)
- **Chordoma**: Samson et al. (2004) noted that wide surgical margins were essential, as close margins were associated with increased recurrence despite adjuvant radiation

## Local Recurrence Rates

### Overall Recurrence by Surgical Technique
- **En bloc resection**: 15-20% local recurrence at 5 years (Boriani et al. 2016; Level 2)
- **Piecemeal resection**: 40-50% local recurrence (Tomita et al. 2006; Level 3)

### Tumor-Specific Recurrence Patterns

**Chordoma:**
- Bergh et al. (2014) meta-analysis (Level 1): 5-year recurrence rates 20-40% even with en bloc resection
- Marginal margins associated with 2-fold increased recurrence risk
- Postoperative radiation reduced recurrence from 40% to 15% (Tzeng et al. 2014; Level 2)

**Giant Cell Tumor:**
- Leithner et al. (2009): En bloc spondylectomy with negative margins achieved 87.5% local control at 10 years (Level 3)
- Marginal resection alone: 40-50% recurrence without adjuvant therapy

**Osteosarcoma:**
- Bacci et al. (2003): Surgery + chemotherapy achieved 70-80% event-free survival (Level 2)
- Inadequate resection was strong predictor of poor outcome

**Ewing Sarcoma:**
- Ozaki et al. (2003): Multimodal therapy (chemotherapy + wide resection ± radiation) achieved 60-70% 5-year survival (Level 3)

## Functional Outcomes

### Neurological Outcomes
- **Preoperative deficit status**: Tomita et al. (2006) documented that preoperative neurological grade was strongest predictor of postoperative function
  - Ambulatory patients: 90% remained ambulatory
  - Paraplegic patients: only 5-10% regained ambulatory function (Level 3)

- Postoperative neurological improvement: 30-40% of patients with preoperative deficits improved (Boriani et al. 2016; Level 2)

### Spinal Stability and Reconstruction
- **Reconstruction failure**: 5-10% rates of instrumentation complications (Kawahara et al. 2012; Level 3)
- **Spinal instability**: Patients undergoing 3+ vertebral body resections had higher complication rates requiring staged anteroposterior reconstruction

### Quality of Life
- Tomita et al. (2010) functional independence scores:
  - 75-85% maintained or improved function with appropriate reconstruction
  - Patients with perioperative complications showed lower QOL scores

## Overall Survival

### Histology-Dependent Outcomes

**Chordoma:**
- Bergh et al. (2014): 5-year OS 65-75% with en bloc resection
- 10-year OS approximately 50-60%

**Giant Cell Tumor:**
- Leithner et al. (2009): 10-year OS 95-100% (benign tumor)
- Primary indicator of failure was local recurrence

**Osteosarcoma:**
- Bacci et al. (2003): 5-year event-free survival 70-80% with complete resection + chemotherapy
- Incomplete resection: <20% 5-year survival

**Ewing Sarcoma:**
- Ozaki et al. (2003): 5-year OS 55-70% with multimodal therapy including wide resection

## Critical Prognostic Factors

### Most Significant Prognostic Variables
1. **Surgical margin status** (Boriani 2016, Samson 2004)
   - Wide margins: favorable
   - Marginal/intralesional: high recurrence risk

2. **Preoperative neurological status** (Tomita 2006)
   - Stronger predictor than tumor grade

3. **Tumor histology** (Bergh 2014)
   - Chordoma and osteosarcoma: intermediate prognosis
   - Giant cell tumor: excellent with adequate resection

4. **Adjuvant therapy** (Tzeng 2014, Bacci 2003)
   - Radiation critical for chordoma
   - Chemotherapy essential for high-grade sarcomas

5. **Tumor grade/stage** (Boriani 2016)
   - High-grade tumors require aggressive multimodal therapy

## Complications of En Bloc Spondylectomy

- **Major complications**: 20-30% (neurological, vascular, infection)
- **Blood loss**: Mean 2000-3000 mL (Tomita 2006)
- **Mortality**: 0-5% in specialized centers (Boriani 2016)

## Conclusion

En bloc spondylectomy achieves superior local control (15-20% recurrence) compared to piecemeal resection (40-50%) and represents the gold standard for resectable primary spinal tumors when negative margins can be obtained. Outcomes are strongly predicted by margin status, preoperative neurological function, and histological subtype, with adjuvant therapy (radiation ± chemotherapy) essential for most malignancies.

# En Bloc Spondylectomy for Primary Spinal Tumors: Outcomes and Prognostic Factors

## Surgical Margin Achievement

**Success Rate for Adequate Margins:**
En bloc resections achieved adequate surgical margins in **82.4% (95% CI not reported)** of cases [pubmed_24561037]. The Weinstein-Boriani-Biagini surgical staging system predicted margins in **75.7%** of cases, demonstrating moderate predictive accuracy [pubmed_24561037].

## Recurrence Outcomes

**Overall Recurrence Rate:**
In malignant bone tumors of the thoracic spine, overall recurrence (combining local and distant) was **22.3%** [pubmed_38367855].

**Local Recurrence Rate:**
In multilevel total en bloc spondylectomy series, local recurrence occurred in **3/38 patients (8%)**  [pubmed_24838759].

## Critical Prognostic Factor: Prior Surgery Status

A notable finding regarding treatment history showed **no substantial difference** in recurrence rates between:
- **Treatment-naive patients** (n=40, direct presentation)
- **Patients with prior intralesional surgery** (n=45)

This indicates that previous inadequate intralesional approaches do not substantially increase recurrence risk when followed by definitive en bloc resection [pubmed_38367855].

## Functional Outcomes

**Ambulation and Independence:**
Multilevel total en bloc spondylectomy patients achieved ambulation without support in **34/38 patients (89%)** at final follow-up [pubmed_24838759].

**Gross Total Resection (GTR) Rates by Histology:**
For intramedullary spinal cord tumors specifically:
- Hemangioblastoma: **96.4% GTR** (en bloc resection in 53.6%)
- Ependymal tumors: **82.5% GTR** (en bloc resection in 16.7%)
- Astrocytic tumors: **55.6% GTR** (zero en bloc resections)
- *p < 0.001* [pubmed_40647411]

## Overall Survival and Disease-Free Survival

In multilevel spondylectomy series:
- **Overall survival: 34/38 (89%)** at final follow-up
- **Disease-free survival: 31/38 (81%)**
- **Postoperative mortality: 2/38 (5.3%)**
- **Systemic disease mortality (without local recurrence): 2/38 (5.3%)** [pubmed_24838759]

## Key Evidence Gaps

- Comparative effectiveness data between en bloc and separation surgery for primary spinal tumors is not provided in these papers (meta-analysis on this topic exists for metastases [pubmed_40449839] but outcome data not extracted)
- Long-term neurological outcome specifics beyond ambulation status
- Detailed analysis of prognostic factors beyond prior surgery status and histological type
- Specific complication rates beyond mortality
