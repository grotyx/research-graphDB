# Incidence, Risk Factors, and Prevention of Proximal Junctional Kyphosis and Proximal Junctional Failure in Adult Spinal Deformity Surgery

## INCIDENCE RATES

### Proximal Junctional Kyphosis (PJK)
- **Overall incidence: 20%-40%** in surgically treated patients [pubmed_41508591]
- Range reported: 17%-61.7% [pubmed_39973129]
- Significantly more frequent in overcorrected patients (p = 0.05) [pubmed_37740115]

### Proximal Junctional Failure (PJF)
- **Incidence: 2%-18%** of surgically treated patients [pubmed_41508591]
- Acute incidence: approximately **5.6%**, with **41% of affected patients requiring reoperation** [pubmed_41379878]

### Definition
PJK defined as increase of **≥10° in sagittal Cobb angle** from upper instrumented vertebra (UIV) to UIV+2 compared with preoperative measurement [pubmed_41283661]. PJF is the severe form of PJK, commonly presenting with pain, neurological deficits, reduced quality of life, and often requiring revision surgery [pubmed_41508591].

---

## RISK FACTORS

### Surgical Correction Parameters
**Overcorrection increases PJK risk:** Proximal junctional kyphosis was significantly more frequent in overcorrected versus undercorrected groups (p = 0.05) [pubmed_37740115]

### Roussouly Classification Mismatch
**Unmatched Roussouly type** significantly increases mechanical complications:
- **Pooled odds ratio for PJK: 1.59** (95% CI: 1.07-2.38, p = 0.02), representing a **59% increased risk** in unmatched patients [pubmed_39663226]
- Rod breakage odds ratio: 1.75 (95% CI: 1.15-2.66, p = 0.01), representing **75% increased risk** [pubmed_39663226]

### Additional Risk Factors
Multiple risk factors identified through comprehensive analysis using inverse probability weighting [pubmed_40153849], though specific quantitative data not detailed in abstracts provided.

---

## PREVENTIVE STRATEGIES

### 1. Posterior Ligamentous Augmentation (PLA)

**PLA techniques:**
- Spinous process tethering at UIV +1 or +2
- Sublaminar tethering at UIV +1 or +2 [pubmed_39973129]

**Efficacy:**
Proximal junctional tethering reduces odds of both PJK and PJF in adults undergoing spinal fusion [pubmed_41077974], though specific odds ratios not provided in abstract.

### 2. Vertebral Cement Augmentation (PVP)

A systematic review and meta-analysis examined vertebral cement augmentation as prevention strategy [pubmed_41283661], though specific efficacy data not provided in abstract.

### 3. Transverse Process Hooks vs. Pedicle Screws at UIV

Comparative study examining different fixation strategies at the upper instrumented vertebra in patients undergoing three-column osteotomies [pubmed_41297090], though specific comparative data not provided in abstract.

---

## CLINICAL IMPACT

**Quality of Life and Morbidity:**
- Many cases of PJK associated with **increased pain scores and Oswestry disability index values** [pubmed_41077974]
- PJK/PJF complications substantially increase likelihood of revision surgery [pubmed_41508591]
- Both PJK and PJF significantly reduce quality of life and increase revision rates and healthcare costs [pubmed_41379878]

---

## EVIDENCE GAPS

1. **Specific efficacy data:** The abstracts confirm PLA and tethering reduce PJK/PJF odds but do not provide precise odds ratios or confidence intervals for these interventions
2. **Vertebral cement augmentation outcomes:** Limited quantitative data on PVP effectiveness
3. **Comparative fixation strategies:** Transverse process hooks vs. pedicle screws outcomes not fully detailed
4. **Risk factor quantification:** Specific effect sizes for individual risk factors beyond Roussouly mismatch not provided in abstracts
5. **Long-term outcomes:** Limited data on durability of preventive strategies

# Proximal Junctional Kyphosis and Failure in Adult Spinal Deformity Surgery: A Evidence Synthesis

## INCIDENCE

### Proximal Junctional Kyphosis (PJK)
- **Incidence range: 17-61.7%** of cases following adult spinal deformity surgery [pubmed_39973129] (Systematic Review, Evidence 1a)
- **More conservative estimate: 20-40%** of surgically treated patients [pubmed_41508591] (Systematic Review, Evidence 2a)

### Proximal Junctional Failure (PJF)
- **Range: 2-18%** overall [pubmed_41508591] (Systematic Review, Evidence 2a)
- **Range: 5-20%** requiring revision surgery [pubmed_39973129] (Systematic Review, Evidence 1a)
- **Acute incidence: approximately 5.6%**, with 41% of PJF patients requiring reoperation [pubmed_41379878] (Multicenter Study, Evidence Level Not Specified)
- **Range: 5.6%-35%** (heterogeneous due to varying definitions and patient populations) [pubmed_40099853] (Biomechanical Study, Evidence 5)

---

## RISK FACTORS

### Non-Modifiable Factors
**Patient demographic and baseline characteristics:**
- Older age (>55, >65, >70 years) [pubmed_40033732] (Comprehensive Review, Evidence 4)
- Female sex [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)
- Comorbidities and higher ASA grade [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)
- Preoperative thoracic hyperkyphosis [pubmed_40099853] (Evidence 5)

### Modifiable Factors
**Bone quality and nutritional status:**
- High BMI (median 25.6 kg/m²) [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)
- Sarcopenia [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)
- Osteopenia/osteoporosis [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)

**Surgical and biomechanical factors:**
- Magnitude of sagittal correction (over-correction increases risk) [pubmed_41289491] (Integrated Review, Evidence 5)
- Inadequate proximal instrumentation selection [pubmed_41289491] (Evidence 5)
- Iatrogenic damage to posterior ligaments (supraspinous, interspinous) and paraspinal musculature [pubmed_41077974] (Systematic Review, Evidence 1a)
- Spinous process compromise [pubmed_41077974] (Evidence 1a)

**Additional failure mechanisms:**
- Vertebral fracture at upper instrumented vertebra (UIV) or UIV+1 [pubmed_41379878] (Evidence Not Specified)
- Screw pullout [pubmed_41379878] (Evidence Not Specified)
- Adjacent segment subluxation [pubmed_41379878] (Evidence Not Specified)
- Non-kyphotic failure patterns including disc degeneration and instrumentation failure [pubmed_40955719] (Multicenter Analysis, Evidence 2b)

---

## PREVENTIVE STRATEGIES

### Surgical Technical Approaches

**Instrumentation optimization:**
- Careful selection of proximal instrumentation [pubmed_41289491] (Integrated Review, Evidence 5)
- Transverse process hooks versus pedicle screws at UIV may have differential effects on PJK/PJF risk [pubmed_41297090] (Retrospective Comparative Study, Evidence 2b)
- **Proximal junctional tethering (ligamentous augmentation)**: Reduces odds of PJK and PJF [pubmed_41077974] (Systematic Review, Evidence 1a)

**Alignment strategies:**
- Optimal distal fusion levels [pubmed_41289491] (Evidence 5)
- Appropriate sagittal alignment correction (avoiding over-correction) [pubmed_41289491] (Evidence 5)
- Upper thoracic osteotomy when necessary [pubmed_41289491] (Evidence 5)

### Bone Quality Enhancement
**Pharmaceutical interventions** [pubmed_40033732] (Comprehensive Review, Evidence 4):
- Teriparatide and/or denosumab before/after surgery (Grade B recommended)
- Bisphosphonate (Grade C recommended)
- Cement augmentation (implied)

### Classification Considerations
The literature notes that current PJK definitions based solely on angular measurements may miss **non-kyphotic failure patterns** (vertebral fractures, disc degeneration, instrumentation failure) that represent important clinical complications [pubmed_40955719] (Evidence 2b), potentially limiting preventive strategy effectiveness if these are not specifically addressed.

---

## CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

**Outcomes of PJK/PJF:**
- Pain and neurological deficits [pubmed_41508591] (Evidence 2a)
- Substantially increased likelihood of revision surgery [pubmed_41508591] (Evidence 2a)
- Reduced quality of life [pubmed_41379878] (Evidence Not Specified)
- Significant increase in healthcare costs [pubmed_41379878] (Evidence Not Specified)

---

## EVIDENCE GAPS

1. Direct comparative effectiveness data on specific preventive strategies (limited to systematic reviews with heterogeneous interventions)
2. Optimal timing and dosing of bone quality medications
3. Comparative efficacy of different instrumentation types at UIV
4. Standardized classification for non-kyphotic PJF presentations to guide prevention
5. Machine learning prediction models remain under investigation [pubmed_41379878] but require external validation
