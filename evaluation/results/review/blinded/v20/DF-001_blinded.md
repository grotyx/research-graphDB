## Critical Limitation

## Relevant Evidence from Retrieved Papers

### 1. **UIV Distribution in Adult Spinal Deformity Studies**

[pubmed_35586905] reports UIV placement patterns in adult spinal deformity surgery (N=179 patients):
- Upper thoracic (T1-T9): 25 patients (14%)
- Lower thoracic (T10-T12): 118 patients (66%)
- Upper lumbar (L1-L3): 36 patients (20%)
- **Median length of instrumentation: 8 levels (IQR 7-9)**
- Osteotomies employed in 38 patients (21%)

This shows T10-T12 was the most common UIV choice (66%), but does not provide comparative outcome data between T10 and L1.

### 2. **Proximal Junctional Complications by Correction Magnitude**

[pubmed_37740115] demonstrates that aggressive correction increases proximal junctional kyphosis (PJK) risk:
- **PJK was significantly more frequent in overcorrected (O) group versus undercorrected (U) group (p = 0.05)**
- This suggests UIV selection influencing correction magnitude matters clinically, but specific T10 vs L1 comparisons are absent.

[pubmed_38270393] emphasizes segmental lordosis targeting:
- **Overcorrection beyond normative values at proximal spinal levels (T10-L2) resulted in dramatically increased proximal junctional failure (PJF) rates**
- No comparative data between T10 and L1 UIV placement

## Evidence Gaps

2. **No subgroup analyses** of Lenke 5C specifically (papers address general adult spinal deformity)
3. **No outcome data** stratified by UIV level for this patient population
4. **Missing data**: Coronal correction, sagittal alignment achievement, PJK/PJF rates by UIV level, long-term durability by UIV choice

## Clinical Implications from Available Evidence

While direct comparison is unavailable, relevant principles from the literature:

- **T10-T12 UIV is more commonly selected** (66% of cases in one series) [pubmed_35586905]
- **Overcorrection at proximal levels (T10-L2) increases PJF risk significantly** [pubmed_38270393]
- **Magnitude of correction matters more than implant choice alone** in proximal junctional complications [pubmed_41297090]
- **Interbody fusion adjuncts reduce proximal junctional complications** [pubmed_29087808]

- Patient-reported outcomes (HRQoL) by UIV selection

# Evidence Synthesis: Optimal UIV Selection for Lenke 5C Adult Degenerative Scoliosis

## Summary Statement

The provided papers do not contain specific evidence comparing T10 versus L1 as the upper instrumented vertebra for Lenke 5C adult degenerative scoliosis. However, general principles for UIV selection from the literature are relevant.

## General UIV Selection Principles

**Anatomic Considerations:**
- UIV selection should avoid spanning across major coronal deformities and consider the apex of thoracic kyphosis [pubmed_37718099, Evidence Level 4]
- The thoracolumbar junction (T10-L2 region) is a transitional area with higher risk of mechanical complications due to mobility differences between the mobile lumbar spine and less mobile thoracic spine [pubmed_40033732, Evidence Level 4]

**Risk Factor Data:**
- More proximal UIV placement (T2 or more proximal) was associated with increased distal junctional kyphosis (DJK) development in adolescent idiopathic scoliosis [pubmed_40103372, Evidence Level 3]. This suggests that proximal UIV placement alters thoracic sagittal alignment and may cause compensatory distal junctional changes.

**General Recommendation:**
- A "neutral and stable vertebra" is recommended as the UIV to minimize proximal junctional kyphosis/failure (PJK/PJF) risk [pubmed_40033732, Evidence Level 4]

## Evidence Gaps

**Critical limitation:** No studies directly comparing T10 versus L1 selection for Lenke 5C curves were identified. The optimal choice requires consideration of:
- Curve apex location
- Preoperative thoracic kyphosis
- Sagittal alignment targets
- Patient-specific factors

**Recommendation:** Evidence-based guidance for this specific clinical scenario requires studies comparing these instrumentation levels in Lenke 5C populations.
