## Radiographic Outcomes

### Segmental Lordosis Correction
**ACR with supplemental PSO vs ACR alone:**
- Addition of PSO to ACR increased segmental lordosis change by **72.7%** (p < 0.001) [pubmed_25820352]
- This demonstrates PSO provides significantly greater correction beyond standalone ACR

### Sagittal Profile Restoration
- Restoration of Roussouly profile achieved with type-matched correction correlated with improved outcomes [pubmed_41521065]

---

## Complication Profile

### Mechanical Complications

**Roussouly Profile Restoration (Sagittal Alignment Strategy):**
- **Restored group:** 7/26 patients (27%) experienced mechanical complications
- **Unrestored group:** 23/26 patients (88%) experienced mechanical complications
- **Difference:** 61 percentage point reduction (p < 0.001) [pubmed_37768339]

### Known PSO Morbidity
PSO is associated with substantial intraoperative complications including:
- **High blood loss** [pubmed_41497819]
- **Neurological injury risk** [pubmed_41497819]
- **Prolonged operative times** [pubmed_41497819]

### Elderly Population (Septuagenarians)
- Traditional 3CO "carries a high risk of complications, especially in the elderly" [pubmed_40968391]
- This population represents a significant limitation for conventional three-column approaches

---

## Clinical Outcomes

No specific quantitative clinical outcome data (ODI, SRS-24 scores, pain scales) were provided in the abstracts for three-column osteotomies. One study [pubmed_25614149] assessed outcomes with ODI and SRS-24 questionnaires but numerical results were not reported in the available text.

---

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Limited complication rate specificity:** The meta-analysis [pubmed_41497819] compares PSO to ACR but does not report specific complication percentages, only qualitative descriptions of "substantial morbidity"
2. **Minimal VCR data:** Only one systematic review [pubmed_38765779] mentions VCRO but provides no outcome data (MINORS scores only documented, not results)
3. **Missing comparative safety data:** No head-to-head complication rates between PSO and ACR are quantified in these abstracts
4. **Septuagenarian outcomes unclear:** While [pubmed_40968391] notes 3CO carries high complication risk in elderly patients, specific complication rates are not provided in the abstract

---

## Summary

Three-column osteotomies (PSO) achieve powerful sagittal correction, with **72.7% greater segmental lordosis change** when combined with ACR compared to ACR alone (p < 0.001). However, PSO carries substantial operative morbidity including high blood loss, neurological injury risk, and prolonged operative times. In elderly populations, traditional 3CO approaches carry elevated complication risk. Achievement of Roussouly profile restoration correlates with 61 percentage point reduction in mechanical complications (27% vs 88%, p < 0.001), suggesting that sagittal alignment strategy significantly impacts outcomes.

# Outcomes of Three-Column Osteotomies for Fixed Sagittal Imbalance

## Radiological Outcomes

### Sagittal Correction Capability
Three-column osteotomies (3-CO), particularly PSO, demonstrate superior correction compared to anterior-only approaches:

- **PSO vs. ACR alone**: Addition of PSO to ACR increased segmental lordosis correction by **72.7%** compared to ACR alone (p < 0.001) [pubmed_25820352], demonstrating that PSO provides significantly greater correction for rigid deformity [pubmed_32382878]

- **Evidence quality**: A meta-analysis [pubmed_41497819] (Evidence: 1a) included three prospective/retrospective clinical studies (2017-2023) and two biomechanical simulation studies (2022, 2024) comparing correction techniques, with lumbar lordosis measurements and complication rates reported across studies with follow-up periods ranging heterogeneously

## Complication Profile

### Overall Complication Rates
In septuagenarians undergoing 3-CO for thoracolumbar kyphosis [pubmed_40968391] (Evidence: 2b):
- **Total complications**: 28 complications in 19 patients (**35.2%**)
- Complications exclusive to 3CO included: surgical site infection (5.6%), hematoma (1.9%), and DVT [pubmed_40968391]

### Neurological Complications
- **New-onset neurological deficits**: 5 cases (9.3%) in 3CO group, all classified as Frankel C, with 3 improving to Frankel D after 2-year rehabilitation [pubmed_40968391]

## Evidence Gaps

- Limited data specifically stratified for PSO vs. VCR outcomes
- Heterogeneous follow-up periods across included studies [pubmed_41497819]
- Systematic review of osteotomy approaches [pubmed_38765779] (Evidence: 4) provided descriptive synthesis but limited outcome quantification
- Long-term HRQOL data synthesis available but specific metric details not fully detailed in retrieved papers [pubmed_32382878]
