# Laminoplasty versus Laminectomy with Fusion for Multilevel Cervical Myelopathy

## Summary of Evidence

Both approaches are effective for treating multilevel cervical myelopathy, but **laminoplasty demonstrates superior outcomes across multiple domains** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362].

---

## Clinical Outcomes

### Neurological Improvement
Both procedures achieve **comparable neurological recovery**:
- Similar improvements in Japanese Orthopaedic Association (JOA) scores between groups [pubmed_30921202, pubmed_30813208]
- Both procedures effectively decompress the spinal cord and improve neurological function [pubmed_30921202]
- Visual analog scale (VAS) neck pain improvements are similar between approaches [pubmed_30921202]

### Neck Disability and Function
- **Laminoplasty achieved lower Neck Disability Index scores** compared to laminectomy with fusion [pubmed_38764362]

---

## Operative Characteristics (Laminoplasty Advantages)

**Shorter operative time** [pubmed_38764362]

**Less estimated blood loss** [pubmed_38764362]

**Comparable hospital length of stay** between both approaches [pubmed_38764362]

---

## Complication Profiles

### Overall Complication Rates
- **Laminoplasty: Lower overall complication rates** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362]
- Laminectomy and fusion: Associated with more complications [pubmed_30921202]

### Specific Complications
- **C5 palsy: Lower incidence with laminoplasty** [pubmed_38764362]
- Laminectomy and fusion: Associated with postoperative segmental instability and kyphosis when performed without fusion [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_33819935]

---

## Biomechanical Outcomes

### Cervical Range of Motion
- **Laminoplasty: Greater postoperative cervical range of motion preservation** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_33819935]
- Laminoplasty preserves motion and minimizes alteration to cervical spine biomechanics [pubmed_27281067]

### Cervical Alignment
- **Laminoplasty: Lower T1 slope angle** (more favorable sagittal alignment) [pubmed_38764362]

---

## Procedure Indications

**Laminectomy with fusion** is favored over laminectomy alone because fusion reduces postoperative segmental instability [pubmed_33819935]

**Laminoplasty** is an alternative approach that provides advantages in motion preservation [pubmed_33819935]

---

## Evidence Quality and Limitations

- **High-quality evidence (1a)**: Multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362, pubmed_27281067, pubmed_25815808, pubmed_30921202]
- **Moderate-quality evidence (2a)**: Systematic reviews with nonrandomized comparative studies [pubmed_27781191, pubmed_33819935]
- **Important limitation**: No randomized controlled trials comparing these approaches; most evidence derives from observational studies with potential surgeon selection bias [pubmed_27781191]
- **Special populations**: Limited specific evidence for ossification of the posterior longitudinal ligament (OPLL) subgroup [pubmed_27781191]

---

## Clinical Recommendation

**Laminoplasty emerges as the preferable approach** for multilevel cervical myelopathy based on superior outcomes in operative time, blood loss, complication rates, C5 palsy incidence, motion preservation, and functional scores [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362], while maintaining comparable neurological improvement to laminectomy with fusion.

# Laminoplasty vs. Laminectomy with Fusion for Multilevel Cervical Myelopathy: Evidence Synthesis

## Overview of Evidence Quality
All major comparative analyses are Level 1a meta-analyses (5 studies) [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_30921202, pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362, pubmed_37062984], with additional systematic reviews [pubmed_23963000, pubmed_27781191].

---

## Primary Outcomes Comparison

### Clinical Improvement (Functional Outcomes)

**JOA Scores (Japanese Orthopedic Association):**
- Both procedures achieve **substantial improvements** with **equivalent JOA scores** [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_30813208]
- Laminoplasty showed **better NDI (Neck Disability Index) scores** compared to laminectomy-fusion [pubmed_37062984]

**VAS (Visual Analog Scale for Pain):**
- **Equivalent pain reduction** between approaches [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_30813208]
- Both achieved clinical improvement [pubmed_24679196]

---

## Operative and Technical Outcomes

| Outcome | Laminoplasty | Laminectomy-Fusion | P-value | Citation |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|---------|-----------|
| **Operation Time** | 19.57 minutes shorter | Baseline | p=0.002 | [pubmed_27281067] |
| **Blood Loss** | Reduced | Higher | Not specified | [pubmed_38764362, pubmed_27281067] |
| **Cervical Range of Motion** | Greater preservation | Reduced | Not specified | [pubmed_30813208] |
| **Cervical Lordosis Preservation** | Equivalent | Equivalent | - | [pubmed_27281067] |
| **Loss of Lordosis** | Similar | Similar | - | [pubmed_27281067] |

---

## Complication Rates: Critical Comparison

### C5 Palsy (Nerve Root Palsy)

**Laminoplasty vs. Laminectomy-Fusion:**
- Laminoplasty: **OR 0.26** (p<0.001) — **74% risk reduction** [pubmed_27281067]
- Incidence: **Lower in laminoplasty** [pubmed_37062984, pubmed_38764362]
- Posterior surgeries overall: C5 palsy **9.6%–25%** [pubmed_39829181]

### Total Complication Rates

- **Laminoplasty: Lower overall complication rates** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_37062984]
- Equivalent total complications in some comparisons [pubmed_27281067]
- **Laminoplasty showed statistically significant advantages** [pubmed_30813208]

### Axial Neck Pain

- **Equivalent incidence** between approaches [pubmed_27281067]
- Posterior surgeries: **22.2% axial pain incidence** [pubmed_39829181]

### Procedure-Specific Complications

**Laminectomy-Fusion risks:**
- Postlaminectomy kyphosis [pubmed_39829181]
- Adjacent segment disease and need for additional surgery [pubmed_30921202]
- Postoperative segmental instability [pubmed_30921202]

**Laminoplasty risks:**
- Generally fewer complications reported [pubmed_30813208]

---

## Spinal Cord Decompression

- **Both approaches achieve substantial spinal cord decompression** [pubmed_30813208]
- No differential advantage documented [pubmed_24679196]

---

## Comparison with Anterior Decompression and Fusion (ADF)

**ADF vs. Posterior Laminoplasty for multilevel myelopathy:**
- ADF associated with **better postoperative neural function** [pubmed_24679196]
- **No apparent difference in neural function recovery rate** between approaches [pubmed_24679196]
- ADF-specific complications: CSF leakage (2.4%–31%), dysphagia/dysarthria/hoarseness (2.4%–16.8%) [pubmed_39829181]

---

## Emerging Technology: Endoscopic Cervical Surgery

- 12 studies, 226 total excluded during screening [pubmed_41489665]
- **All studies reported improvement in clinical outcomes** compared to preoperative assessment [pubmed_41489665]
- Limited comparative data against open approaches

---

## Special Populations

### Ossification of Posterior Longitudinal Ligament (OPLL)

- Laminoplasty: **Fewer complications**, **lower C5 palsy incidence**, **better NDI scores** [pubmed_37062984]
- Both procedures showed **similar clinical improvement** [pubmed_37062984]
- **Limitations in available evidence:** Nonrandomized studies with surgeon selection bias [pubmed_27781191]

---

## Summary of Comparative Efficacy

### Laminoplasty Advantages:
1. **Shorter operative time** (19.57 minutes, p=0.002) [pubmed_27281067]
2. **Significantly lower C5 palsy** (OR 0.26, p<0.001) [pubmed_27281067]
3. **Reduced blood loss** [pubmed_38764362]
4. **Lower overall complication rates** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_37062984]
5. **Better cervical ROM preservation** [pubmed_30813208]
6. **Better NDI scores** [pubmed_37062984]

### Laminectomy-Fusion Advantages:
- **None documented with statistical superiority**
- Equivalent JOA improvement [pubmed_27281067]

### Equivalent Outcomes:
- Functional improvement (JOA scores) [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_30813208]
- Pain reduction (VAS) [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_30813208]
- Spinal cord decompression [pubmed_30813208]
- Axial pain incidence [pubmed_27281067]
- Cervical lordosis preservation [pubmed_27281067]

---

## Clinical Recommendation

**Laminoplasty remains the preferable approach** for multilevel cervical myelopathy based on:
- Superior operative metrics (time, blood loss)
- Significantly reduced C5 palsy risk (74% relative risk reduction)
- Lower total complication rates
- Equivalent functional outcomes
- Better cervical ROM and NDI preservation [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362, pubmed_37062984]

**However, careful patient selection remains paramount,** as both procedures achieve clinical improvement and are effective surgical treatments [pubmed_38764362, pubmed_30813208].

---

## Evidence Gaps

1. **No randomized controlled trials** comparing these approaches directly [pubmed_27781191]
2. Limited data on **long-term adjacent segment disease rates** for fusion
3. Insufficient data on **endoscopic approaches vs. open surgery** outcomes [pubmed_41489665]
4. Need for prospective validation studies in OPLL populations [pubmed_37062984]
