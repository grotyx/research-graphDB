# Proximal Junctional Kyphosis After Adult Spinal Deformity Surgery: Incidence and Risk Factors

## INCIDENCE OF PJK

### Overall Incidence Rates
PJK demonstrates substantial variability in reported incidence across studies:
- **17-61.7%** of adult spinal deformity cases [pubmed_39973129]
- PJK occurs frequently enough to represent "one of the most frequent complications after adult spinal deformity surgery" [pubmed_41379878]

### Proximal Junctional Failure (PJF) - Related Complication
- **Acute incidence of PJF: 5-20%** of patients with PJK may progress to PJF requiring revision surgery [pubmed_39973129]
- **PJF incidence: 5.6%** with 41% of PJF patients requiring reoperation [pubmed_41379878]

---

## IDENTIFIED RISK FACTORS

### 1. **Surgical Correction Strategy**
| Risk Factor | Finding | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Overcorrection | PJK significantly more frequent in overcorrected group (p = 0.05) | [pubmed_37740115] |
| PI-LL Overcorrection | Associated with increased proximal junctional failure | [pubmed_41167281] |
| Undercorrection | Associated with increased implant failure rates | [pubmed_38270393] |

### 2. **Preoperative Alignment Parameters**

**High Preoperative Thoracic Kyphosis (Independent Risk Factor)**
- OR = 1.046, p = 0.003 [pubmed_41167281]
- Patients with severe preoperative thoracic misalignment face inherent risk for proximal junctional complications "regardless of the magnitude of lumbar correction" [pubmed_41167281]

### 3. **Postoperative Vertebral Orientation**
- L1 vertebra orientation in postoperative period is associated with PJK occurrence, though specific effect sizes not fully detailed [pubmed_40521654]

### 4. **Thoracolumbar Inflection Point Positioning**
- Achieving postoperative correction to normative Roussouly values reduced rod breakage and some mechanical complications; however, **PJK rates remained elevated** despite proper inflection point positioning [pubmed_40450887]
- This suggests additional factors beyond inflection point positioning influence PJK development, including "individual variations in bone quality, soft tissue compliance, neuromuscular factors" [pubmed_40450887]

### 5. **Segmental Lordosis Correction**
- Overcorrection of normative segmental lordosis values correlates with higher proximal junctional failure rates [pubmed_38270393]
- Undercorrection correlates with increased implant failure [pubmed_38270393]

---

## CLINICAL IMPACT

PJK and PJF represent significant sources of patient morbidity with the following consequences:
- Reduction in quality of life [pubmed_41379878]
- Significantly increased revision surgery rates [pubmed_41379878]
- Increased healthcare costs [pubmed_41379878]
- Often require revision surgery necessitating additional procedures [pubmed_41297090]

2. **Surgical planning tools validation**: Reference [pubmed_36999928] compares four preoperative planning guidelines but specific incidence rates by guideline are not provided
3. **Transverse process hooks vs. pedicle screws**: Reference [pubmed_41297090] suggests comparative analysis but specific PJK/PJF rates not detailed in provided text

**Summary**: PJK occurs in 17-61.7% of adult spinal deformity surgery patients, with 5-20% progressing to PJF requiring revision. Key modifiable risk factors include surgical overcorrection (particularly PI-LL overcorrection), while non-modifiable factors include high preoperative thoracic kyphosis (OR=1.046, p=0.003). Risk factors are multifactorial, and even optimal alignment correction does not completely prevent PJK development.

# Proximal Junctional Kyphosis After Adult Spinal Deformity Surgery: Incidence and Risk Factors

## INCIDENCE OF PJK

**Overall Incidence Rates:**
PJK occurs in **17-61.7% of cases** following adult spinal deformity surgery [pubmed_39973129, systematic review and meta-analysis, Evidence 1a]. More severe complications progress to proximal junctional failure (PJF) requiring revision surgery in **5-20% of patients** [pubmed_39973129, Evidence 1a]. PJF has an acute incidence of approximately **5.6%**, with **41% of patients requiring reoperation** [pubmed_41379878].

---

## RISK FACTORS FOR PJK

### **Surgical/Biomechanical Factors**

**Greater Sagittal Plane Correction (Primary Factor):**
- **Larger global lumbar lordosis correction** was an independent predictor of PJK (p < 0.001) [pubmed_40153849, inverse probability weighting analysis, Evidence 2b]
- **Greater sagittal vertical axis (SVA) correction** was significantly associated with PJK (p = 0.020) [pubmed_40153849, Evidence 2b]
- These findings suggest that **aggressive sagittal plane correction creates excessive biomechanical stress at the proximal junction** [pubmed_40153849]

**Vertebral Orientation:**
- **More caudal baseline inflection point** was associated with higher PJK/PJF risk. Patients with inflection point at L2 inferior endplate versus L1 body had **25% higher odds of developing PJF by 2 years (OR 1.26, 95% CI 1.08-1.46, p = 0.003)** [pubmed_40450887, adjusted analysis, Evidence 2b]

### **Patient-Related Factors**

**Non-Modifiable Factors:**
- Older age (>55, >65, >70 years) [pubmed_40033732, Evidence 4]
- Female sex [pubmed_40033732, Evidence 4]
- Comorbidities and higher ASA grade [pubmed_40033732, Evidence 4]

**Modifiable Factors:**
- High BMI (median 25.6 kg/m²) [pubmed_40033732, Evidence 4]
- **Paraspinal sarcopenia** - independent predictor of increased PJK/PJF risk [pmid_39091148, multivariate analysis, Evidence 2b]
- **Lower Hounsfield units** (indicating poor bone quality) - independent predictor of PJK/PJF [pmid_39091148, Evidence 2b]
- Osteopenia/osteoporosis [pubmed_40033732, Evidence 4]

---

## CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

- PJK and PJF represent **the most frequent mechanical complications** after long-segment spinal instrumentation [pubmed_40099853]
- Revision surgery is indicated in **up to 50% of patients with PJK** [pubmed_40099853]
- These complications **significantly reduce quality of life and increase healthcare costs** [pubmed_41379878]

---

## EVIDENCE GAPS

The provided literature does not comprehensively detail:
- Comparative effectiveness of different fixation strategies (e.g., transverse process hooks vs. pedicle screws at UIV, though one study addresses this [pubmed_41297090])
- Long-term follow-up data beyond 2 years
- Optimal correction thresholds to balance deformity correction with PJK prevention
