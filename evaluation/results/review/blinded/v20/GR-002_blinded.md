# Shared and Unique Outcomes: TLIF vs PLIF

## Shared Outcomes Between TLIF and PLIF

Based on the available evidence, there is **limited direct comparison** between TLIF and PLIF in the provided papers. However, one study provides relevant information:

**Fusion Success and 2-Year Outcomes:**
A systematic review [pubmed_23646277] (Evidence: 1a) comparing fusion techniques reported that "the type of fusion, PLF or PLIF did not affect 2-year outcomes" in patients with isthmic spondylolisthesis. Additionally, "they reported no cases of pseudoarthrosis in either group" in a comparative study of PLF versus PLIF (n=11 per group). This suggests both interbody fusion approaches achieve comparable long-term fusion rates and clinical outcomes.

## Unique Outcomes by Procedure

### TLIF-Specific Outcomes
The papers provide extensive TLIF outcome data but do NOT directly compare TLIF-unique benefits to PLIF:

- **Complication profiles:** TLIF showed higher rates of dural tears and wound complications compared to LLIF [analyzed_ba35d6e5] (Evidence: 2b), though this comparison does not involve PLIF
- **Surgical approach variations:** Outcomes differ between endoscopic, biportal endoscopic, and minimally invasive TLIF approaches [pubmed_41420307] (Evidence: 1a)

### PLIF-Specific Outcomes
The provided papers contain **no outcome data specific to PLIF** that would allow differentiation from TLIF.

## Evidence Gaps

**Critical limitation:** The retrieved papers do NOT contain direct head-to-head comparisons of TLIF versus PLIF clinical outcomes. A comprehensive answer to this question would require additional literature specifically comparing these two approaches.

# Comparative Outcomes: TLIF vs PLIF

## Summary of Available Evidence

The provided papers contain **limited direct comparison** between TLIF and PLIF. Here's what can be extracted:

### Direct TLIF vs PLIF Comparison

**Paper [pubmed_18316711]** provides qualitative comparison only:
- States TLIF has **lower complication rates compared to PLIF** [pubmed_18316711]
- Notes TLIF has **greater versatility throughout the entire lumbar spine** [pubmed_18316711]
- **No specific numerical data provided** on complication rates, VAS, ODI, or other outcomes

### OLIF vs PLIF Comparison (Indirect Context)

**Paper [pubmed_37950267]** compares OLIF to PLIF across 574 patients (287 per group), providing relevant context on PLIF outcomes:

| Outcome | OLIF | PLIF | P-value |
|---------|------|------|---------|
| Postop VAS improvement | −0.43 better | Baseline | P=0.04 |
| Postop ODI improvement | −1.22 better | Baseline | P=0.007 |
| Disc height recovery | Better | Baseline | P≤0.05 |

[pubmed_37950267] describes these as PLIF baseline comparators but does not provide absolute PLIF values.

### TLIF-Specific Data

**Paper [pubmed_41676385]** demonstrates TLIF achieves:
- Significant ODI improvement (p<0.05) at 12-24 months [pubmed_41676385]
- Significant EQ-5D improvement (p<0.05) at 12-24 months [pubmed_41676385]
- Significant VAS back pain reduction (p<0.05) at 12-24 months [pubmed_41676385]
- Leg pain improvement approaching but not reaching significance [pubmed_41676385]

## Critical Evidence Gap

**The provided papers do NOT contain direct head-to-head outcome comparisons** with specific numerical values for both TLIF and PLIF on shared outcomes (VAS, ODI, complication rates, fusion rates). Paper [pubmed_18316711] is limited to Evidence Level 4 and provides only qualitative conclusions without quantitative outcome data.

**No unique outcomes to either procedure** are identifiable from the provided literature.
