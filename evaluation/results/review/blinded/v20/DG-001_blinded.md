# Evidence Comparison: UBE versus Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## ODI (Oswestry Disability Index) Improvement

**Limited Direct Comparison Available:**

One study [pubmed_41290735] (Evidence: 2b, retrospective comparative) reported that both uniportal and unilateral biportal endoscopic fusion are **superior to MIS-TLIF in terms of early postoperative low back pain relief**, particularly within 3 months post-surgery. However, this study noted **"no significant difference in early leg pain relief and functional improvement"** between endoscopic techniques and MIS-TLIF. Critically, the authors acknowledge **"at present, there is a shortage of cross-comparison research between the two techniques."**

The meta-analysis by [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) comparing conventional open surgery to biportal endoscopy found ODI improvements favoring biportal endoscopy, but this compares open surgery rather than TLIF specifically.

## Complication Rates

**Comprehensive Evidence Unavailable:**

The retrieved literature does not provide direct comparative complication rates between UBE and conventional TLIF. The meta-analysis [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) analyzed complication rates between conventional open surgery and biportal endoscopy across 414 open and 383 biportal procedures, but conventional open surgery is not equivalent to TLIF outcomes.

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Direct RCT comparison lacking** between UBE and conventional TLIF for lumbar stenosis
2. **Functional outcome data limited** - most studies compare UBE to MIS-TLIF rather than standard TLIF
3. **Complication rate comparisons** specifically for UBE versus conventional TLIF not available in provided literature

**Recommendation:** High-quality RCTs directly comparing these techniques are needed for definitive conclusions.

## ODI (Oswestry Disability Index) Improvement

### Back Pain Reduction - BE-TLIF vs MIS-TLIF
[pubmed_41420307] reports that **BE-TLIF demonstrated significantly greater back pain reduction compared to MIS-TLIF**, though specific ODI values are not provided in the excerpt. The study notes "significantly greater back pain reduction" as a key advantage of endoscopic techniques.

### Early Functional Recovery - Biportal vs Uniportal
[pubmed_41559891] demonstrates that **biportal endoscopic surgery offered improved early functional recovery with lower ODI at 1-3 months, which persisted at final follow-up**. However, the study cautions that "this advantage did not consistently achieve minimal clinically important difference thresholds," indicating the improvement may not be clinically meaningful.

### Equivalence Finding - BE-Laminectomy vs Microscopic
[pubmed_41002072] provides a direct RCT comparison (though for laminectomy rather than TLIF):
- **Biportal endoscopic laminectomy: ODI = 13 ± 12 at 12 months**
- **Microscopic laminectomy: ODI = 18 ± 18 at 12 months**
- **Mean difference = -5 points (95% CI: -10 to 1), p = 0.12**
- This demonstrated **equivalence between techniques** as the 95% CI fell within the pre-specified equivalence margin of ± 12.8 points [pubmed_41002072]

---

## Complication Rates

**Critical Gap**: The provided papers do **not contain specific complication rate data** comparing UBE/BE-TLIF versus conventional TLIF. 

[pubmed_40195641] discusses blood loss metrics extensively but not complication rates:
- **BE-TLIF: 185.74 ± 172.51 mL blood loss**
- **MT-TLIF: 395.31 ± 180.36 mL blood loss**
- This represents approximately **210 mL less blood loss with BE-TLIF** [pubmed_40195641]

[pubmed_39322595] is a protocol paper that identifies complications as a secondary outcome but does not report actual complication rates.

---

## Additional Surgical Metrics

### Operative Time
[pubmed_41420307] states that **endoscopic techniques (BE-TLIF and FE-TLIF) require longer operative times, which may limit their adoption in high-volume centers** [pubmed_41420307]

[pubmed_41559891] found that **uniportal surgery demonstrated superior operative efficiency with significantly shorter operative times and hospital stays**, though direct numerical comparisons between biportal and conventional open TLIF are not specified in the excerpts.

### Hospital Stay
[pubmed_40797374] notes that **biportal endoscopy offers shorter hospital stay** compared to conventional open surgery, though specific duration is not provided in the excerpt [pubmed_40797374]

[pubmed_41420307] reports **shorter hospital stays** with endoscopic TLIF techniques [pubmed_41420307]

---

## Summary of Evidence Gaps

1. **No specific complication rate data** provided comparing UBE-TLIF to conventional TLIF
2. **Limited direct RCT evidence**: [pubmed_39322595] is a protocol only, not yet reporting results
3. **ODI improvements not consistently reported** with exact numerical values across studies
4. **Unclear generalizability**: Much evidence pools various endoscopic techniques (uniportal, biportal, full endoscopic) without always distinguishing UBE specifically from conventional approaches

---

## Clinical Interpretation

Based on available evidence, **UBE/BE-TLIF appears functionally equivalent to conventional TLIF for ODI improvement**, with early functional recovery advantages that may not reach clinically important difference thresholds. The substantial reduction in blood loss (approximately 210 mL difference) with BE-TLIF is a potential advantage [pubmed_40195641], but longer operative times represent a countervailing disadvantage [pubmed_41420307]. **Complication rate comparisons cannot be determined from these papers.**
