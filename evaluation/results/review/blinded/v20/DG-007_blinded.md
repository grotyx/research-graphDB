## Evidence Summary: LLIF/XLIF vs TLIF for Degenerative Spondylolisthesis

### Study Design and Quality Assessment

Based on the provided papers, **there are NO randomized controlled trials (RCTs) directly comparing LLIF/XLIF versus TLIF for degenerative spondylolisthesis**. The highest level of evidence available is:

- **Systematic reviews and meta-analyses (Level 1a)** comparing OLIF (oblique lateral interbody fusion) versus TLIF [pubmed_38622724, pubmed_39575949]
- **Comparative cohort studies (Level 1b-2a)** examining endoscopic TLIF variants [pubmed_38752236, pubmed_39823634]

### OLIF vs TLIF Evidence (Most Relevant Proxy)

Since OLIF is a lateral approach technique, the OLIF vs TLIF comparisons provide the most relevant evidence:

**Systematic Review/Meta-Analysis Findings [pubmed_38622724, pubmed_39575949]:**

| Outcome | OLIF vs TLIF | Evidence |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Surgical Duration** | OLIF shorter | [pubmed_38622724] |
| **Intraoperative Bleeding** | OLIF reduced | [pubmed_38622724] |
| **Hospital Stay** | OLIF shorter | [pubmed_38622724] |
| **Postoperative VAS** | OLIF superior improvement | [pubmed_38622724] |
| **Postoperative ODI** | OLIF superior improvement | [pubmed_38622724] |
| **Disc Height Restoration** | OLIF better | [pubmed_38622724] |
| **Lumbar Lordosis Correction** | OLIF superior | [pubmed_38622724, pubmed_39575949] |
| **Segmental Lordosis** | OLIF superior to TLIF | [pubmed_39575949] |

**Specific Quantitative Data from OLIF vs PLIF [pubmed_37950267]:**
- Sample: 574 patients (287 OLIF, 287 PLIF) across 8 retrospective case-control studies
- **Postoperative VAS**: OLIF −0.43 points better (P=0.04)
- **Postoperative ODI**: OLIF −1.22 points better (P=0.007)
- **Disc height recovery**: OLIF superior [specific values not numerically detailed]

### Endoscopic TLIF Comparisons [pubmed_38752236]

**Unilateral biportal endoscopic TLIF vs Standard endoscopic TLIF:**
- **Major complications**: 0% in both groups
- **Operative time**: UBE-TLIF advantage noted (specific times not provided)
- **Blood loss**: UBE-TLIF showed increased blood loss (specific values not provided)

### Broader Lateral Fusion Technique Network Analysis [pubmed_39895414]

Network meta-analysis comparing 6 minimally invasive techniques (X/DLIF, OLIF, SA-LLIF, MIS-TLIF, UELIF, BELIF):
- **Clinical outcomes (pain/disability relief)**: Equivalent across techniques
- **Operative time**: SA-LLIF superior (shorter)
- **Hospitalization recovery**: SA-LLIF faster
- **Limitation**: Cage subsidence risk and reoperation needs mentioned but specific rates not detailed in abstract

---

## Critical Evidence Gaps

1. **No RCTs available** comparing LLIF/XLIF specifically versus TLIF for spondylolisthesis
2. **OLIF data** (oblique lateral) used as proxy—direct LLIF/XLIF vs TLIF RCT evidence is absent
3. **Specific numerical comparisons unavailable** for operative time, blood loss, and hospital stay (qualitative superiority reported but not quantified)
4. **Spondylolisthesis-specific outcomes**: The meta-analysis [pubmed_38622724] addressed spondylolisthesis but did not provide stratified quantitative data separating outcomes by approach

### Recommendation

**Evidence-based practice cannot be solely grounded in direct RCT evidence for this specific comparison**. Clinical decisions should incorporate the Level 1a evidence from OLIF vs TLIF comparisons while acknowledging this represents lateral oblique rather than direct lateral (LLIF/XLIF) approaches, and recognizing the absence of dedicated RCT evidence.

# Evidence Summary: LLIF/XLIF vs TLIF for Degenerative Spondylolisthesis

## Key Finding
**No RCTs are available in the provided papers.** The highest-quality evidence comes from systematic reviews and meta-analyses of observational studies.

---

## Available Evidence by Study Design

### Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analyses (Evidence Level 1a)

**OLIF vs TLIF for Spondylolisthesis** [pubmed_38622724]
- Systematic review and meta-analysis comparing oblique lateral approach (OLIF) with TLIF for **degenerative lumbar spondylolisthesis**
- Study design: Systematic review of comparative studies (January 2014-February 2024)
- *Specific outcome data not provided in abstract*

**Endoscopic TLIF vs OLIF-360 for Spondylolisthesis** [pubmed_41525955]
- Retrospective cohort comparison (Evidence: 2b, not RCT)
- **Complementary advantages noted:**
  - Endo-TLIF: Superior minimization of perioperative morbidity through direct visualization
  - OLIF-360: Superior structural reconstruction

### General Lateral Fusion Comparisons (Evidence Level 1a)

**Network Meta-Analysis of Multiple MIS Techniques** [pubmed_39895414]
- Included X/DLIF, OLIF, SA-LLIF, MIS-TLIF, UELIF, and BELIF
- Finding: **Equivalent clinical outcomes** in pain and disability relief regardless of fusion approach
- Note: Stand-alone LLIF (SA-LLIF) showed superior early efficacy (shorter operative time, faster recovery)

**Segmental Lordosis Restoration** [pubmed_39575949]
- OLIF demonstrated **superior restoration of segmental lordosis** compared with TLIF
- LLIF demonstrated **improved segmental lordosis restoration** compared with TLIF in 2 of 3 directly comparative studies

---

## Evidence Gaps

- **No randomized controlled trials** identified comparing LLIF/XLIF with TLIF specifically for degenerative spondylolisthesis
- Highest-quality evidence for spondylolisthesis comes from observational comparative studies
- Long-term outcome data limited in retrieved papers
