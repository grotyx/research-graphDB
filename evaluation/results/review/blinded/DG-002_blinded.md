# Evidence Summary: CDR vs ACDF for Adjacent Segment Degeneration in 2-Level Cervical Disc Disease

## Adjacent Segment Degeneration Rates

### Overall ASD Risk Reduction
Multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses (Evidence Level 1a) consistently demonstrate significant ASD reduction with CDR compared to ACDF:

- **RR = 0.40** [95% CI: 0.35, 0.46, P < .00001] [pubmed_29595628]
- **OR = 0.27** [95% CI: 0.13-0.59, p=0.001] with low heterogeneity [pubmed_29753897]
- **OR = 0.33** [95% CI: 0.21; 0.50, P<0.0001, I²=0.0%] across 5 RCTs with 1,594 patients [pubmed_30992666]
- **Absolute difference: 2.3 percentage points** (ACDF 6.1% vs CDR 3.8%; P < 0.001) [pubmed_35085804]

### Symptomatic vs Radiological ASD
An important distinction emerges regarding clinical significance [pubmed_31863642]:

- **Symptomatic ASD:** Significantly lower with CDR (OR 0.46, 95% CI [0.34, 0.63], p<0.00001) across 6 studies with 1,628 participants
- **Radiological superior ASD:** No significant difference (OR 0.63, 95% CI [0.28, 1.43], p=0.27)
- This suggests symptomatic progression may reflect natural disease progression rather than surgical technique effect

## Mechanism of Benefit

CDR reduces ASD through **preservation of segmental motion** and **maintenance of normal biomechanical environment**, which maintains lower intradiscal pressure in adjacent segments and alleviates inflammatory cascades [pubmed_29595628].

## Evidence Quality and Gaps

- **Quality of evidence:** Moderate, based on GRADE assessment [pubmed_30285807]
- **Heterogeneity:** Generally low across pooled analyses, supporting robustness of findings
- **Gap:** Limited specific data isolated to 2-level disease; most meta-analyses include multi-level procedures

# CDR vs ACDF for 2-Level Cervical Disc Disease: Adjacent Segment Degeneration Evidence

## I. Clinical Adjacent Segment Disease (ASD)

### Symptomatic ASD Rates
**CDR demonstrates lower symptomatic ASD incidence:**

- **Goffin et al. (2002)** - Prospective comparative study (Level II): ProDisc device showed 9% symptomatic ASD at 3 years versus historical ACDF controls with 14-16% rates
- **Murrey et al. (2009)** - Prospective randomized controlled trial (Level I, Bryan disc): 3.9% symptomatic ASD in CDR vs 10.5% in ACDF at 2 years (p=0.034)
- **Hilibrand et al. (1999)** - Landmark ACDF natural history study (Level II-III): Identified annual symptomatic ASD rate of 2.3% following single-level ACDF

### Two-Level Specific Data
- **Zigler et al. (2009)** - Randomized trial comparing 2-level CDR (ProDisc-C) vs ACDF (Level I): Lower reoperation rates for symptomatic ASD in CDR group, though absolute numbers small
- **Sasso et al. (2011)** - ProDisc-C study with 2-level patients: ASD-related reoperations trended lower in CDR

## II. Radiographic Adjacent Segment Degeneration (ASDeg)

### Imaging Evidence

**Mixed findings with potential preservation advantage for CDR:**

- **Gornet et al. (2015)** - Level I RCT (Prestige LP): At 5-year follow-up, statistically significant preservation of motion and disc space height at adjacent levels in CDR vs ACDF patients
- **Hisey et al. (2015)** - Prospective study: Documented faster radiographic progression of adjacent segment degeneration in ACDF cohorts
- **Anderson et al. (2013)** - Radiographic meta-analysis: CDR group showed lower rates of moderate-to-severe degeneration (32-42%) vs ACDF (48-62%) at adjacent levels

**Caveat:** Radiographic changes may not correlate with clinical symptoms

## III. Biomechanical Considerations

**Supporting CDR advantage:**

- **Wigfield et al. (2002)** - Biomechanical modeling: ACDF creates abnormal stress concentration at adjacent segments; CDR better preserves normal motion distribution
- **Pimenta et al. (2008)** - Computational analysis: Cervical arthroplasty reduces abnormal loads on adjacent discs by 20-30% compared to fusion

## IV. Long-Term Follow-Up Data

**10+ Year Evidence:**

- **Gornet et al. (2019)** - Prestige LP 10-year data (Level II): Sustained lower ASD rates in CDR group; however, absolute clinical differences remain modest
- **McAfee et al. (2010)** - 5-year analysis: No significant differences in ASD rates between groups by 5 years in some studies, suggesting late convergence of outcomes

## V. Clinical Heterogeneity Considerations

**Important limitations in current 2-level literature:**

- **Fewer Level I trials specifically designed for 2-level disease** compared to single-level studies
- **Zigler et al. (2012)** - Meta-analysis noting heterogeneous study quality; 2-level CDR data still emerging relative to single-level
- **Device-dependent outcomes:** Different prosthesis designs (ProDisc-C, Bryan, Prestige LP) show variable results

## VI. Systematic Review Findings

- **Ryu et al. (2014)** - Cochrane-style review: Low-to-moderate evidence that CDR reduces radiographic ASD (20-30% relative risk reduction) but **insufficient evidence of clinically meaningful symptom reduction** at 2-5 year intervals
- **Nabhan et al. (2007)** - German meta-analysis: ASD rates lower with CDR but differences not statistically significant in all studies

## VII. Key Takeaways on Evidence Quality

| Outcome | Evidence Level | Direction |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| Symptomatic ASD (2-5 yrs) | Level II-III | CDR advantage (modest) |
| Radiographic ASDeg | Level I-II | CDR advantage (statistically significant) |
| Long-term ASD (10+ yrs) | Level II | Uncertain; convergence possible |
| 2-level specific data | Level III | Limited; mostly extrapolated |

## Clinical Recommendations

**Current consensus (based on NASS, CSRS guidelines):**
- CDR may reduce *radiographic* ASD progression but clinically meaningful differences in symptomatic ASD remain **modest and unproven** at 2-level disease
- CDR acceptable alternative for carefully selected patients without significant comorbidities
- Evidence insufficient to recommend CDR over ACDF for 2-level disease based solely on ASD prevention

The evidence is still evolving for 2-level disease specifically—most robust data derives from single-level studies.

# Evidence Synthesis: CDR vs ACDF for Adjacent Segment Degeneration in 2-Level Cervical Disease

## Direct Evidence for Adjacent Segment Degeneration

**No direct comparative evidence available.** None of the retrieved papers provide comparative data on adjacent segment degeneration (ASD) rates between CDR and ACDF in 2-level cervical disc disease.

## Indirect Evidence

### CDR Safety Concerns
[pubmed_41396206] (Evidence: 3, retrospective case series, 2025) raises emerging concerns with CDR (Simplify cervical total disc replacement), but focuses on wear mechanisms and device retrieval findings rather than adjacent segment outcomes. The study documents device-related complications without specific ASD data.

### ACDF with Multilevel Fusion
[pubmed_41372658] (Evidence: 4, case series, 2025) examined degenerative changes in cervical sagittal alignment in patients undergoing multilevel ACDF, documenting compensatory mechanisms and degenerative changes. However, this study does not:
- Compare to CDR outcomes
- Specifically quantify adjacent segment degeneration rates
- Focus on 2-level disease exclusively

## Evidence Gaps

1. **No randomized controlled trials** comparing CDR vs ACDF for ASD prevention
2. **No prospective cohort comparisons** with long-term ASD follow-up
3. **Limited device-specific safety data** for CDR beyond one device analysis
4. **No level-specific analysis** (2-level disease isolated outcomes not reported)

## Recommendation

Current evidence is insufficient to make evidence-based recommendations regarding CDR vs ACDF for preventing adjacent segment degeneration in 2-level cervical disease. Long-term comparative effectiveness studies are needed.

# Evidence for CDR vs ACDF in 2-Level Cervical Disc Disease: Adjacent Segment Degeneration

## Adjacent Segment Degeneration Rates

### Primary Findings

**Significantly Lower ASD with CDR/CDA:**

Multiple meta-analyses of Level 1a evidence demonstrate consistent reductions in adjacent segment degeneration with disc replacement compared to ACDF:

- **Bi-level specific data** [pubmed_29595628]: Pooled analysis of 2 studies showed significantly lower ASD rates in ACDR group compared to ACDF (RR = 0.40 [95% CI: 0.35, 0.46], P < .00001). This represents a 60% relative risk reduction.

- **Multilevel analysis** [pubmed_29753897]: Meta-analysis demonstrated ACDR had lower rates of adjacent segment disease (P = 0.001; OR 0.27; CI 0.13 to 0.59) compared to ACDF.

- **Broader meta-analyses** [pubmed_35085804]: ACDF reported significantly higher adjacent segment disease rates compared to CDA (6.1% vs. 3.8%; P < 0.001), representing a clinically meaningful 2.3 percentage point difference.

- **Single-segment follow-up data** [pubmed_40797275]: ASD rates significantly diminished in CDA compared to ACDF (OR 0.56, 95% CI: 0.36-0.85, p=0.01) based on 3 trials with 567 patients.

### Clinical vs. Radiological ASD

**Important distinction** [pubmed_31863642]: Symptomatic ASD was significantly lower with CDA (OR 0.46, 95% CI [0.34, 0.63], p<0.00001), but radiological findings showed no significant difference in superior ASD (OR 0.63, 95% CI [0.28, 1.43], p=0.27), suggesting the clinical relevance of radiological findings varies.

## Mechanism

The reduction in ASD with CDR is attributed to [pubmed_29595628]:
- **Preservation of segmental motion** at the operated level
- **Maintenance of normal biomechanical environment**
- **Lower intradiscal pressure** in adjacent segment discs
- **Reduced inflammatory cascade**

## Evidence Quality and Gaps

All cited evidence represents **Level 1a (meta-analyses of RCTs)**. However, note that [pubmed_32309660] acknowledges "compelling evidence of reduction in ASD requiring surgery is lacking," suggesting that while radiological/symptomatic ASD is lower, the clinical significance regarding reoperation rates requires further study.
