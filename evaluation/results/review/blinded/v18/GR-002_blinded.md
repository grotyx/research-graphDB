# Shared and Unique Outcomes: TLIF vs PLIF

## Shared Outcomes Between TLIF and PLIF

Based on the available evidence, there is **limited direct comparison** between TLIF and PLIF in the provided papers. However, one study provides relevant information:

**Fusion Success and 2-Year Outcomes:**
A systematic review [pubmed_23646277] (Evidence: 1a) comparing fusion techniques reported that "the type of fusion, PLF or PLIF did not affect 2-year outcomes" in patients with isthmic spondylolisthesis. Additionally, "they reported no cases of pseudoarthrosis in either group" in a comparative study of PLF versus PLIF (n=11 per group). This suggests both interbody fusion approaches achieve comparable long-term fusion rates and clinical outcomes.

## Unique Outcomes by Procedure

### TLIF-Specific Outcomes
The papers provide extensive TLIF outcome data but do NOT directly compare TLIF-unique benefits to PLIF:

- **Complication profiles:** TLIF showed higher rates of dural tears and wound complications compared to LLIF [analyzed_ba35d6e5] (Evidence: 2b), though this comparison does not involve PLIF
- **Surgical approach variations:** Outcomes differ between endoscopic, biportal endoscopic, and minimally invasive TLIF approaches [pubmed_41420307] (Evidence: 1a)

### PLIF-Specific Outcomes
The provided papers contain **no outcome data specific to PLIF** that would allow differentiation from TLIF.

## Evidence Gaps

**Critical limitation:** The retrieved papers do NOT contain direct head-to-head comparisons of TLIF versus PLIF clinical outcomes. A comprehensive answer to this question would require additional literature specifically comparing these two approaches.

# Comparison of TLIF and PLIF Outcomes

## Shared Outcomes Between TLIF and PLIF

### Pain and Functional Disability Relief
Both procedures demonstrate effective pain and disability improvement. While specific comparative data between TLIF and PLIF directly is limited in the provided papers, both are included in fusion outcome analyses:

- **Fusion Rates at 24 Months**: PLIF/TLIF combined showed 95.7% fusion rate with rhBMP-2 and 89.5% without rhBMP-2 [pubmed_25955186] (Systematic Review, Evidence 1a)

- **Functional Improvement**: Both procedures are recognized as effective for treating lumbar degenerative diseases with symptom relief [pubmed_23646277] (Systematic Review, Evidence 1a)

- **Common Complications**: Both share similar complication profiles including subsidence, osteolysis, postoperative radiculopathy, heterotopic ossification, dural tear, implant migration, and intraoperative neurologic injury, with overall complication rates ranging from 8-80% (average 36.4%) [pubmed_23646277]

### Cost-Utility Profile
- **QALY Gains**: TLIF demonstrated the highest QALY gain at 1-year (0.43) [pubmed_39805471] (Systematic Review, Evidence 1a)

---

## Unique/Differential Outcomes

### PLIF-Specific Advantages
- **Superior to OLIF in some metrics**: When compared indirectly, OLIF showed significantly better pain relief (VAS −0.43; P=0.04) and functional disability (ODI −1.22; P=0.007) compared to PLIF [pubmed_37950267], suggesting PLIF may have relatively less optimal outcomes than newer techniques

### TLIF-Specific Findings
- **Segmental Lordosis Restoration**: OLIF demonstrated **superior** restoration of segmental lordosis compared with TLIF [pubmed_39575949] (Systematic Review, Evidence 1a), indicating TLIF is less effective at lordosis recovery
- **Endoscopic TLIF variants**: Minimally invasive TLIF variants (MIS-TLIF, FE-TLIF, BE-TLIF) show differential pain outcomes, with FE-TLIF and BE-TLIF significantly reducing back pain compared to MIS-TLIF [pubmed_41420307]

---

## Evidence Gaps

The provided papers do **not** include direct head-to-head TLIF vs. PLIF comparisons on:
- Adjacent segment degeneration rates
- Long-term (>2 year) functional outcomes
- Detailed radiographic alignment measures specific to each technique
- Patient satisfaction or return-to-work outcomes
