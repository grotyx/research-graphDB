# Outcomes of Three-Column Osteotomies for Fixed Sagittal Imbalance

## Radiological Correction Outcomes

### Magnitude of Correction
**Posterior Vertebral Column Resection (PVCR/VCR):**
PVCR demonstrated substantial and durable correction in thoracolumbar kyphosis:
- Local kyphosis reduction: **75.3° immediately postoperatively** (from 107.3° to 32.0°) [pubmed_40513242]
- Long-term durability: minimal loss of correction over mean **105.7-month follow-up** (only 5.1° increase from 32.0° to 37.1°) [pubmed_40513242]

**Three-Column Osteotomy (3CO) General:**
- 3CO approaches (including PSO and VCR variants) are recognized as necessary to achieve adequate correction for **severe and rigid spinal deformity** [pubmed_32382878]
- Meta-analysis examined plan-to-achieved fidelity and durability across multiple 3CO techniques (PSO, SPO, VCR) in studies from 2006-2025 [pubmed_41559490]

## Complication Profile

### Neurological Complications
**Modified PVCR vs Traditional PVCR:**
- Modified PVCR (with preservation of posterior ligamentous complex): **16.1% neurological complication rate** (5/31 patients)
- Traditional PVCR: **38.7% neurological complication rate** (12/31 patients)
- Absolute risk reduction: **22.6%**; Relative risk reduction: **58.4%** (p=0.046) [pubmed_40381021]

### General PSO Morbidity
**Posterior Subtraction Osteotomy (PSO):**
PSO is associated with **substantial intraoperative morbidity** including:
- High blood loss
- Neurological injury risk
- Prolonged operative times [pubmed_41497819]

### Comparative Safety Data
- Alternative approaches like **single-position navigated lateral column realignment with anterior ligament release** were developed as potentially safer alternatives to traditional 3CO, particularly in elderly patients (septuagenarians) [pubmed_40968391]
- **Halo-pelvic traction (HPT)** combined with posterior fusion was proposed as a **safer alternative** to pVCR that may reduce the need for high-grade osteotomy by partial preoperative correction [pubmed_41106603]

## Quality of Life Outcomes

### Functional Improvement
**Combined Anterior-Posterior Approach (LLIF+PSF vs PSO alone):**
- LLIF+PSF group achieved significantly:
  - **Lower ODI scores**
  - **Higher SRS-22 self-image/total scores**
  - **Greater achievement of minimal clinically important difference for SRS-22 pain score** [pubmed_29087808]

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Limited PSO-specific outcome data**: While PSO is described as the "gold standard" for powerful sagittal corrections [pubmed_41497819], specific correction magnitudes and complication rates for PSO alone are not detailed in the retrieved papers
2. **Heterogeneous study designs**: Evidence comes from meta-analyses [pubmed_41497819, pubmed_32382878, pubmed_41559490] and comparative studies (2b level) with varying methodologies
3. **Long-term follow-up**: While PVCR data extends to 105.7 months, comparative long-term durability data between PSO and VCR is not provided
4. **Patient-specific factors**: Limited stratification by age, deformity severity, or rigidity for predicting outcomes

# Outcomes of Three-Column Osteotomies for Fixed Sagittal Imbalance

## Radiological Outcomes

### Sagittal Correction Capability
Three-column osteotomies (3-CO), particularly PSO, demonstrate superior correction compared to anterior-only approaches:

- **PSO vs. ACR alone**: Addition of PSO to ACR increased segmental lordosis correction by **72.7%** compared to ACR alone (p < 0.001) [pubmed_25820352], demonstrating that PSO provides significantly greater correction for rigid deformity [pubmed_32382878]

- **Evidence quality**: A meta-analysis [pubmed_41497819] (Evidence: 1a) included three prospective/retrospective clinical studies (2017-2023) and two biomechanical simulation studies (2022, 2024) comparing correction techniques, with lumbar lordosis measurements and complication rates reported across studies with follow-up periods ranging heterogeneously

## Complication Profile

### Overall Complication Rates
In septuagenarians undergoing 3-CO for thoracolumbar kyphosis [pubmed_40968391] (Evidence: 2b):
- **Total complications**: 28 complications in 19 patients (**35.2%**)
- Complications exclusive to 3CO included: surgical site infection (5.6%), hematoma (1.9%), and DVT [pubmed_40968391]

### Neurological Complications
- **New-onset neurological deficits**: 5 cases (9.3%) in 3CO group, all classified as Frankel C, with 3 improving to Frankel D after 2-year rehabilitation [pubmed_40968391]

## Evidence Gaps

- Limited data specifically stratified for PSO vs. VCR outcomes
- Heterogeneous follow-up periods across included studies [pubmed_41497819]
- Systematic review of osteotomy approaches [pubmed_38765779] (Evidence: 4) provided descriptive synthesis but limited outcome quantification
- Long-term HRQOL data synthesis available but specific metric details not fully detailed in retrieved papers [pubmed_32382878]
