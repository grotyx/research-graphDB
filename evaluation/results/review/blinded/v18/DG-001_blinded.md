# Evidence Comparison: UBE versus Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## ODI (Oswestry Disability Index) Improvement

**Limited Direct Comparison Available:**

One study [pubmed_41290735] (Evidence: 2b, retrospective comparative) reported that both uniportal and unilateral biportal endoscopic fusion are **superior to MIS-TLIF in terms of early postoperative low back pain relief**, particularly within 3 months post-surgery. However, this study noted **"no significant difference in early leg pain relief and functional improvement"** between endoscopic techniques and MIS-TLIF. Critically, the authors acknowledge **"at present, there is a shortage of cross-comparison research between the two techniques."**

The meta-analysis by [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) comparing conventional open surgery to biportal endoscopy found ODI improvements favoring biportal endoscopy, but this compares open surgery rather than TLIF specifically.

## Complication Rates

**Comprehensive Evidence Unavailable:**

The retrieved literature does not provide direct comparative complication rates between UBE and conventional TLIF. The meta-analysis [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) analyzed complication rates between conventional open surgery and biportal endoscopy across 414 open and 383 biportal procedures, but conventional open surgery is not equivalent to TLIF outcomes.

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Direct RCT comparison lacking** between UBE and conventional TLIF for lumbar stenosis
2. **Functional outcome data limited** - most studies compare UBE to MIS-TLIF rather than standard TLIF
3. **Complication rate comparisons** specifically for UBE versus conventional TLIF not available in provided literature

**Recommendation:** High-quality RCTs directly comparing these techniques are needed for definitive conclusions.

# Evidence Summary: UBE vs Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## ODI (Functional Outcome) Improvement

**Comparable Long-term Outcomes with Early Functional Advantages for UBE:**

[pubmed_41420307] - Network meta-analysis (Evidence 1a): BE-TLIF and FE-TLIF demonstrated **superior clinical efficacy compared to MIS-TLIF**, offering "improved short-term functional outcomes" and "significantly greater back pain reduction." However, specific ODI numeric data were not provided in the excerpt.

[pubmed_41559891] - Meta-analysis of UBE vs biportal endoscopy (Evidence 1a): Biportal endoscopic surgery offered **improved early functional recovery (lower ODI at 1-3 months) which persisted at final follow-up**, though "this advantage did not consistently achieve minimal clinically important difference thresholds."

[pubmed_40797374] - Biportal vs open surgery meta-analysis (Evidence 1b): Achieved **comparable immediate and long-term postoperative outcomes with conventional open spine surgery** for lumbar degenerative disease.

---

## Complication Rates

**Limited Direct Comparison Data:**

The retrieved papers provide minimal direct complication rate comparisons between UBE-TLIF and conventional TLIF specifically.

[pubmed_38752236] - Prospective comparison of UBE-TLIF vs Endo-TLIF (Evidence 1b): Noted that "UBE-TLIF requires further improvement in minimally invasive techniques to reduce tissue trauma and blood loss" compared to Endo-TLIF, suggesting UBE may have higher tissue trauma profile, though specific complication rates were not detailed in the excerpt.

[pubmed_41354742] - MI tubular decompression vs open laminectomy (Evidence 1a): MI approaches showed **lower overall complication rates (OR 0.42, 95% CI 0.21–0.86)** and **reduced surgical site infections (OR 0.28, 95% CI 0.11–0.69)** compared to open surgery, providing indirect evidence for minimally invasive advantage.

---

## Evidence Gaps

1. **No head-to-head RCT data**: The only identified protocol is [pubmed_39322595], a planned prospective RCT comparing UBE-TLIF vs MT-TLIF (not conventional open TLIF)—results not yet published.

2. **Lack of specific complication rate numerics**: Direct complication comparisons between UBE-TLIF and conventional TLIF are not quantified in the provided papers.

3. **ODI improvement magnitude**: Absolute ODI improvement values and clinically important differences between techniques are not clearly specified.
