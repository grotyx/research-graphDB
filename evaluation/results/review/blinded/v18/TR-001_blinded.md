# Evidence Summary: Short-Segment vs Long-Segment Fixation for Thoracolumbar Burst Fractures

## Radiographic Outcomes

**Long-segment instrumentation shows superior radiographic correction:**
- Long-segment fixation demonstrated better preservation of local kyphosis, sagittal index, and anterior vertebral height compared to short-segment fixation (p < 0.05) [pubmed_16306834, Level 2b]

**However, long-term kyphosis progression differs:**
- A meta-analysis found no significant difference in kyphosis correction or implant failure rates between short-segment and long-segment fixations at final follow-up, though both showed kyphosis progression over time [pubmed_39560536, Level 4]
- A 20-year retrospective study found NO kyphotic progression with short-segment posterior instrumentation and fusion, suggesting proper surgical technique may minimize progression [pubmed_39560536, Level 4]

**Monosegmental vs short-segment fixation:**
- Monosegmental transpedicular fixation achieved comparable radiologic outcomes to short-segment instrumentation, including equivalent load-sharing classification index, sagittal deformity correction, and anterior vertebral body height restoration [pubmed_20535041, Level 2b]

## Clinical Outcomes

**Functional outcomes are equivalent:**
- Low Back Outcome Score was equivalent between short-segment and long-segment groups despite radiographic differences [pubmed_16306834, Level 2b]

**Short same-segment fixation shows promising results:**
- 36% complete neurological recovery (Frankel E), 30.93% kyphosis reduction, 15% vertebral body height improvement in 25 patients [pubmed_30152769, Level 2b]

## Operative Morbidity

**Short-segment fixation advantages:**
- Associated with shorter operation times and reduced intraoperative blood loss compared to fusion approaches (p = 0.001 and p < 0.001 respectively) [pubmed_39089628, Level 2a]
- Requires less operative time and blood loss than long-segment fixation [pubmed_16306834, Level 2b]

**Short-segment fixation complications:**
- Associated with 55% failure rate [pubmed_16306834, Level 2b]

## Special Consideration: Neurologically Intact Fractures

**Short-segment fixation without fusion recommended:**
- For neurologically intact thoracolumbar burst fractures, short-segment fixation without fusion demonstrated superior outcomes compared to fusion approaches, with shorter operation times and reduced blood loss [pubmed_39089628, Level 2a]

## Evidence Gaps

- Limited long-term follow-up data in comparative studies [pubmed_35451255, Level 2a]
- Absence of long-term outcomes documentation in most comparative trials [pubmed_35451255, Level 2a]
- Most evidence is from Level 2-4 studies; highest-level evidence (Level 1a systematic review) [pubmed_40489858] was retrieved but specific outcomes not detailed in excerpt provided

## Clinical Synthesis

Short-segment fixation appears appropriate for most thoracolumbar burst fractures, offering equivalent functional outcomes to long-segment fixation with reduced operative morbidity, though long-segment fixation provides superior immediate radiographic correction. For neurologically intact patients, avoiding fusion appears beneficial.

# Evidence Summary: Short-Segment vs Long-Segment Fixation in Thoracolumbar Burst Fractures

## Overall Evidence Quality
This comparison is supported by **Level 1a-2b evidence** from systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and comparative studies.

---

## CLINICAL OUTCOMES

### Pain and Functional Disability
**Short-segment fixation shows comparable functional outcomes:**
- Functional outcomes measured by Low Back Outcome Score were **equivalent between SSF and LSF groups** [pubmed_16306834] (Level 2b)
- VAS pain scores improved significantly (8.8±0.9 → 1.4±0.4 at final follow-up) with combined balloon kyphoplasty and pedicle screw fixation [pubmed_34353039] (Level 2b)
- At 20-year follow-up: 100% of patients achieved good pain control; 83.3% had acceptable work capacity [pubmed_39560536] (Level 4)

### Quality of Life and Sexual Function
**SSF demonstrates superior outcomes in patient-reported measures:**
- Short-segment fixation showed **better quality of life and sexual function in both men and their female partners** compared to LSF in thoracolumbar junction burst fractures [pubmed_26202101] (Level 1b)

### Neurological Recovery
- All 24 patients with incomplete neurologic deficit achieved **complete neurological recovery** with combined balloon kyphoplasty and posterior pedicle screw fixation [pubmed_34353039] (Level 2b)

---

## RADIOGRAPHIC OUTCOMES

### Kyphosis Preservation
**LSF demonstrates superior radiographic results:**
- Long-segment instrumentation showed **superior radiographic outcomes with better preservation of local kyphosis, sagittal index, and anterior vertebral height** (p < 0.05) [pubmed_16306834] (Level 2b)

### Fusion and Early Bone Healing
**SSF including fracture level shows accelerated early healing:**
- Short-segment fixation **including fractured level achieved superior early fusion outcomes and accelerated clinical recovery** compared to LSF in acute thoracolumbar burst fractures [pubmed_28803178] (Level 1b)
- However, **both approaches resulted in equivalent 2-year clinical and radiologic outcomes**, suggesting early advantages of SSF do not persist long-term [pubmed_28803178] (Level 1b)

---

## SURGICAL MORBIDITY & OPERATIVE OUTCOMES

### Operative Time and Blood Loss
**SSF offers clear surgical advantages:**
- SSF without fusion demonstrated **shorter operation times (p = 0.001) and reduced intraoperative blood loss (p < 0.001)** compared to fusion approaches [pubmed_39089628] (Level 2a)
- SSF required **less operative time and blood loss** than LSF [pubmed_16306834] (Level 2b)

### Motion Segment Preservation
**SSF preserves more spinal motion:**
- SSF allows **saving of two or more vertebral motion segments** compared to LSF [pubmed_33830133] (Level 2b)
- SSF involves **fewer mobile segments during fixation** [pubmed_40775716] (Level 1a)

---

## COMPLICATIONS & FAILURE RATES

### Instrumentation Failure
**SSF has higher failure rates:**
- Short-segment fixation was associated with a **55% failure rate** [pubmed_16306834] (Level 2b)
- LSF has advantages of **better fixed strength and dispersed stress, reducing the possibility of instrument failure and collapse of injured vertebra** [pubmed_40489858] (Level 1a)

### Adjacent Segment Degeneration
- LSF results in **more surgical trauma, sacrifices more spinal motion segments, and accelerates adjacent disc degeneration** [pubmed_40489858] (Level 1a)
- SSF offers **reduction of stress in adjacent segments** [pubmed_40489858] (Level 1a)

---

## OPTIMAL SURGICAL TECHNIQUE

### SSF Including Fracture Level
**Current evidence supports inclusion of fracture level in SSF:**
- **Good radiological and clinical outcomes** can be achieved by including the fracture level in short-segment fixation [pubmed_33830133] (Level 2b)
- This approach achieves **superior early fusion outcomes** while preserving motion segments [pubmed_28803178] (Level 1b)

### Fusion vs Non-Fusion Approach
- **SSF without fusion demonstrated superior outcomes** compared to monosegmental, short-segment, and long-segment fusion approaches in neurologically intact fractures [pubmed_39089628] (Level 2a)

---

## CURRENT CLINICAL PRACTICE

- Short-segment posterior fixation **represents the most popular surgical approach among spine surgeons** for thoracolumbar burst fractures [pubmed_35451255] (Level 2a)
- However, **evidence supporting this preference remains limited by short-term follow-up data and absence of long-term outcomes documentation** [pubmed_35451255] (Level 2a)

---

## EVIDENCE GAPS

1. **Long-term follow-up**: Most studies lack adequate long-term data beyond 2 years
2. **AO type specificity**: Limited evidence stratified specifically for A3-A4 fracture types
3. **Failure predictors**: While factors linked to PSSI failure have been identified [pubmed_40775716], specific thresholds for SSF vs LSF selection in severe fractures remain undefined
4. **Decompression strategies**: Limited evidence on combined decompression and fixation approaches for neurologically involved fractures [pubmed_35451255]

---

## SUMMARY RECOMMENDATION

**Short-segment fixation (including fracture level) appears to be the preferred approach for thoracolumbar burst fractures** based on superior early recovery, reduced morbidity, and preserved quality of life—with equivalent 2-year outcomes to LSF. However, LSF should be considered for fractures with severe kyphotic deformity or high risk factors for SSF failure. **Patient-centered factors (quality of life, sexual function) should guide surgical selection.**
