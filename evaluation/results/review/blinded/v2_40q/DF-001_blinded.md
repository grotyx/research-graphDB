# Proximal Junctional Kyphosis and Failure in Adult Spinal Deformity Surgery: Incidence, Risk Factors, and Prevention

## INCIDENCE

### Proximal Junctional Failure
- **Acute incidence: 5.6%** with approximately **41% of patients requiring reoperation** [pubmed_41379878]

### Proximal Junctional Kyphosis
- Represents **one of the most frequent complications** after adult spinal deformity surgery [pubmed_41379878]
- Specific incidence rates vary by risk factor profiles and prevention strategies (see risk factors section)

---

## PREVENTIVE STRATEGIES

### Posterior Ligamentous Augmentation (PLA)

**Systematic Review and Meta-analysis (Evidence: 1a)** [pubmed_39973129]

| Outcome | Effect Size | 95% CI | Risk Reduction |
|---------|------------|--------|-----------------|
| PJK Development | OR 0.54 | 0.34-0.85 | 46% |
| PJF Requiring Revision | OR 0.23 | 0.12-0.47 | 77% |

- Benefits maintained over follow-up period of **17.6 to 31.2 months** [pubmed_39973129]
- Minimal heterogeneity between studies for PJF outcomes [pubmed_39973129]

### Surgical Alignment Optimization

#### Avoiding Overcorrection
- **Overcorrected group (O) vs. Undercorrected group (U):** PJK was significantly more frequent in overcorrected patients (p = 0.05) [pubmed_37740115]
- **Age-adjusted PI-LL alignment targets:** Overcorrection beyond age-adjusted pelvic incidence minus lumbar lordosis targets increases PJF risk [pubmed_41167281]
- Normative segmental lordosis correction prevents both PJF (associated with overcorrection) and implant failure (associated with undercorrection) [pubmed_38270393]

#### Transverse Process Hooks vs. Pedicle Screws at UIV
- A retrospective comparative study examined transverse process hooks versus pedicle screws at the upper instrumented vertebra in three-column osteotomy patients [pubmed_41297090]
- No specific quantitative outcomes reported in abstract; full data suggests differential PJK/PJF rates but exact figures not provided

---

## RISK FACTORS

### Baseline Thoracolumbar Inflection Point Location

**Prospective Analysis** [pubmed_40450887]

| Factor | Odds Ratio | 95% CI | p-value |
|--------|-----------|--------|---------|
| More caudal baseline inflection point (L2 inferior vs. L1 body) | 1.26 | 1.08-1.46 | 0.003 |

- Patients with more caudal baseline inflection point had **25% higher odds of developing PJF by 2 years** [pubmed_40450887]
- Development of PJF/PJK with reoperation was associated with more caudal baseline inflection point (p < 0.001) [pubmed_40450887]

### L1 Vertebra Orientation
- Postoperative orientation of L1 vertebra is associated with PJK occurrence; however, the relationship was not previously well-characterized in literature [pubmed_40521654]

### Three-Column Osteotomy
- Patients undergoing major sagittal correction through three-column osteotomies are at particularly high risk for PJK and PJF [pubmed_41297090]
- These complications often require revision surgery [pubmed_41297090]

### Additional Risk Factors Under Investigation
- **Comprehensive IPW analysis:** Advanced statistical evaluation of PJK/PJF risk factors using inverse probability weighting [pubmed_40153849]
- **Machine learning prediction:** Multicenter validation study for PJK prediction in long-segment fusion for degenerative scoliosis [pubmed_41379878]
- **Surgical planning tools:** Validation of four preoperative surgical planning tools with impact on PJK development at ≥2-year follow-up [pubmed_36999928]

---

## CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

- Both PJK and PJF **reduce quality of life** and **significantly increase revision rates and healthcare costs** [pubmed_41379878]
- PJF involves vertebral fracture at upper instrumented vertebra (UIV) or UIV+1, screw pullout, adjacent segment subluxation, or neurological deficits [pubmed_41379878]

---

## EVIDENCE GAPS

1. Specific quantitative outcomes for transverse process hooks versus pedicle screws strategy not fully detailed [pubmed_41297090]
2. Machine learning and comprehensive IPW analyses appear to be in progress with results pending [pubmed_41379878, pubmed_40153849]
3. Role of L1 orientation requires further characterization [pubmed_40521654]
4. Comparative effectiveness of different surgical planning tools not quantified in abstracts provided [pubmed_36999928]

# Incidence, Risk Factors, and Prevention of PJK/PJF After Adult Spinal Deformity Surgery

## INCIDENCE

### Proximal Junctional Kyphosis (PJK)
- **17-61.7%** of cases develop PJK following ASD surgery [pubmed_39973129] (systematic review/meta-analysis, Evidence 1a)

### Proximal Junctional Failure (PJF)
- **5-20%** of patients progress to PJF requiring revision surgery [pubmed_39973129] (Evidence 1a)
- **5.6%** acute incidence of PJF, with **41% of patients requiring reoperation** [pubmed_41379878] (training-validation study, Evidence not rated)
- Reported range varies **5.6-35%** due to heterogeneous definitions and patient populations [pubmed_40099853] (biomechanical analysis, Evidence 5)

---

## RISK FACTORS

### Non-Modifiable Patient Factors
- **Older age** (>55, >65, >70 years) [pubmed_40033732] (comprehensive review, Evidence 4)
- **Female sex** [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)
- **Comorbidities and higher ASA grade** [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)

### Modifiable Patient Factors
- **High BMI** (median 25.6 kg/m²) [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)
- **Sarcopenia**: Paraspinal sarcopenia and lower Hounsfield units are **independent predictors of increased risk** for proximal junctional complications [pmid_39091148] (retrospective cohort, Evidence 2b)
- **Osteopenia/osteoporosis** [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)

### Radiographic Risk Factors
- **More caudal baseline thoracolumbar inflection point** (L2 inferior endplate vs L1 body):
  - Associated with **25% higher odds of developing PJF by 2 years** (OR 1.26 [95% CI 1.08-1.46], p = 0.003) [pubmed_40450887] (retrospective comparative study, Evidence 2b)
  - p < 0.001 for association with PJF/PJK with reoperation [pubmed_40450887] (Evidence 2b)

- **Postoperative L1 vertebra orientation**: L1 vertebra orientation is associated with PJK occurrence, though specific risk thresholds require further study [pubmed_40521654] (retrospective cohort, Evidence 2b)

### Surgical Factors
- **Major sagittal correction through three-column osteotomies** increases PJK/PJF risk [pubmed_41297090] (comparative study, Evidence 2b)

---

## PREVENTIVE STRATEGIES

### Bone Quality Optimization
- **Teriparatide and/or denosumab** before/after surgery (Grade B recommendation) [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)
- **Bisphosphonates** (Grade C recommendation) [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)
- **Cement augmentation** [pubmed_40033732] (Evidence 4)

### Instrumentation Techniques
- **Transverse process hooks versus pedicle screws at UIV**: Comparative effectiveness in reducing PJK/PJF in three-column osteotomy patients requires further investigation [pubmed_41297090] (Evidence 2b)
- **Novel tether pedicle screws**: Biomechanical analysis suggests potential benefit for proximal junctional mechanics in long-segment thoracolumbar instrumentation [pubmed_40099853] (Evidence 5)

### Posterior Ligamentous Augmentation
- **Systematic review and meta-analysis** confirms posterior ligamentous augmentation as a preventive strategy for PJK and PJF in ASD surgery [pubmed_39973129] (Evidence 1a)

### Preoperative Surgical Planning
- **Utilization of validated surgical planning tools** considering alignment parameters (pelvic incidence, lumbar lordosis) impacts PJK development rates [pubmed_36999928] (retrospective cohort, Evidence 2b)

---

## CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

PJK and PJF represent significant sources of patient morbidity, reducing quality of life and substantially increasing **revision rates and healthcare costs** [pubmed_41379878, pubmed_39973129] (Evidence 1a, variable quality)

---

## EVIDENCE GAPS

- Limited high-quality randomized controlled trials comparing specific preventive interventions
- Heterogeneous definitions of PJF across studies affect incidence comparisons
- Specific optimal thresholds for L1 vertebra orientation not yet established
- Comparative effectiveness of instrumentation techniques (hooks vs. screws vs. novel devices) requires higher-level evidence
