# Evidence Synthesis: Vertebroplasty/Kyphoplasty vs Conservative Management for Osteoporotic Vertebral Compression Fractures

## Important Limitation
**The provided papers do NOT contain direct comparative evidence between vertebral augmentation techniques and conservative management.** This is a critical gap that prevents answering the clinical question as posed.

---

## What the Evidence Shows

### Pain Relief (Vertebral Augmentation - No Conservative Comparator)

**Immediate/Short-term outcomes:**
- **PVP (Percutaneous Vertebroplasty):** VAS decrease median 6.0 points immediately postoperative [pubmed_41816215, Evidence 2b]
- **PKP (Percutaneous Kyphoplasty):** VAS decrease median 6.0 points immediately postoperative, with superior pain reduction at 6 months (mean reduction 5.5±1.8) in high-risk patients [pubmed_41576219, Evidence 2b]
- **General statement:** "Significant pain relief" reported at short-term and final follow-up [pubmed_41709492, Evidence 1a - systematic review]

**Clinical context on conservative management risks:**
Conservative treatment "typically necessitates prolonged bed rest, which can lead to various complications, such as pressure ulcers, thrombosis, pneumonia, exacerbated calcium loss, and nonunion of fractures" [pubmed_41650074, Evidence 2b]. This suggests vertebroplasty is preferred in elderly patients due to "minimal invasiveness and rapid recovery."

### Kyphosis Correction

**PKP demonstrates superior kyphosis correction compared to PVP** (not vs. conservative care):
- PKP: CA correction -4.67° ± 0.32° immediate postoperative
- PVP: CA correction -3.02° ± 0.17° immediate postoperative
- Difference: p=0.007 [pubmed_41816215, Evidence 2b]

### Adjacent-Level Disease and Recurrent Fractures

The evidence addresses **repeat procedures** for adjacent-level fractures but not prevention rates vs. conservative management:
- "Repeat vertebroplasty and balloon kyphoplasty offer substantial and durable pain reduction for patients with recurrent or adjacent-level osteoporotic vertebral compression fractures" [pubmed_41797828, Evidence 1a meta-analysis]
- Complication rates comparable to primary procedures

---

## Evidence Gaps

1. **No randomized controlled trials comparing augmentation vs. conservative management** were provided
2. **No data on kyphosis progression rates** comparing treatments
3. **No adjacent fracture incidence rates** comparing augmentation vs. conservative care
4. **Functional outcomes (ODI)** reported for augmentation only: immediate ODI decrease median 39.7-39.8 points, no conservative comparator [pubmed_41816215]

---

## Conclusion

While the papers support vertebral augmentation for pain relief and kyphosis correction, **direct evidence comparing these techniques to conservative management is absent from the provided literature.** A systematic review addressing this comparison would be needed to adequately answer the clinical question.

# Evidence Synthesis: Vertebroplasty/Kyphoplasty vs Conservative Management for OVCF

## CRITICAL LIMITATION
**The provided papers do NOT include any direct comparisons between vertebral augmentation (vertebroplasty/kyphoplasty) and conservative management.** All retrieved papers compare vertebroplasty to kyphoplasty, evaluate timing of surgery, analyze technique modifications, or examine risk factors—but none include a conservative treatment control group.

---

## Available Evidence on Vertebral Augmentation Efficacy

### Pain Relief (Quantified Outcomes)

| Study | Design | N | Intervention | Baseline VAS | Follow-up VAS | Change | Timeline |
|-------|--------|---|--------------|--------------|---------------|--------|----------|
| [pubmed_41576219] | Prospective Phase 3 | 112 (PKP) | PKP | 7.8±1.2 | 2.3±1.5 | -5.5±1.8 | 6 months |
| [pubmed_41576219] | Prospective Phase 3 | 94 (PVP) | PVP | 7.5±1.3 | 3.1±1.8 | -4.4±2.1 | 6 months |
| [pubmed_41808498] | Comparative study | PCKP | PCKP | 6.35±2.34 | 0.97±0.80 | -5.38±2.14 | 12 months |
| [pubmed_34353039] | Combined approach | 24 | PKP + posterior fixation | 8.8±0.9 | 1.4±0.4 | -7.4±0.9 | Final FU |

**PKP vs PVP Pain Comparison:** PKP achieved superior pain reduction (difference 5.5 vs 4.4 points; p=0.018, 95% CI reported) at 6 months [pubmed_41576219].

### Functional Disability Outcomes

[pubmed_41576219] reported comparable immediate functional disability reduction between interventions with ODI decrease of approximately 39.8 points [pubmed_41816215].

[pubmed_34353039] demonstrated ODI improvement from 88.6%±3.5% preoperatively to 17.5%±1.8% at final follow-up (p<0.05) [pubmed_34353039].

### Kyphosis Correction and Maintenance

[pubmed_41816215] states that "PVP's superiority in maintaining long-term deformity correction and clinical outcomes at 2-year follow-up, particularly when bone cement distribution is optimized" [pubmed_41816215], but **specific kyphotic angle values are not provided in the abstracts/retrieved text**.

### Adjacent-Level Fracture Risk

[pubmed_41767384] examined risk factors for subsequent vertebral fracture (SVF) in postmenopausal women following PVP or PKP:
- **Control group (n=455):** postmenopausal women, mean age 69.79±8.29 years, BMI 23.28±3.05 kg/m²
- **SVF group (n=73):** those who developed subsequent fractures, mean age 72.78±8.09 years, BMI 21.99±2.76 kg/m²

**Significant risk factors identified (p-values from univariate analysis):**
- Age (p=0.005)
- BMI (p=0.001)
- Previous fracture history (p<0.001)
- Bone cement volume (p=0.029)
- Preoperative DMVC (p=0.009)
- HU value (p<0.001)

**Notably, no difference in procedure type was identified**, suggesting both PVP and PKP carry similar adjacent-level fracture risk [pubmed_41767384].

### Repeat/Adjacent-Level Vertebral Augmentation Safety

[pubmed_41797828] meta-analysis reports that repeat vertebral augmentation demonstrates "favorable safety profile with complication rates comparable to primary vertebral augmentation procedures," though **specific complication rate percentages are not provided** [pubmed_41797828].

### Neurological Outcomes (Combined Approach)

[pubmed_34353039] reported that "all 24 patients with incomplete neurologic deficit achieved complete neurological recovery during the follow-up period" following PKP combined with posterior pedicle screw fixation [pubmed_34353039].

---

## Timing of Intervention

[pubmed_41650074] retrospective analysis of early vs delayed curved-angle PKP:
- **Early treatment (≤14 days)** associated with: greater pain relief, functional improvement, restoration of vertebral height, correction of kyphotic angle, and **reduced complication rates**
- Study limitations acknowledged: "retrospective and observational nature...causal inferences cannot be drawn" [pubmed_41650074]

---

## GAPS IN EVIDENCE

1. **No conservative management comparison:** All papers examine surgical techniques in isolation or compare procedures to each other
2. **No control/sham group data:** The primary clinical question cannot be answered from these papers
3. **Missing absolute complication rates:** [pubmed_41797828] states complications are "comparable" but provides no specific percentages
4. **Incomplete kyphotic angle data:** While kyphosis correction is mentioned as a benefit, specific angular measurements and maintenance are not detailed in retrieved abstracts
5. **No long-term conservative outcome data:** Studies do not report natural history or conservative treatment trajectories

---

## CONCLUSION

**The provided evidence cannot directly answer the clinical question** regarding vertebral augmentation versus conservative management. The papers demonstrate that vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty achieve substantial pain relief (4.4-7.4 point VAS reduction) and functional improvement, with PKP showing superiority over PVP for pain relief (p=0.018) [pubmed_41576219]. However, **evidence comparing these interventions to conservative management is entirely absent from the retrieved literature.** To answer the posed question, studies with conservative control arms would be required.
