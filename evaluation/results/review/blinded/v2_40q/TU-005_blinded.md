# Surgical Outcomes and Prognostic Factors for Intramedullary Spinal Cord Tumors

## Gross Total Resection (GTR) Rates by Histology

**Overall GTR Achievement:**
- Overall GTR rate: 73.1-76.67% across studies [pubmed_40647411, pubmed_38608817] (Evidence: 2b, retrospective)

**By Tumor Histology:**

*Ependymoma:*
- GTR achieved in 82.5% of cases [pubmed_40647411]
- En-bloc resection in 16.7% of ependymal tumors [pubmed_40647411]

*Astrocytoma:*
- GTR achieved in only 55.6% of cases [pubmed_40647411]
- Zero en-bloc resections in astrocytic tumors [pubmed_40647411]
- Difference between ependymal and astrocytic tumors was statistically significant (p < 0.001) [pubmed_40647411]

*Hemangioblastoma:*
- Highest GTR rate at 96.4% [pubmed_40647411]
- En-bloc resection most common at 53.6% [pubmed_40647411]

---

## Survival Outcomes: GTR vs. Subtotal Resection (STR)

**Pediatric Astrocytoma Population:**
- 5-year survival in astrocytoma subgroup (n=198): 72.1% (95% CI: 65.8-77.4%) [pubmed_41653291]
- **No significant survival difference between GTR and STR** (HR 0.88, 95% CI 0.52-1.49, p = 0.63) [pubmed_41653291] (Evidence: 2b, SEER database analysis)
- Extent of surgical resection did **not significantly predict survival outcomes** (log-rank p = 0.42) [pubmed_41653291]

---

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Pediatric vs. Adult Outcomes:** GTR survival benefit data lacking for adult astrocytomas and ependymomas
2. **Ependymoma-Specific Outcomes:** No direct survival comparison data between GTR and STR for ependymomas
3. **Functional Outcomes:** Limited data on neurological morbidity trade-offs between aggressive resection and STR
4. **Long-term Follow-up:** Unclear if short-term lack of survival benefit translates to long-term equivalence

# Surgical Outcomes and Prognostic Factors for Intramedullary Spinal Cord Tumors

## I. GROSS TOTAL RESECTION RATES BY HISTOLOGY

### Ependymoma
- **GTR rate: 82.5%** in a cohort of ependymal tumors [pubmed_40647411]
- **Pediatric ependymoma (n=156): 5-year survival 85.2% (95% CI: 79.4-89.7%)** [pubmed_41653291]

### Astrocytoma
- **GTR rate: 55.6%** - significantly lower than ependymoma [pubmed_40647411]

### Overall Cohort
- **GTR achieved in 76.67% (46/60 patients)** [pubmed_38608817]
- **STR/partial resection in 23.33% (14/60 patients)** where complete removal was not feasible or would cause unacceptable neurologic morbidity [pubmed_38608817]

---

## II. SURVIVAL OUTCOMES BY RESECTION EXTENT

### GTR vs. STR/No Resection (Ependymoma)
GTR demonstrated significantly superior outcomes in pediatric ependymomas:
- **Log-rank p = 0.008** (statistically significant survival difference) [pubmed_41653291]
- **Multivariable analysis: HR 0.050 (95% CI: 0.008-0.312, p = 0.015)** - GTR was protective
- **95% reduction in mortality risk with GTR** [pubmed_41653291]

### Long-term Survival by Histology

**Ependymoma:**
- **20-year survival: 65.4%** [pubmed_40647411]
- Recurrence rate: **14.4%** [pubmed_40647411]

**Astrocytoma:**
- **Lower 20-year survival** (specific percentage not stated) [pubmed_40647411]
- Recurrence rate: **43.8%** - three-fold higher than ependymoma [pubmed_40647411]

---

## III. KEY PROGNOSTIC FACTORS

### Tumor Histology and Grade
**Tumor histology and WHO grade are the most significant predictors of survival and neurological outcomes** [pubmed_40647411]

### Resection Completeness
GTR emerged as an independent protective factor in multivariable analysis for pediatric intramedullary tumors [pubmed_41653291]

## V. FUNCTIONAL AND NEUROLOGICAL OUTCOMES

**Short-term outcomes (3 months post-op):**
In a mixed cohort (en bloc vs. separation surgery for plasmacytoma):
- Neurological symptom improvement: **statistically significant (p<0.05)** [pubmed_41633419]
- Performance status improvement: **statistically significant (p<0.05)** [pubmed_41633419]
- Pain relief: **statistically significant (p<0.05)** [pubmed_41633419]

---

## VI. COMPLICATIONS AND RISK FACTORS

### Spinal Deformity
In pediatric low-grade intramedullary spinal cord tumors:
- Identified as a **common and significant complication**
- **Risk factors: preoperative deformity, thoracic location, and syrinx presence** [pubmed_41546829]

---

## VII. GAPS IN THE EVIDENCE

1. Specific long-term survival rates for astrocytoma not quantified
2. Direct comparison of neurological outcomes (morbidity rates) between GTR and STR not provided across studies
3. Complication rates and morbidity metrics not systematically reported
4. No prospective randomized controlled trials (all studies are retrospective cohort analysis, Evidence level 2b)

---

## CONCLUSION

**GTR is significantly associated with improved survival in ependymomas** (HR 0.050, p=0.015, representing 95% mortality reduction) [pubmed_41653291], with ependymomas achieving GTR in 82.5% of cases versus only 55.6% in astrocytomas [pubmed_40647411]. Tumor histology and WHO grade are the primary prognostic drivers, with ependymomas demonstrating substantially better outcomes than astrocytomas regardless of resection extent. STR remains necessary in 23.3% of cases to avoid unacceptable neurologic morbidity [pubmed_38608817].
