# Outcomes of Separation Surgery Followed by SBRT for Metastatic Spine Tumors

## Local Control

**1-Year Local Control Rates:**
- Separation surgery paired with postoperative SBRT achieves **84% 1-year local control rate** [pubmed_40968026]
- Postoperative SBRT setting demonstrates **over 80% 1-year local control rate** [pubmed_41039525]
- Combined approach supports **high local control rates ranging from 85-95%** depending on tumor size, dose delivered, and biological equivalent dose (BED) [pubmed_39739070]

**Epidural Disease Management:**
- Significant improvements in local control were observed when patients with high-grade preoperative epidural disease were surgically downgraded to Bilsky grade 0–1 [pubmed_40968026]

## Surgical Outcomes

**Comparative Safety Profile:**
- Separation surgery resulted in **lower blood loss and shorter operating times** compared to corpectomy [pubmed_40968026]
- Separation surgery outcomes: **1-year survival 41-78%, recurrence rate 4-22%, reoperation 5%, complications 5-14%** [pubmed_35155240]

## Radiation Dosing

Standard postoperative SBRT dosing protocols: **24-30 Gy in 1-3 fractions delivered 1-4 weeks post-operatively**, with doses prescribed using biological effective dose (BED) calculations [pubmed_41532750]

## Adjacent Level Progression

The combined approach **does not increase the risk of adjacent level progression**, validating current multidisciplinary practice patterns [pubmed_39838517] (Evidence: 2b)

## Evidence Quality

These recommendations are supported by evidence ranging from Level 2a-2b studies and Level 5 reviews [pubmed_39739070, pubmed_39838517].

# Outcomes of Separation Surgery Followed by SBRT for Metastatic Spine Tumors

## Local Control

**1-Year Local Control Rate:**
- Separation surgery combined with postoperative SBRT achieves a **1-year local control rate of 84%** [pubmed_40968026]

**Local Failure Rates by Epidural Disease Grade:**
- In Class B-C lesions with **high-grade epidural disease**: separation surgery plus SBRT resulted in **7.9% local failure (LF)** compared to **20% LF with SBRT alone** (p=0.051, borderline significant) [pubmed_40516631]
  - Note: This trend suggests combined modality therapy may improve outcomes, though statistical significance was not achieved (n=261) [pubmed_40516631]

**Neurological Improvement with Epidural Decompression:**
- Separation surgery followed by SBRT has shown **improved neurological outcomes** compared to SBRT alone, particularly in patients with epidural disease [pubmed_39739070]
- Significant improvements in local control were observed when patients with **high-grade preoperative epidural disease were surgically downgraded to Bilsky grade 0–1** [pubmed_40968026]

## Radiation Dose Parameters

Standard postoperative SBRT dosing protocols:
- **24-30 Gy in 1-3 fractions** [pubmed_41532750]
- **Timing**: 1-4 weeks post-operatively [pubmed_41532750], with 1-2 weeks recommended to allow surgical wound healing while maximizing tumor control [pubmed_39739070]
- Dose calculations used **biological effective dose (BED)** with α/β ratios specific to spinal cord (BED10) and tumor (BED3) [pubmed_41532750]

## Adjacent Level Progression

- The combined approach of **piecemeal separation surgery followed by conformal postoperative SBRT did NOT increase the risk of adjacent level progression** in patients with metastatic spinal disease [pubmed_39838517]
- This validates current multidisciplinary practice patterns [pubmed_39838517]

## Vertebral Compression Fracture (VCF) Outcomes

- **Baseline VCF and 18-24 Gy delivered in single fraction are predictive of further collapse** [pubmed_35155240]
- The specific VCF rate following separation surgery plus SBRT was not directly reported; however, radiotherapy-alone approaches showed significant failure with **20%-30% vertebral compression fracture rates** [pubmed_40968025]

## Preoperative Embolization Impact

- All patients in the preoperative embolization study received **separation surgery (complete or near-complete resection/decompression) followed by SBRT**, though specific survival outcomes from this intervention were not numerically detailed in the provided summaries [pubmed_41532750]

## Key Clinical Recommendations

- **Combined separation surgery + SBRT is recommended** over either modality alone [pubmed_39739070]
- In patients with **epidural disease and neurological compromise, immediate surgical decompression combined with adjuvant SBRT is recommended** [pubmed_39739070]
- Separation surgery followed by SBRT is supported particularly for patients with **severe epidural compression and neurological symptoms** [pubmed_39838517]

## Evidence Gaps

- Overall survival data specific to separation surgery + SBRT were not numerically quantified in the provided papers
- Long-term follow-up beyond 1 year is not detailed
- Sample sizes in some comparisons are modest (n=261 for the LF comparison)
