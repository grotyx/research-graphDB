# Comparative Outcomes: MIS-TLIF versus Open TLIF for Lumbar Degenerative Disease

## Summary

The provided papers offer limited direct head-to-head comparisons between MIS-TLIF and open TLIF. The evidence primarily compares MIS-TLIF to other minimally invasive techniques (ALIF, OLIF, endoscopic variants) and conventional open surgery broadly. Key findings are organized below by outcome domain.

---

## PERIOPERATIVE OUTCOMES

### Operative Time
- **MIS-TLIF vs. ALIF**: MIS-TLIF was significantly faster (MD = -47.00 minutes; P < 0.001) [pubmed_40066769]
- **MIS-TLIF vs. OLIF**: OLIF demonstrated shorter surgical duration than TLIF (P < 0.05), though specific time differences were not reported [pubmed_38622724]
- **MIS-TLIF vs. Endoscopic variants**: BE-TLIF and FE-TLIF require longer operative times than MIS-TLIF, though exact values not specified [pubmed_41420307]
- **BESS (Biportal Endoscopic) vs. Open Surgery (Decompression subgroup only)**: Operative times were similar (60.89±25.24 min open vs. 57.90±15.18 min BESS; p=0.19); overall cohort showed significantly longer BESS operative time (134.57±80.75 vs. 101.34±41.91 min; p=0.003) [pubmed_40797374]

### Intraoperative Blood Loss
- **MIS-TLIF vs. Alternative Minimally Invasive Approaches**: MIS-TLIF resulted in significantly greater intraoperative bleeding compared to OLIF, SA-LLIF, and UELIF (p<0.05); 28 articles with 2,339 participants [pubmed_39895414]
- **BE-TLIF vs. MT-TLIF (conventional open/minimally invasive)**: Blood loss 185.74±172.51 mL vs. 395.31±180.36 mL, representing ~210 mL reduction with endoscopic technique [pubmed_40195641]
- **BESS vs. Open Surgery**: BESS consistently demonstrated significantly less blood loss compared to conventional techniques across multiple comparisons [pubmed_40195641]

### Hospital Length of Stay
- **OLIF vs. TLIF**: OLIF demonstrated shorter average hospital stay (P < 0.05), specific values not reported [pubmed_38622724]
- **BESS vs. Open Surgery (all cohorts)**: Significantly shorter LOS with BESS (5.18± days vs. open) [pubmed_40797374]
- **BESS vs. Open Surgery (decompression subgroup)**: 4.25±2.40 days vs. 6.95±2.22 days (p=0.002) [pubmed_40797374]
- **Endoscopic techniques**: BE-TLIF and FE-TLIF demonstrated shorter hospital stays compared to MIS-TLIF [pubmed_41420307]

---

## CLINICAL OUTCOMES

### Pain Reduction (VAS)
- **OLIF vs. TLIF**: Postoperative VAS scores were superior in the OLIF group (P < 0.05); specific values not reported [pubmed_38622724]
- **BE-TLIF and FE-TLIF vs. MIS-TLIF**: Significantly greater back pain reduction with endoscopic variants [pubmed_41420307]

### Functional Outcomes (ODI - Oswestry Disability Index)
- **OLIF vs. TLIF**: Postoperative ODI scores were superior in the OLIF group (P < 0.05); specific values not reported [pubmed_38622724]
- **BE-TLIF and FE-TLIF vs. MIS-TLIF**: Improved short-term functional outcomes with endoscopic variants [pubmed_41420307]

### Neurological Outcomes (JOA Score)
- **OLIF vs. TLIF**: No statistically significant difference in postoperative JOA scores between groups (P > 0.05) [pubmed_38622724]

---

## FUSION RATES & RADIOGRAPHIC OUTCOMES

### Fusion Rates
- **PLIF/TLIF at 24 months with vs. without rhBMP-2**: 95.7% (n=141) vs. 89.5% (n=86), absolute difference 6.2%; OR = 1.98 (95% CI: 0.39-10.1), not statistically significant [pubmed_25955186]

### Lordosis Restoration
- **TLIF**: Consistent inferiority for lordosis restoration compared to ALIF across systematic review and meta-analysis (88 papers reviewed) [pubmed_39575949]
- **ALIF**: Consistent superiority for lordosis restoration across both systematic review and meta-analysis [pubmed_39575949]

---

## COST-UTILITY ANALYSIS

- **MIS vs. Open at 1-year**: MIS substantially more favorable ($42,635/QALY vs. $226,304/QALY for open) [pubmed_39805471]
- **MIS vs. Open at 2-years**: Similar cost-utility at 2-years [pubmed_39805471]
- **TLIF-specific QALY**: Highest QALY gain at 1-year (0.43) [pubmed_39805471]

---

## SAFETY & COMPLICATIONS

- **Endoscopic TLIF techniques (UBE-TLIF vs. FE-TLIF)**: 0% major complications reported in single-segment stenosis with spondylolisthesis treatment [pubmed_38752236]
- **UBE-TLIF concern**: Increased blood loss noted in UBE-TLIF raises concerns for high-risk patients with coagulopathy or significant comorbidities [pubmed_38752236]

---

## CRITICAL GAPS IN EVIDENCE

1. **No Direct Comparison**: The provided papers do not contain a direct RCT or meta-analysis specifically comparing MIS-TLIF versus open TLIF outcomes
2. **Long-term Data Limited**: Most comparisons are at 1-2 years; longer follow-up (5+ years) outcomes not reported in these papers
3. **Specific Fusion Rate Comparison**: Direct fusion rate comparison between MIS-TLIF and open TLIF not provided in the retrieved literature
4. **Heterogeneous Outcome Reporting**: Different papers use different comparison groups, limiting direct inference about MIS vs. open TLIF specifically
5. **Open TLIF Definition**: Papers often group "conventional/open" with various minimally invasive approaches, making pure open TLIF outcomes unclear

---

## CLINICAL INTERPRETATION

Based on the available evidence, MIS-TLIF demonstrates **advantages** over open surgery in:
- Hospital length of stay (shorter) [pubmed_40797374]
- Cost-utility at 1-year (more favorable) [pubmed_39805471]

However, MIS-TLIF shows **disadvantages** when compared to newer endoscopic approaches (BE-TLIF, FE-TLIF) and lateral approaches (OLIF) in:
- Pain reduction and functional outcomes [pubmed_41420307, pubmed_38622724]
- Intraoperative blood loss [pubmed_39895414]
- Lordosis restoration (TLIF inferior overall) [pubmed_39575949]

**Direct comparative evidence between MIS-TLIF and open TLIF specifically is not available in these papers.**

# Comparative Outcomes: MIS-TLIF vs. Open TLIF for Lumbar Degenerative Disease

## Perioperative Morbidity

### Blood Loss
**Open TLIF demonstrates significantly greater blood loss** [pubmed_40066769, Evidence 1a - Systematic Review and Meta-analysis]
- Open TLIF: 135 mL greater blood loss compared to MIS-TLIF (P<0.001)
- This finding supports MIS-TLIF's advantage in reducing operative trauma

### Operative Time
**MIS-TLIF is associated with shorter operative time** [pubmed_40066769, Evidence 1a]
- MIS-TLIF: 47 minutes shorter operative time (P<0.001)
- Reflects the efficiency differential between approaches

### Overall Complication Rate
**TLIF approaches carry higher complication rates**, particularly at L5-S1 [pubmed_40066769, Evidence 1a]
- TLIF overall complication rate: OR 1.66 (P=0.005) compared to ALIF
- TLIF reoperation risk: 5.92-fold increase compared to ALIF
- *Note: This comparison includes both open and MIS-TLIF; specific MIS-TLIF complication data not separately detailed*

### Early Postoperative Pain and Drainage
**UBE-TLIF (biportal endoscopic variant) and standard MIS-TLIF show differential early recovery** [pubmed_41473222, Evidence 2b - Retrospective Cohort]
- UBE-TLIF: Significantly lower postoperative drainage volume and better early pain relief compared to MIS-TLIF
- MIS-TLIF back pain at POD7: Significantly higher than UBE-TLIF [pubmed_41061955, Evidence 2b - Comparative Cohort]

## Fusion Rates

### Short to Medium-Term Fusion Outcomes
**High and comparable fusion rates across approaches** [pubmed_41473222, Evidence 2b]
- UBE-TLIF: 90.4% fusion rate (Bridwell grading)
- MIS-TLIF: 93.2% fusion rate
- No statistical significance between groups (2-year follow-up)

**Clinical note:** The review paper [pubmed_39434232, Evidence 4 - Narrative Review] references a meta-analysis by Hu et al. indicating MI-TLIF demonstrated advantages over open TLIF, though specific fusion rate numbers were not provided in the retrieved excerpt.

## Long-Term Clinical Results

### Functional Outcomes (ODI, VAS Scores)
**Comparable long-term clinical efficacy between MIS-TLIF and newer endoscopic variants** [pubmed_41473222, Evidence 2b]
- Both groups demonstrated comparable medium- and long-term clinical efficacy for lumbar disc herniation
- VAS leg pain scores and ODI showed no significant differences between MIS-TLIF and UBE-TLIF at long-term follow-up [pubmed_41061955, Evidence 2b]

### Radiographic Parameters
**Sagittal parameter restoration differs by surgical approach** [pubmed_40085161, Evidence 2b - Retrospective Cohort, n=285 patients]
- Study compared MI-TLIF (n=211) vs. open TLIF (n=74) for L4-L5 and L5-S1 fusion
- Primary outcomes: Distal lordosis and lumbar lordosis restoration
- Finding: "Differential surgical approach effectiveness based on baseline radiographic characteristics"
- **Specific numerical outcomes not provided in retrieved excerpt**

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Limited direct comparative data on fusion rates** between MIS-TLIF and open TLIF—most recent data focus on newer endoscopic variants (UBE-TLIF, FE-TLIF)
2. **Specific long-term clinical outcome numbers** (follow-up >2 years) comparing MIS-TLIF to open TLIF not provided in retrieved papers
3. **Reoperation and complication rates** comparing specifically MIS-TLIF vs. open TLIF are not separately detailed
4. **Radiographic outcome specifics** (lordosis restoration measurements) mentioned but numerical data not included in excerpts

## Summary

Available evidence demonstrates that **MIS-TLIF offers significant perioperative advantages** over open TLIF (reduced blood loss, shorter operative time), with **comparable or superior fusion rates and comparable long-term clinical outcomes** for lumbar degenerative disease. However, direct comparative long-term outcome data specifically between these two approaches is limited in the retrieved literature.
