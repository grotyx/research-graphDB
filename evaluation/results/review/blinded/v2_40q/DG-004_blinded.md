# Cage Subsidence Risk Factors and Design Effects in Lumbar Interbody Fusion

## RISK FACTORS FOR CAGE SUBSIDENCE

### Patient-Related Risk Factors

**Bone Quality and Density:**
- Low bone mineral density (BMD) is a significant risk factor for cage subsidence [pubmed_38754736] (Evidence: 2b)
- Endplate bone quality (EBQ) scores obtained from MRI reliably determine regional BMD and predict subsidence risk following OLIF [pubmed_38754736]
- In non-osteoporotic patients, lower bone mineral density remained a predictor of subsidence in multivariate analysis [pubmed_41782914] (Evidence: 2b)

**Sarcopenia and Age:**
- Sarcopenia and advanced age were examined as potential risk factors in stand-alone OLIF [pubmed_41782914] (Evidence: 2b)
- Cage subsidence (>2 mm height loss) occurred in 32.7% of cases in a non-osteoporotic cohort [pubmed_41782914]

### Procedure-Related Risk Factors

**Endplate Preparation:**
- Endplate damage or reduction in contact area between endplate and cage are possible factors for cage subsidence [pubmed_38509573] (Evidence: 2b)
- Protecting the endplates presents a surgical challenge; cage subsidence was observed in 1.08% of cases (2 of 185), both occurring during the learning phase of unilateral biportal endoscopic transforaminal LLIF [pubmed_38509573]
- Proper endplate preparation is theoretically "the key to lumbar fusion" [pubmed_38509573]

---

## CAGE DESIGN AND MATERIAL EFFECTS ON SUBSIDENCE

### Architectural Design Over Material Composition

**Material Stiffness is Not Predictive:**
- Material stiffness alone is NOT a reliable predictor of subsidence resistance [pubmed_35346883] (Evidence: 4, biomechanical study)
- Despite solid titanium having the highest stiffness (57.1 kN/mm), it exhibited the greatest subsidence displacement (0.538 mm) [pubmed_35346883]

**Superior Design Characteristics:**
- A porous titanium cage with body lattice structure and microporous endplates demonstrated superior subsidence performance compared to structurally dissimilar designs, regardless of stiffness [pubmed_36130418] (Evidence: 4, biomechanical study)
- Porous titanium cage with intermediate stiffness (40.4 kN/mm) demonstrated the lowest subsidence displacement (0.195 mm) [pubmed_35346883]
- **Key finding:** Pore structure and compliance optimization are equally important to material modulus [pubmed_35346883]

### Expandable Cage Technology

- Expandable LLIF cages that avoid forceful insertion may help prevent subsidence [pubmed_38146053] (Evidence: 4, comprehensive review)
- **Knowledge gap:** The association between expandable cages and improved clinical outcomes compared with fixed cages remains uncertain, and variability based on surgical technique needs further study [pubmed_38146053]

### Cage Geometry and Positioning

**Optimal Dimensions (OLIF-based finite element analysis):**
- Extended cage length (60 mm) with apophyseal ring contact provides superior load distribution and stress shielding of endplates [pubmed_40194709] (Evidence: 5, FEA)
- Reduced cage height (10 mm) decreases stress concentration [pubmed_40194709]
- Increased lordotic angulation (12°) improves load orientation [pubmed_40194709]
- Bilateral pedicle screw fixation provides additional protection by distributing forces across multiple load-bearing elements [pubmed_40194709]

**Cage Shape:**
- Cage shape and material, cage position, and surgical disc height correction represent important influencing factors on subsidence [pubmed_36944178] (Evidence: 2a, comparative clinical study with biomechanics)

## CLINICAL OUTCOMES ASSOCIATED WITH CAGE SUBSIDENCE

### Consequences of Subsidence

Cage subsidence may lead to compromised clinical results including:
- Loss of disc height correction [pubmed_34324459] (Evidence: 4, systematic review)
- Alterations in spinal alignment [pubmed_34324459]
- Recurrent pain [pubmed_34324459]
- Vertebral body fracture [pubmed_34324459]
- Progressive deformity and neural element compression [pubmed_34324459]
- Reduced chance of successful fusion and possible revision surgery [pubmed_34324459]
- Reduction in fusion segment height, spinal curvature alteration, and nerve compression leading to neurological complications [pubmed_36726086] (Evidence: 5, FEA)

### Subsidence Prevalence

- Cage subsidence complication rates range from 15.9-70% in lumbar interbody fusion overall [pubmed_36726086] (Evidence: 5, finite element analysis)

---

## KNOWLEDGE GAPS

1. **Expandable cages:** Clinical efficacy compared to fixed cages remains uncertain; variability based on surgical technique needs investigation [pubmed_38146053]

2. **Material effects:** Most studies examined show cage material (PEEK, titanium, tantalum) does not significantly affect bone fusion rates, though subsidence rates vary [pubmed_36726086]

3. **Comprehensive risk stratification:** While individual factors (BMD, age, sarcopenia, endplate quality) have been identified, integrated predictive models for clinical practice are limited

4. **Learning curve effects:** Data from one study suggest endplate protection improves with experience, but systematic evidence on technique-specific subsidence rates is needed [pubmed_38509573]

## 1. CAGE DESIGN EFFECTS ON SUBSIDENCE

### Double vs. Single Banana-Shaped Cages

**Subsidence Rates:** [pubmed_36944178]
- **Single banana-shaped cages: 19.9%** cage subsidence at 12-month follow-up
- **Double banana-shaped cages: 11.8%** cage subsidence at 12-month follow-up
- **Difference: p=0.002** (statistically significant)

**Biomechanical Stress Analysis:** [pubmed_36944178]
Under axial loading, maximum endplate stress in single-cage model was **1.72 times greater** than double-cage model. Stress differentials were more pronounced during:
- **Extension: 2.64-fold greater stress** in single-cage model
- **Side bending: 2.30-fold greater stress** in single-cage model

These biomechanical findings directly correlate with the higher clinical subsidence rates observed with single cages.

---

## 2. SUBSIDENCE RATES ACROSS SURGICAL TECHNIQUES

### Oblique Lateral Interbody Fusion (OLIF) with Different Fixation

**Transfacet Screw (TFS) vs. Bilateral Pedicle Screw (BPS):** [pubmed_36522754]
- **TFS subsidence: 16%** (subsidence from immediate postoperative)
- **BPS subsidence: 10%** (subsidence from immediate postoperative)

Despite subsidence, both groups maintained net disc height improvement over preoperative baseline:
- **TFS: +2.2 mm (+26% improvement)**
- **BPS: +3.7 mm (+42% improvement)**

**Fusion rates despite subsidence:** [pubmed_36522754]
- TFS: 91% fusion rate
- BPS: 100% fusion rate

### Stand-Alone Oblique Interbody Fusion (SA-OLIF)

**Subsidence rate in non-osteoporotic patients:** [pubmed_41782914]
- **32.7% of cases** experienced cage subsidence (>2 mm height loss)
- Sample: 98 non-osteoporotic patients

### Lateral Lumbar Interbody Fusion (LLIF)

**Variable BMP-2 dosing effects:** [pubmed_37850225]
- **Group A (BMP-2 >4.2 mg): 20.8%** subsidence (10 of 48 levels) at 12 months
- **Group B (BMP-2 4.2 mg): 13.1%** subsidence (10 of 76 levels) at 12 months
- **Group C (BMP-2 <4.2 mg): 17.8%** subsidence (8 of 45 levels) at 12 months
- **p=0.64** (no significant differences)

## 3. ENDPLATE PREPARATION AND QUALITY AS RISK FACTORS

### Learning Curve Effects on Endplate Damage

**Endoscopic TLIF (ULIF) early learning phase:** [pubmed_38509573]
- **1.08% cage subsidence rate overall** (2 of 186 cases)
- **Both subsidence cases occurred during learning phase**, suggesting operator-dependent technique sensitivity
- **Key mechanism identified:** "Damage to the endplate or reduction in contact area between endplate and cage are possible factors for cage subsidence"
- **Critical factor:** "Endplate preparation is the key to lumbar fusion"

### Endplate Bone Quality (EBQ) Scoring

**MRI-based assessment:** [pubmed_38754736]
- Low bone mineral density (BMD) identified as **significant risk factor** for cage subsidence
- **Endplate bone quality (EBQ) from clinical MRI deemed reliable** in determining regional BMD
- **Association established:** EBQ score predicts cage subsidence following OLIF (specific outcomes not fully detailed in excerpt)

## 4. PATIENT DEMOGRAPHIC AND SYSTEMIC FACTORS

### Obesity and Fusion Outcomes

**Meta-analysis findings:** [pubmed_40433998]
- Obesity impacts fusion outcomes with variable nonunion rates depending on follow-up duration
- **Five of seven studies** reporting follow-up used ~2-year follow-up period
- **Note:** Whether obesity is independently associated with increased subsidence rates was not clearly quantified in the provided excerpt

### Age and BMI

**No significant associations with subsidence:** [pubmed_41677169]
- **Age: no statistically significant association** with fusion grades or cage subsidence
- **BMI: no associations** with TLIF interbody fusion quality, posterolateral intertransverse fusion quality, or cage subsidence rates
- These null findings suggest age and BMI are not independent subsidence predictors in the study population

### Smoking Status

**Smoking and fusion impairment:** [pubmed_41066602]
- Smoking associated with **worse results with PEEK interbody cages** (material-dependent effect)
- **Mechanism:** Nicotine-induced vasoconstriction reduces blood flow and nutrient availability, leading to pseudoarthrosis
- Associated with **lower vitamin D levels** and comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension) increasing pseudoarthrosis risk
- **Specific smoking-subsidence quantification:** not provided in excerpt

**Smoking and TLIF/posterolateral arthrodesis:** [pubmed_41677169]
- Study examined whether smoking influences fusion quality or cage subsidence
- Specific subsidence rates by smoking status not detailed in provided excerpt

## 5. CAGE MATERIAL EFFECTS

### Titanium/PEEK (Ti/PEEK) Cages with BMP-2

**ALIF with Ti/PEEK 3-screw integral cage:** [pubmed_35386238]
- **No lucency or halo formation** adjacent to titanium endplates at 6-month follow-up
- Consistent with **bone/porous titanium incorporation**
- Demonstrates biocompatibility advantage over standard materials
- **Specific subsidence rates:** not provided in excerpt
- Effective for isthmic spondylolisthesis, spondylotic radiculopathy, and discogenic low back pain

### PEEK Cages

**Material-specific vulnerability:** [pubmed_41066602]
- Smokers demonstrated **worse results with PEEK cages** compared to other materials
- Suggests PEEK may be less resilient to compromised vascular supply

## 7. CLINICAL OUTCOMES DESPITE SUBSIDENCE

### Fusion Success Despite Subsidence

**Critical finding:** Subsidence does **NOT uniformly prevent fusion consolidation** [pubmed_36522754]

- **TFS group: 91% fusion rate** despite 16% subsidence
- **BPS group: 100% fusion rate** despite 10% subsidence
- Both groups maintained **positive net disc height gains** over baseline despite subsidence

**Endoscopic TLIF:** [pubmed_38509573]
- High fusion rates achieved with minimal subsidence (1.08%) when endplate preparation technique mastered

## 8. EVIDENCE GAPS

1. **Quantified subsidence thresholds:** No papers defined clinically significant subsidence levels that correlate with worse long-term outcomes
2. **Smoking-specific subsidence rates:** Impact of smoking on subsidence quantified only for fusion quality, not specific subsidence percentages
3. **Obesity-subsidence relationship:** Meta-analysis noted but detailed quantitative relationship not provided
4. **Sarcopenia as independent predictor:** Examined in SA-OLIF cohort but specific odds ratios/effect sizes not detailed in excerpt
5. **Long-term follow-up:** Most studies use 12-24 month follow-up; longer-term subsidence progression unclear
6. **Optimal BMP-2 dosing:** No significant differences found (p=0.64) but study may be underpowered

---

## SUMMARY

**Modifiable factors with strongest evidence for reducing subsidence:**
1. **Double banana-shaped cages** reduce subsidence 41% compared to single (11.8% vs. 19.9%, p=0.002) through biomechanical stress reduction (1.72-2.64-fold) [pubmed_36944178]
2. **Meticulous endplate preparation** critical—surgeon experience-dependent, with subsidence concentrated in learning phase [pubmed_38509573]
3. **Enhanced fixation** (bilateral pedicle screws vs. transfacet) reduces subsidence 38% (10% vs. 16%) [pubmed_36522754]
4. **Avoid PEEK cages in smokers** due to vasoconstriction-related pseudoarthrosis risk [pubmed_41066602]

**Non-modifiable factors:**
- Endplate bone quality/BMD is strong predictor but pre-existing [pubmed_38754736]
- Age and BMI alone are not independent predictors in most cohorts [pubmed_41677169]
