## What the Papers Address

### PSO Outcomes (Available Data)

**Sagittal Correction:**
- PSO demonstrates effective lumbar lordosis correction in adult spinal deformity [pubmed_41497819], though specific magnitude compared to VCR is not reported in these papers

**Complications:**
- PSO is associated with **longer operative time and greater intraoperative blood loss** compared to Smith-Petersen osteotomy [pubmed_25868095], a meta-analysis of ankylosing spondylitis patients (Evidence: 1a)
- PSO has been reported with excellent radiologic results and **no pseudarthrosis in 11 patients** with severe cervicothoracic kyphotic deformities [pubmed_38765779], though 91% did not have ankylosing spondylitis

### Studies Reviewed
- One meta-analysis [pubmed_41497819] compares **PSO versus anterior column realignment (ACR)**, not VCR
- Clinical studies included range from 15-65 patients per group with heterogeneous follow-up periods
- No data on reoperation rates comparing PSO to VCR

## Evidence Gap

**To answer your clinical question, evidence directly comparing PSO and VCR outcomes is needed.** The retrieved papers address PSO comparisons with other techniques (SPO, ACR) but do not provide the PSO-VCR comparison you are asking about.

# Comparative Outcomes: PSO vs VCR for Fixed Sagittal Imbalance

## Evidence Summary

The provided papers do **not contain a direct head-to-head comparison** of PSO versus VCR for fixed sagittal imbalance. However, I can extract relevant data on each technique separately and one indirect comparison study.

---

## Correction Magnitude

### Posterior Vertebral Column Resection (PVCR/VCR)

**PVCR for thoracolumbar Pott's deformity** [pubmed_40513242]:
- **Immediate postoperative correction**: 75.3° reduction in local kyphosis (from 107.3° to 32.0°)
- **Long-term durability**: Minimal loss of 5.1° at mean 105.7-month follow-up (final angle 37.1°)
- **Correction maintenance**: 93.2% maintenance of correction over long-term

**Modified PVCR with posterior ligamentous complex preservation** [pubmed_40381021]:
- **Deformity correction rate**: 62.26%
- **Neurological complication rate**: 16.1% (significantly lower than traditional PVCR at 35-45%, p<0.05 implied)

**Single-level vs multilevel VCR in pediatric patients** [pubmed_30475336]:
- **Single-level VCR coronal correction**: 63%
- **Multilevel VCR coronal correction**: 58%
- Difference not statistically significant (p>0.05)

**PVCR with vs without anterior column support** [pubmed_30267947]:
- **Osteotomy distance required**:
  - Without ACS: 4.0 cm
  - With ACS: 5.3 cm (p<0.001)
- **Gap shortening**:
  - Without ACS: 4.0 cm
  - With ACS: 3.5 cm (p=0.005)

### Pedicle Subtraction Osteotomy (PSO)

**No specific sagittal correction magnitudes reported** for PSO in the retrieved papers. Papers note PSO allows "significant angular correction at a single level" [pubmed_41497819] but do not provide quantitative correction data.

## Quality of Life Outcomes

**SRS-22 scores: LCR-A vs 3-column osteotomy in septuagenarians** [pubmed_40968391]:

| Measure | Preoperative 3CO | Preoperative LCR-A | Final 3CO | Final LCR-A | P-value |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| SRS-22 Total | 2.6±0.6 | 3.1±0.2 | 3.3±0.8 | 4.0±0.1 | 0.006 |

LCR-A (lateral column realignment) showed significantly better final SRS-22 scores compared to 3-column osteotomy (p=0.006). All subdomains (function, pain, self-image, mental health, satisfaction) improved significantly in both groups (p=0.001) [pubmed_40968391].

---

## Reoperation/Rod Fracture Risk

**ACR with extended fusion constructs** [pubmed_31229745]:
- **Rod fracture risk**: 4.4%
- **Timing**: 75% of fractures occurred within first postoperative year
- Longer fusion lengths increase rod fracture risk
- Clinical implication: Extended fusion constructs require careful surveillance

---

## Critical Evidence Gaps

1. **No direct PSO vs VCR randomized controlled trial** identified
2. **PSO-specific complication quantification**: Blood loss volume, neurological injury rate, operative time not provided numerically
3. **No comparison of reoperation rates** between PSO and VCR for sagittal imbalance specifically
4. **Limited PSO outcome data**: Only one meta-analysis on PSO for HRQOL [pubmed_32382878] without specific correction magnitudes
5. **Population specificity**: Available VCR data primarily from thoracolumbar Pott's disease and severe kyphoscoliosis; limited data on isolated sagittal imbalance

## Summary

**VCR demonstrates**:
- Substantial sagittal correction (75.3° in Pott's disease; 62.26% correction rate with ligament-preserving technique)
- Durable long-term correction (93.2% maintenance at 105+ months)
- Neurological complication rate reduced to 16.1% with modern techniques (vs 35-45% historically)

**PSO**:
- Described as capable of "significant angular correction at a single level" but specific magnitudes not provided
- Associated with high blood loss and neurological injury risk, but rates not quantified

**No direct head-to-head comparison with specific reoperation/rod fracture data available for this patient population.**
