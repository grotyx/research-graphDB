All retrieved papers are meta-analyses (Evidence 1a) or systematic reviews (Evidence 2a) comparing laminoplasty (LP) versus laminectomy with fusion (LF) for multilevel cervical spondylotic myelopathy (CSM) and ossification of the posterior longitudinal ligament (OPLL).

# Comparative Outcomes: Laminoplasty vs. Laminectomy with Fusion for Multilevel Cervical Myelopathy

## C5 Palsy Rates

**Laminoplasty demonstrates significantly lower C5 palsy incidence:**

- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_30921202]: Laminoplasty showed significantly lower nerve palsy rates compared to laminectomy and fusion
- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_27281067]: Laminoplasty demonstrated fewer C5 palsy cases (OR 0.26, p<0.001)
- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_38764362]: C5 palsy incidence was significantly lower in laminoplasty group (P = 0.003)
- Systematic review (Evidence 2a) [pubmed_27781191]: All four studies reported higher C5 palsy incidence following laminectomy and fusion than laminoplasty

## Clinical Outcomes (Neurological and Pain Improvement)

**Similar functional improvements between procedures:**

- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_27281067]: Equivalent JOA scores, CCI, and VAS pain scores between groups
- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_30921202]: Despite similarities in clinical outcomes (JOA, VAS, CCI, Nurich grade, reoperation rate), laminoplasty demonstrates superior safety
- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_30813208]: Both procedures achieved substantial improvements in functional scores (JOA) and pain reduction (VAS)

**Axial neck pain:**
- Prospective cohort (Evidence II) and retrospective cohort (Evidence III) [pubmed_27781191]: No significant difference in axial neck pain between procedures

## Range of Motion and Cervical Lordosis

**Laminoplasty superior for ROM preservation:**

- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_30813208]: Laminoplasty achieved greater postoperative cervical range of motion preservation
- Systematic review (Evidence 2a) [pubmed_33819935]: One study reported significantly greater ROM in flexion, extension, and side bending for laminoplasty

**Lordosis preservation equivalent:**
- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_27281067]: Both procedures showed equivalent cervical lordosis preservation

## Operative and Perioperative Outcomes

**Shorter operative time with laminoplasty:**
- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_27281067]: Laminoplasty demonstrated shorter operation time (19.57 minutes less, p=0.002)

**Overall complications:**
- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_27281067]: Equivalent blood loss and total complications between procedures
- Meta-analysis (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_30813208]: Laminoplasty had lower overall complication rates

## Special Considerations for OPLL

**Limited evidence with varying findings:**

- Systematic review (Evidence 2a) [pubmed_27781191]: One retrospective cohort (Evidence III) study suggested no significant difference in OPLL progression between groups
- Systematic review (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_23963000]: Limited evidence suggests laminectomy-fusion may offer superior myelopathy improvement for OPLL, but this must be weighed against pseudarthrosis risk
- All four OPLL-specific studies [pubmed_33819935]: Consistently reported higher C5 nerve palsy incidence following laminectomy-fusion than laminoplasty

**Recovery outcomes for OPLL:**
- Systematic review (Evidence 2a) [pubmed_33819935]: One study found no significant difference in recovery between groups; another reported significantly higher recovery for laminoplasty than laminectomy-fusion

## Clinical Decision-Making

Systematic review (Evidence 1a) [pubmed_23963000] recommends individualized decision-making incorporating: **age, comorbidities, surgeon familiarity, technical expertise, and patient preferences**, as both procedures demonstrate similar effectiveness for cervical spondylotic myelopathy based on available evidence.

## Evidence Gaps

- Most evidence derives from non-randomized studies and meta-analyses with variable quality [pubmed_30921202]
- High-quality randomized controlled trials with long-term follow-up and large sample sizes are needed
- OPLL-specific comparative data remains limited, with some conflicting findings between procedures
