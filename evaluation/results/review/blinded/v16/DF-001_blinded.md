**Limited Direct Comparative Data**: The provided papers do not include studies specifically comparing T10 versus L1 as UIV in Lenke 5C degenerative scoliosis. This represents a significant evidence gap for your clinical question.

## Relevant Indirect Evidence

### UIV Distribution in Adult Spinal Deformity Surgery [pubmed_35586905]
- **Study Design**: Retrospective cohort analysis (Evidence: 2b)
- **UIV Placement in 179 adult spinal deformity patients**:
  - Upper thoracic (T1-T9): 25 patients (14%)
  - Lower thoracic (T10-T12): 118 patients (66%)
  - Upper lumbar (L1-L3): 36 patients (20%)

This distribution suggests T10 placement is more common than L1 in adult spinal deformity cohorts, but does not address relative outcomes between these specific levels.

### Proximal Junctional Complications

**PJK/PJF Rates** [pubmed_39973129]
- Overall incidence: PJK occurs in 17-61.7% of cases; PJF requiring revision in 5-20%
- **Clinical relevance**: UIV selection directly influences proximal junctional failure risk, but the papers do not stratify this by specific UIV level (T10 vs L1)

**Segmental Lordosis Overcorrection Risk** [pubmed_38270393]
- At proximal spinal levels **(T10-L2)**, overcorrection beyond normative values resulted in "dramatically increased proximal junctional failure rates"
- Both T10 and L1 fall within this critical zone, suggesting that correction magnitude at UIV may matter more than absolute level choice
- **Evidence Level**: 2b

### Soft-Landing Concept [pubmed_41297090]
- Transverse process hooks (vs rigid pedicle screws) at UIV proposed to reduce proximal junctional complications through gradual stress transition
- Relevant to any UIV selection but does not differentiate T10 vs L1 outcomes

## Alignment Framework Considerations [pubmed_38771063]

- **Roussouly-based individualization** recommended to minimize mechanical complications and PJK
- Suggests patient-specific anatomic factors should guide UIV selection rather than fixed level choice
- **No level-specific recommendation** for T10 vs L1

## Critical Evidence Gaps

1. **No head-to-head comparison** of T10 vs L1 UIV in Lenke 5C or similar degenerative lumbar scoliosis
2. **No stratified PJK/PJF data** by UIV level
3. **No Lenke 5C-specific outcomes** reported in available literature
4. **Limited evidence** on how UIV level influences long-term sagittal alignment or functional outcomes

## Clinical Implications

Based on available evidence, the choice between T10 and L1 UIV should consider:
- **Magnitude of correction** at UIV (overcorrection at either level increases PJK risk) [pubmed_38270393]
- **Patient age** (age-adjusted alignment thresholds may reduce morbidity) [pubmed_37740115]
- **Specific anatomic morphology** (Roussouly classification) [pubmed_38771063]
- **Institutional outcomes** with sacral versus L5 extension [pubmed_15371700]

# Evidence Synthesis: Optimal UIV Selection for Lenke 5C Adult Degenerative Scoliosis

## Summary Statement

The provided papers do not contain specific evidence comparing T10 versus L1 as the upper instrumented vertebra for Lenke 5C adult degenerative scoliosis. However, general principles for UIV selection from the literature are relevant.

## General UIV Selection Principles

**Anatomic Considerations:**
- UIV selection should avoid spanning across major coronal deformities and consider the apex of thoracic kyphosis [pubmed_37718099, Evidence Level 4]
- The thoracolumbar junction (T10-L2 region) is a transitional area with higher risk of mechanical complications due to mobility differences between the mobile lumbar spine and less mobile thoracic spine [pubmed_40033732, Evidence Level 4]

**Risk Factor Data:**
- More proximal UIV placement (T2 or more proximal) was associated with increased distal junctional kyphosis (DJK) development in adolescent idiopathic scoliosis [pubmed_40103372, Evidence Level 3]. This suggests that proximal UIV placement alters thoracic sagittal alignment and may cause compensatory distal junctional changes.

**General Recommendation:**
- A "neutral and stable vertebra" is recommended as the UIV to minimize proximal junctional kyphosis/failure (PJK/PJF) risk [pubmed_40033732, Evidence Level 4]

## Evidence Gaps

**Critical limitation:** No studies directly comparing T10 versus L1 selection for Lenke 5C curves were identified. The optimal choice requires consideration of:
- Curve apex location
- Preoperative thoracic kyphosis
- Sagittal alignment targets
- Patient-specific factors

**Recommendation:** Evidence-based guidance for this specific clinical scenario requires studies comparing these instrumentation levels in Lenke 5C populations.
