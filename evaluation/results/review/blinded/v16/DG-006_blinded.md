# Laminoplasty versus Laminectomy with Fusion for Multilevel Cervical Myelopathy

## Summary of Evidence

Both approaches are effective for treating multilevel cervical myelopathy, but **laminoplasty demonstrates superior outcomes across multiple domains** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362].

---

## Clinical Outcomes

### Neurological Improvement
Both procedures achieve **comparable neurological recovery**:
- Similar improvements in Japanese Orthopaedic Association (JOA) scores between groups [pubmed_30921202, pubmed_30813208]
- Both procedures effectively decompress the spinal cord and improve neurological function [pubmed_30921202]
- Visual analog scale (VAS) neck pain improvements are similar between approaches [pubmed_30921202]

### Neck Disability and Function
- **Laminoplasty achieved lower Neck Disability Index scores** compared to laminectomy with fusion [pubmed_38764362]

---

## Operative Characteristics (Laminoplasty Advantages)

**Shorter operative time** [pubmed_38764362]

**Less estimated blood loss** [pubmed_38764362]

**Comparable hospital length of stay** between both approaches [pubmed_38764362]

---

## Complication Profiles

### Overall Complication Rates
- **Laminoplasty: Lower overall complication rates** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362]
- Laminectomy and fusion: Associated with more complications [pubmed_30921202]

### Specific Complications
- **C5 palsy: Lower incidence with laminoplasty** [pubmed_38764362]
- Laminectomy and fusion: Associated with postoperative segmental instability and kyphosis when performed without fusion [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_33819935]

---

## Biomechanical Outcomes

### Cervical Range of Motion
- **Laminoplasty: Greater postoperative cervical range of motion preservation** [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_33819935]
- Laminoplasty preserves motion and minimizes alteration to cervical spine biomechanics [pubmed_27281067]

### Cervical Alignment
- **Laminoplasty: Lower T1 slope angle** (more favorable sagittal alignment) [pubmed_38764362]

---

## Procedure Indications

**Laminectomy with fusion** is favored over laminectomy alone because fusion reduces postoperative segmental instability [pubmed_33819935]

**Laminoplasty** is an alternative approach that provides advantages in motion preservation [pubmed_33819935]

---

## Evidence Quality and Limitations

- **High-quality evidence (1a)**: Multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362, pubmed_27281067, pubmed_25815808, pubmed_30921202]
- **Moderate-quality evidence (2a)**: Systematic reviews with nonrandomized comparative studies [pubmed_27781191, pubmed_33819935]
- **Important limitation**: No randomized controlled trials comparing these approaches; most evidence derives from observational studies with potential surgeon selection bias [pubmed_27781191]
- **Special populations**: Limited specific evidence for ossification of the posterior longitudinal ligament (OPLL) subgroup [pubmed_27781191]

---

## Clinical Recommendation

**Laminoplasty emerges as the preferable approach** for multilevel cervical myelopathy based on superior outcomes in operative time, blood loss, complication rates, C5 palsy incidence, motion preservation, and functional scores [pubmed_30813208, pubmed_38764362], while maintaining comparable neurological improvement to laminectomy with fusion.

# Laminoplasty vs Laminectomy with Fusion for Multilevel Cervical Myelopathy

## Summary of Evidence Quality
Multiple meta-analyses (Evidence Level 1a) and systematic reviews (Evidence Level 1a-2a) directly compare these approaches [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_30921202, pubmed_38764362, pubmed_25815808, pubmed_30813208, pubmed_23963000, pubmed_37062984, pubmed_27781191].

## Functional Outcomes

**Both approaches achieve substantial clinical improvement:**
- Both techniques produce significant improvements in functional scores (JOA), pain reduction (VAS), and spinal cord decompression [pubmed_30813208]
- For ossification of the posterior longitudinal ligament (OPLL) specifically, no significant differences were observed in postoperative JOA scores (p=0.20) [pubmed_37062984]
- At 12-month follow-up in ACDF studies (anterior approach for comparison), 76.8% of patients reported symptom improvement with NDI reduction of 23.0 points and VAS arm pain reduction of 30.7 points [pubmed_40047144]

## Postoperative Complications and Safety

**Laminoplasty shows lower overall complication rates:**
- Laminoplasty emerged as the preferable approach based on **greater postoperative cervical range of motion preservation and lower overall complication rates** [pubmed_30813208]

**Laminectomy with fusion concerns:**
- Associated with postoperative segmental instability and kyphosis [pubmed_27281067, pubmed_30921202]
- May alter cervical spine biomechanics and increase adjacent segment degeneration risk [pubmed_38764362]
- Fusion may require additional surgery for adjacent segment disease [pubmed_30921202]

**Laminoplasty concerns:**
- Concerns about inadequate decompression, neck pain, and recurrent stenosis [pubmed_38764362]
- Not universally accepted despite benefits [pubmed_38764362]

## Range of Motion Preservation

- Laminoplasty demonstrates **greater preservation of cervical range of motion** compared to laminectomy with fusion [pubmed_30813208]
- For OPLL, no significant differences in postoperative range of motion between approaches (p=0.63) [pubmed_37062984]

## Special Consideration: OPLL (Ossification of Posterior Longitudinal Ligament)

- Limited evidence suggests laminectomy-fusion may offer superior myelopathy improvement for OPLL, but this must be weighed against pseudarthrosis risk [pubmed_23963000]
- Conflicting results in nonrandomized comparative studies, with baseline confounding (laminectomy-fusion patients had preoperative cervical kyphosis and more severe neurologic deficits) [pubmed_27781191]

## Evidence Gaps and Clinical Decision-Making

**Key limitations:**
- Overall evidence quality is described as "low" [pubmed_23963000]
- For OPLL, all comparative studies were nonrandomized controlled studies with significant limitations [pubmed_27781191]
- High heterogeneity in study designs and patient populations [pubmed_30813208]

**Recommended approach:**
- Clinical decision-making should incorporate **individual patient factors including age, comorbidities, surgeon familiarity, technical expertise, and patient preferences** [pubmed_23963000]
- Both procedures demonstrate similar effectiveness based on available evidence, with choice individualized per patient [pubmed_23963000]

## Conclusion

**Laminoplasty is the preferable approach for most multilevel cervical myelopathy** based on lower complication rates, better motion preservation, and comparable functional outcomes [pubmed_30813208]. However, for OPLL specifically, evidence is more conflicting, and laminectomy with fusion may benefit select patients with severe kyphosis or neurologic deficits. Patient-centered factors should guide final surgical selection.
