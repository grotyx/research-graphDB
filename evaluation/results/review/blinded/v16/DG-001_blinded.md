# Evidence Comparison: UBE versus Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## ODI (Oswestry Disability Index) Improvement

**Limited Direct Comparison Available:**

One study [pubmed_41290735] (Evidence: 2b, retrospective comparative) reported that both uniportal and unilateral biportal endoscopic fusion are **superior to MIS-TLIF in terms of early postoperative low back pain relief**, particularly within 3 months post-surgery. However, this study noted **"no significant difference in early leg pain relief and functional improvement"** between endoscopic techniques and MIS-TLIF. Critically, the authors acknowledge **"at present, there is a shortage of cross-comparison research between the two techniques."**

The meta-analysis by [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) comparing conventional open surgery to biportal endoscopy found ODI improvements favoring biportal endoscopy, but this compares open surgery rather than TLIF specifically.

## Complication Rates

**Comprehensive Evidence Unavailable:**

The retrieved literature does not provide direct comparative complication rates between UBE and conventional TLIF. The meta-analysis [pubmed_40797374] (Evidence: 1b) analyzed complication rates between conventional open surgery and biportal endoscopy across 414 open and 383 biportal procedures, but conventional open surgery is not equivalent to TLIF outcomes.

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Direct RCT comparison lacking** between UBE and conventional TLIF for lumbar stenosis
2. **Functional outcome data limited** - most studies compare UBE to MIS-TLIF rather than standard TLIF
3. **Complication rate comparisons** specifically for UBE versus conventional TLIF not available in provided literature

**Recommendation:** High-quality RCTs directly comparing these techniques are needed for definitive conclusions.

# Evidence Comparison: UBE/Biportal Endoscopic TLIF vs Conventional TLIF for Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

## Functional Outcomes (ODI Improvement)

**Superior pain reduction with endoscopic approaches:**
Biportal endoscopic TLIF (BE-TLIF) and full endoscopic TLIF (FE-TLIF) demonstrated **superior clinical efficacy compared to MIS-TLIF**, offering **significantly greater back pain reduction and improved short-term functional outcomes** [pubmed_41420307, Network Meta-analysis, Evidence 1a]. However, the specific ODI improvement values are not quantified in the available literature.

The systematic review comparing biportal endoscopy to tubular approaches for decompression procedures and TLIF notes comparable outcomes overall, but specific ODI comparisons are not detailed [pubmed_40568817, Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis, Evidence 1a].

## Complication Rates

**Comparable safety profile:**
- Studies examining biportal endoscopic discectomy versus conventional microdiscectomy found **comparable complication rates with no statistically significant differences in perioperative complications** [pubmed_38388729, Meta-Analysis, Evidence 1a; pubmed_41452813, Meta-Analysis, Evidence 1a]
- Minimally invasive tubular decompression demonstrated **lower overall complication rates (OR 0.42, 95% CI 0.21–0.86)** and **reduced surgical site infections (OR 0.28, 95% CI 0.11–0.69)** compared to conventional open laminectomy [pubmed_41354742, Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis, Evidence 1a]

## Secondary Benefits

**Perioperative advantages of endoscopic TLIF:**
- **Significantly longer operative times** (a notable limitation) [pubmed_41420307]
- **Shorter hospital stays** [pubmed_41420307, pubmed_41420307]
- **Reduced perioperative blood loss** (BE-TLIF: 185.74±172.51 mL vs MT-TLIF: 395.31±180.36 mL) [pubmed_40195641, Systematic Review, Evidence 1a]

## Evidence Gaps

**Critical limitation:** No studies directly comparing UBE/biportal TLIF versus conventional open TLIF report specific **ODI improvement values or direct head-to-head comparisons** for this outcome measure. A prospective RCT protocol exists (UBE-TLIF vs MT-TLIF) but has not yet published results [pubmed_39322595].
