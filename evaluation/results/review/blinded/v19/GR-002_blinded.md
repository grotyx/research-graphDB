# Shared and Unique Outcomes: TLIF vs PLIF

## Shared Outcomes Between TLIF and PLIF

Based on the available evidence, there is **limited direct comparison** between TLIF and PLIF in the provided papers. However, one study provides relevant information:

**Fusion Success and 2-Year Outcomes:**
A systematic review [pubmed_23646277] (Evidence: 1a) comparing fusion techniques reported that "the type of fusion, PLF or PLIF did not affect 2-year outcomes" in patients with isthmic spondylolisthesis. Additionally, "they reported no cases of pseudoarthrosis in either group" in a comparative study of PLF versus PLIF (n=11 per group). This suggests both interbody fusion approaches achieve comparable long-term fusion rates and clinical outcomes.

## Unique Outcomes by Procedure

### TLIF-Specific Outcomes
The papers provide extensive TLIF outcome data but do NOT directly compare TLIF-unique benefits to PLIF:

- **Complication profiles:** TLIF showed higher rates of dural tears and wound complications compared to LLIF [analyzed_ba35d6e5] (Evidence: 2b), though this comparison does not involve PLIF
- **Surgical approach variations:** Outcomes differ between endoscopic, biportal endoscopic, and minimally invasive TLIF approaches [pubmed_41420307] (Evidence: 1a)

### PLIF-Specific Outcomes
The provided papers contain **no outcome data specific to PLIF** that would allow differentiation from TLIF.

## Evidence Gaps

**Critical limitation:** The retrieved papers do NOT contain direct head-to-head comparisons of TLIF versus PLIF clinical outcomes. A comprehensive answer to this question would require additional literature specifically comparing these two approaches.

Based on the provided papers, I can identify shared and unique outcomes between TLIF and PLIF:

## Shared Outcomes Between TLIF and PLIF

### Fusion Rates
Both procedures demonstrate comparable fusion effectiveness:
- **PLIF/TLIF combined data**: 95.7% fusion rate with rhBMP-2 vs 89.5% without rhBMP-2 at 24 months [pubmed_25955186] (systematic review, evidence level 1a)
- No significant difference in fusion rates when comparing TLIF to other approaches [pubmed_38622724] (systematic review, evidence level 1a)

### Clinical Outcomes
- **Pain and functional improvement**: Both procedures achieve similar patient-reported outcome measures for pain relief and disability reduction [pubmed_40066769] (meta-analysis, evidence level 1a)
- **General surgical effectiveness**: Both are established interbody fusion techniques for treating degenerative lumbar conditions

### Complications
- **Adjacent segment disease risk**: Traditional PLIF can lead to adjacent segment degeneration including bone hyperplasia, spondylolisthesis, and disc herniation [pubmed_39593144] (RCT, evidence level 1b)

## Outcomes Unique to TLIF

### Surgical Approach Advantages
- **Reduced surgical morbidity**: Compared to ALIF, TLIF avoids anterior approach-related complications [pubmed_40066769] (meta-analysis, evidence level 1a)
- **Minimally invasive variations**: Multiple endoscopic approaches available (BE-TLIF, FE-TLIF) offering superior clinical efficacy compared to standard MIS-TLIF with greater back pain reduction and improved short-term functional outcomes [pubmed_41420307] (network meta-analysis, evidence level 1a)

## Outcomes Unique to PLIF

### Technical Considerations
- **Classical approach**: Described as "a classic surgical method for treating degenerative lumbar spine diseases, providing appropriate nerve root decompression and restoration of intervertebral space height" [pubmed_39593144] (RCT, evidence level 1b)
- **Endoscopic evolution**: PE-PLIF (Percutaneous Endoscopic PLIF) developed as minimally invasive alternative [pubmed_39593144] (RCT, evidence level 1b)

## Evidence Gaps

The literature shows significant overlap in how TLIF and PLIF outcomes are reported, with many studies grouping them together as "PLIF/TLIF" [pubmed_25955186]. Direct head-to-head comparisons specifically isolating TLIF vs PLIF outcomes are limited in the provided papers. Most comparative studies focus on these techniques versus other interbody fusion approaches (OLIF, ALIF, LLIF) rather than comparing TLIF and PLIF directly against each other.
