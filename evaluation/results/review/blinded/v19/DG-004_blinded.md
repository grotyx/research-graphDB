### Answer

Based on the provided systematic reviews and meta-analyses, here is the evidence comparing microdiscectomy versus endoscopic discectomy for lumbar disc herniation:

## Clinical Efficacy

**Pain Relief and Functional Outcomes:**
- Multiple network meta-analyses demonstrate **clinical equivalence** between endoscopic and microscopic approaches [pubmed_41666862, pubmed_38190892]
- Both techniques show significant improvements in Visual Analog Scale scores and Oswestry Disability Index compared to conservative treatment [pubmed_40195642]
- Back pain, leg pain, and functional measures showed nonsignificant trends favoring endoscopic decompression [pubmed_38190892]

## Perioperative Outcomes

**Blood Loss:**
- Endoscopic approaches demonstrate **33.29 mL reduction** in blood loss compared to microscopic techniques (p=0.0032) [pubmed_38190892]

**Hospital Stay:**
- Endoscopic techniques show **1.79 days reduction** in hospital stay (p=0.001) [pubmed_38190892]
- Multiple studies confirm shorter hospital stays with endoscopic approaches [pubmed_41666862]

## Complication Profiles

**Infection Rates:**
- Endoscopic discectomy shows **77% reduction in surgical site infection risk** (RR 0.23, p=0.001) [pubmed_38190892]
- Lower infection rates consistently reported across endoscopic techniques [pubmed_41666862]

**Durotomy Risk:**
- Endoscopic approach demonstrates **37% reduction in durotomy rates** (RR 0.63, p=0.0184) [pubmed_38190892]

**Overall Complications:**
- Network meta-analysis of 18 RCTs comparing five surgical techniques found varying complication rates across different endoscopic approaches [pubmed_31529215]

## Tissue Preservation

**Surgical Invasiveness:**
- Endoscopic techniques offer **reduced tissue trauma** compared to traditional microdiscectomy [pubmed_41666862]
- Minimally invasive approaches minimize paraspinal muscle stripping and reduce postoperative biomechanical instability risks [pubmed_40155927]

## Comparative Rankings

**Network Meta-Analysis Findings:**
- Among surgical options, endoscopic discectomy with internal fixation (EDF) showed **best performance** compared to microdiscectomy (MD) and open discectomy [pubmed_40611244]
- All surgical approaches (including both endoscopic and microscopic) were significantly superior to conservative treatment [pubmed_40611244]

## Evidence Quality and Limitations

**Study Design:**
- Evidence primarily from Level 1a systematic reviews and meta-analyses [pubmed_41666862, pubmed_31529215, pubmed_40611244, pubmed_38190892]
- Most included studies were retrospective rather than RCTs, potentially affecting conclusion strength [pubmed_40797374]
- Inconsistent definitions and techniques across studies may affect comparability [pubmed_40797374]

## Evidence Gaps

- Limited long-term follow-up data comparing recurrence rates
- Variations in surgeon experience and surgical approach (transforaminal vs. interlaminar) not adequately addressed
- Standardization of endoscopic techniques remains inconsistent across studies

**Conclusion:** The evidence suggests that while microdiscectomy remains the gold standard, endoscopic discectomy demonstrates clinical equivalence in pain relief and functional outcomes while offering superior perioperative advantages including reduced blood loss, shorter hospital stays, and lower complication rates.

# Evidence Summary: Microdiscectomy versus Endoscopic Discectomy for Lumbar Disc Herniation

## Clinical Efficacy Outcomes

**Pain and Functional Outcomes - No Significant Differences:**
Multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses found no clinically meaningful differences between microdiscectomy and endoscopic discectomy in primary outcome measures [pubmed_41666862, pubmed_41452813, pubmed_34213864 - all Evidence 1a]. Specifically:

- Back pain, leg pain, and functional disability showed "nonsignificant trends" favoring endoscopic approaches, but these did not reach statistical significance [pubmed_38190892 - Evidence 1a]
- A Cochrane review analysis found no significant differences in disability outcomes at long-term follow-up [pubmed_39434232 - Evidence 4]

**Notable Exception - Recurrent Herniation:**
In recurrent lumbar disc herniation, open microdiscectomy was associated with significantly **lower postoperative leg pain** compared to biportal endoscopic discectomy (p=0.003), though the clinical significance remains unclear [pubmed_41482265 - Evidence 2b, retrospective comparative analysis]

---

## Perioperative Advantages of Endoscopic Approaches

**Blood Loss and Hospital Stay:**
- Blood loss reduction: 33.29 mL (p=0.0032) [pubmed_38190892 - Evidence 1a]
- Hospital stay reduction: 1.79 days (p=0.001) [pubmed_38190892 - Evidence 1a]

**Surgical Complications:**
- Surgical site infection reduction: RR 0.23 (p=0.001) [pubmed_38190892 - Evidence 1a]
- Dural puncture/durotomy rate reduction: RR 0.63 (p=0.0184) [pubmed_38190892 - Evidence 1a]

---

## Study Quality and Evidence Limitations

**Strength of Evidence:**
- Highest quality evidence comes from systematic reviews and meta-analyses of RCTs [pubmed_41666862, pubmed_41452813, pubmed_34213864 - all Level 1a evidence]
- However, a network meta-analysis of discectomy complications [pubmed_31529215 - Evidence 1a] included only 18 RCTs comparing five different techniques

**Critical Evidence Gap:**
The meta-analysis by Overdevest et al. and findings from [pubmed_41002072 - Evidence 1b RCT] demonstrate that "anatomical benefits do not translate into superior functional outcomes or pain relief from the patient's perspective," suggesting potential publication bias favoring minimally invasive techniques in earlier literature.

---

## Clinical Conclusion

Based on available evidence, **microdiscectomy and endoscopic discectomy demonstrate equivalent clinical efficacy** for symptomatic lumbar disc herniation (Evidence 1a). The choice between techniques should be based on:

1. **Patient-centered factors:** Perioperative benefits (shorter hospital stay, reduced infection, less blood loss) may favor endoscopic approaches for appropriate candidates
2. **Surgeon expertise:** Technical proficiency likely influences outcomes more than technique selection
3. **Clinical context:** Open microdiscectomy may warrant consideration for recurrent herniation cases, pending further investigation [pubmed_41482265]

**Notable gap:** Long-term functional equivalence has been established, but evidence comparing cost-effectiveness and patient satisfaction between approaches remains limited in the provided literature.
