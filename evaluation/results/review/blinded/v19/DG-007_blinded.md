### Answer

Based on the provided papers, I must note that **no randomized controlled trials (RCTs) directly comparing LLIF/XLIF versus TLIF for degenerative spondylolisthesis were included in these studies**.

However, the available systematic reviews and meta-analyses provide the following evidence from observational studies:

## Pain and Functional Outcomes
**No significant differences** were found between LLIF and TLIF in patient-reported outcomes:
- A network meta-analysis [pubmed_39895414] (Evidence level 1a) found that minimally invasive techniques including LLIF and TLIF "demonstrated equivalent clinical outcomes in pain and disability relief for lumbar degenerative disease patients"
- An observational comparative study [analyzed_ba35d6e5] (Evidence level 2b) showed "no significant between-group effects for Oswestry Disability Index (p = 0.335) or low back pain (p = 0.069)"

## Surgical Efficiency
**LLIF showed advantages** in perioperative metrics:
- Stand-alone LLIF demonstrated "superior early efficacy metrics including shorter operative time and faster hospitalization recovery" [pubmed_39895414]

## Radiographic Outcomes
**LLIF appeared superior** for spinal alignment restoration:
- "LLIF demonstrated improved SL [segmental lordosis] restoration compared with TLIF in 2 of the 3 studies directly comparing these two procedures" [pubmed_39575949] (Evidence level 1a)

## Complications
**Different complication profiles** were observed:
- "TLIF showed higher rates of dural tears and wound complications, while LLIF had more sympathetic chain injuries and transient psoas weakness. Overall complication rates were comparable (p > 0.05)" [analyzed_ba35d6e5]

## Evidence Gaps
The major limitation is the **absence of RCT data**. The systematic reviews included only retrospective comparative studies and case series. High-quality RCTs specifically comparing LLIF/XLIF versus TLIF for degenerative spondylolisthesis are needed to provide definitive evidence on their relative efficacy and safety.

# Evidence Summary: LLIF/XLIF vs TLIF for Degenerative Spondylolisthesis

## Key Finding
**No RCTs are available in the provided papers.** The highest-quality evidence comes from systematic reviews and meta-analyses of observational studies.

---

## Available Evidence by Study Design

### Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analyses (Evidence Level 1a)

**OLIF vs TLIF for Spondylolisthesis** [pubmed_38622724]
- Systematic review and meta-analysis comparing oblique lateral approach (OLIF) with TLIF for **degenerative lumbar spondylolisthesis**
- Study design: Systematic review of comparative studies (January 2014-February 2024)
- *Specific outcome data not provided in abstract*

**Endoscopic TLIF vs OLIF-360 for Spondylolisthesis** [pubmed_41525955]
- Retrospective cohort comparison (Evidence: 2b, not RCT)
- **Complementary advantages noted:**
  - Endo-TLIF: Superior minimization of perioperative morbidity through direct visualization
  - OLIF-360: Superior structural reconstruction

### General Lateral Fusion Comparisons (Evidence Level 1a)

**Network Meta-Analysis of Multiple MIS Techniques** [pubmed_39895414]
- Included X/DLIF, OLIF, SA-LLIF, MIS-TLIF, UELIF, and BELIF
- Finding: **Equivalent clinical outcomes** in pain and disability relief regardless of fusion approach
- Note: Stand-alone LLIF (SA-LLIF) showed superior early efficacy (shorter operative time, faster recovery)

**Segmental Lordosis Restoration** [pubmed_39575949]
- OLIF demonstrated **superior restoration of segmental lordosis** compared with TLIF
- LLIF demonstrated **improved segmental lordosis restoration** compared with TLIF in 2 of 3 directly comparative studies

---

## Evidence Gaps

- **No randomized controlled trials** identified comparing LLIF/XLIF with TLIF specifically for degenerative spondylolisthesis
- Highest-quality evidence for spondylolisthesis comes from observational comparative studies
- Long-term outcome data limited in retrieved papers
