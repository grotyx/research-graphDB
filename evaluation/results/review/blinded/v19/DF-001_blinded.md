Based on the provided papers, I cannot find direct evidence comparing T10 versus L1 as the optimal upper instrumented vertebra (UIV) specifically for Lenke 5C adult degenerative scoliosis. However, the available evidence provides some relevant insights about UIV selection in adult spinal deformity surgery:

## Upper Instrumented Vertebra Distribution in Adult Spinal Deformity

From a retrospective cohort study [pubmed_35586905], the distribution of UIV levels in adult spinal deformity surgery was:
- Upper thoracic (T1-T9): 14%
- Lower thoracic (T10-T12): 66%
- Upper lumbar (L1-L3): 20%

This suggests that lower thoracic levels (including T10) are most commonly selected as UIV in practice.

## Proximal Junctional Complications by UIV Level

A comparative study examining transverse process hooks versus pedicle screws at the UIV [pubmed_41297090] found:
- Proximal junctional kyphosis (PJK) rates: 21.4% with transverse process hooks vs 37.0% with pedicle screws
- Proximal junctional failure (PJF) rates: 17.1% with transverse process hooks vs 32.2% with pedicle screws

However, this study did not specifically compare T10 versus L1 UIV levels.

## Segmental Lordosis and Overcorrection Risks

Evidence suggests that overcorrection at proximal spinal levels (T10-L2) results in dramatically increased proximal junctional failure rates [pubmed_38270393]. This finding is relevant to both T10 and L1 UIV selection but doesn't differentiate between these specific levels.

## Evidence Gaps

The provided literature does not contain:
1. Direct comparisons of T10 versus L1 UIV selection
2. Specific data for Lenke 5C curve patterns
3. Comparative outcomes data between these two UIV options
4. Curve-specific recommendations for degenerative scoliosis

## Conclusion

Based on the available evidence, I cannot provide a definitive recommendation for optimal UIV selection between T10 and L1 for Lenke 5C adult degenerative scoliosis. The literature shows that lower thoracic UIV levels (T10-T12) are most commonly used in practice, but specific comparative data between T10 and L1 for this curve pattern is not available in the provided papers. Clinical decision-making would need to rely on additional evidence not included in this review.

# Evidence Synthesis: Optimal UIV Selection for Lenke 5C Adult Degenerative Scoliosis

## Summary Statement

The provided papers do not contain specific evidence comparing T10 versus L1 as the upper instrumented vertebra for Lenke 5C adult degenerative scoliosis. However, general principles for UIV selection from the literature are relevant.

## General UIV Selection Principles

**Anatomic Considerations:**
- UIV selection should avoid spanning across major coronal deformities and consider the apex of thoracic kyphosis [pubmed_37718099, Evidence Level 4]
- The thoracolumbar junction (T10-L2 region) is a transitional area with higher risk of mechanical complications due to mobility differences between the mobile lumbar spine and less mobile thoracic spine [pubmed_40033732, Evidence Level 4]

**Risk Factor Data:**
- More proximal UIV placement (T2 or more proximal) was associated with increased distal junctional kyphosis (DJK) development in adolescent idiopathic scoliosis [pubmed_40103372, Evidence Level 3]. This suggests that proximal UIV placement alters thoracic sagittal alignment and may cause compensatory distal junctional changes.

**General Recommendation:**
- A "neutral and stable vertebra" is recommended as the UIV to minimize proximal junctional kyphosis/failure (PJK/PJF) risk [pubmed_40033732, Evidence Level 4]

## Evidence Gaps

**Critical limitation:** No studies directly comparing T10 versus L1 selection for Lenke 5C curves were identified. The optimal choice requires consideration of:
- Curve apex location
- Preoperative thoracic kyphosis
- Sagittal alignment targets
- Patient-specific factors

**Recommendation:** Evidence-based guidance for this specific clinical scenario requires studies comparing these instrumentation levels in Lenke 5C populations.
